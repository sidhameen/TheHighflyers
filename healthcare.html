Company Profile

Healthcare is the very foundation for the flourishing of a nation and its exports enable inclusive growth globally. In turn, a quality-driven, innovative pharmaceutical sector makes the world safer for its citizens and safeguards the future for everyone. Flying the UAE’s flag high in this field is Julphar (Gulf Pharmaceutical Industries), a Ras Al Khaimahheadquartered leader in the production and distribution of pharmaceutical products across five continents.

Since its inception in 1980, Julphar has been committed to meeting the needs of an evolving society and playing an instrumental role in global healthcare development. Its approach to a fast-paced market is to focus on strategic initiatives, quality product development and responsible practices.

Taking inspiration from the UAE’s policy of caring for the people beyond its borders, Julphar’s goal is to create innovative and high-quality products, whilst maintaining a competitive cost. It is a true reflection of Ras al Khaimah’s significant development and continues to lead as one of the UAE’s most influential local businesses making an impact on the global stage.

The two-pronged approach that the company has been built on comprises world-class manufacturing facilities based in the UAE and beyond, and a reliable distribution network. Julphar contains a diverse portfolio of products which targets a wide range of major therapeutic segments.

The company’s mission is to deliver excellence in healthcare which will enable its customers to live a long and healthy life. It is through this aim that it has emerged as one of the largest distributors of pharmaceutical products in the MENA region within just over three decades.

Julphar currently has over 3,500 registered products across the world that is distributes through its reliable logistics network to clients in over 40 countries globally. The segments in this diverse product portfolio include endocrinology, antiinfective, cardiovascular and gastroenterology, over-thecounter, nephrology, dermatology, respiratory, metabolic, and burn and wound management. Further on, highlighting its commitment to diabetes management in the region, it also became the only company in the MRNA region to produce the raw material needed to make insulin through its dedicated division.

Julphar’s eleven local world-class manufacturing facilities are located in Ras Al Khaimah and it expanded this regionally with the launch of its first overseas manufacturing facility in Ethiopia during 2013. It also endeavours to take this ambition further with additional facilities in strategic countries such as Saudi Arabia and Algeria. Some of the key trusted products in the Julphar Consumer Health line-up are Adol®, Profinal®, Calvitalis®, Ginsavit® and Amydramine®.

The launch of both local and global facilities, teamed with an emphasis on difficult markets has taken the forefront of its overall strategy. This has evidently paid-off, with its biotechnology sector showing excellent performance.

The company’s other subsidiaries include Menacool; a transportation division which maintains a fleet of vehicles for efficient distribution of products, thus enabling it to react quickly to market demand and Planet Pharmacies; a joint venture with Global Investment House which was established in 2007 and grew from the acquisition of independent pharmacy businesses located across the United Arab Emirates, Oman and Saudi Arabia. Planet Pharmacy has region-wide exclusive agency agreements with major international brands and currently has more than 100 retail pharmacies as well as a wholesale distribution division.

Julphar’s promise is to continually develop products, people and services by achieving and maintaining the highest international standards in treatment, education, and community outreach. In terms of commendations, Julphar has twice won the prestigious Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum Business Award, as well as the 2011 Arab Achievement Award.

The Julphar Quality Management System (QMS) enables it to integrate quality into all stages of manufacturing. Its objective is to incorporate continuous Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP). Its facilities and QMS are accredited by ISO: 9001:2000 and ISO 14001. Julphar is also approved by international authorities such as UNICEF, JFDA, SFDA, German and Romanian authority.

A major factor in its success to date has been the ability to both cultivate talent from inside the organisation and recruit new talent to ensure that the right people do the right jobs. For this purpose, it established a standardised corporate process to identify and recruit personnel most suited to the company’s current and future needs in a fair, objective and non-discriminative manner. Currently, over 2,800 professionals worldwide are working together to bring Julphar’s ambitions and goals to fruition.

The organisation’s solid balance sheet, low-gearing and continued focus on operating cash flow generation gives it the financial flexibility to pursue growth opportunities, including strategic acquisitions that supplement strong organic growth. The end of 2011 marked a major milestone for the company, when sales figures exceeded AED 1 billion for the first time – a testament to the high-quality processes and growing demand of its products.

Taking it further, Julphar closed the year 2012 at AED1.18 billion, producing overall steady results, with a year-onyear growth of 15.3% over sales from 2011. By adhering to a prudent expansion approach and constantly striving for excellence, Julphar is today not just well positioned to meet tomorrow’s healthcare challenges, but is also shaping the future of this industry.

Success Stories
Enabling accessible cure in Ethiopia

From the outset, Africa has been an important market for Julphar. In recognition of the economic importance of this continent and the need for increasingly sophisticated pharmaceutical and healthcare facilities, Julphar, in conjunction with its local Ethiopian partner, Medtech, officially inaugurated its first manufacturing facility in Africa in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia in early-2013.

Julphar had first begun providing medicines in Ethiopia in 2005, significantly growing to cover major hospitals, clinics and retail pharmacies. The Julphar Ethiopia plant took this success further by providing access to high-quality medicine closer to home.

This 40,000 sq ft facility, designed and built by Julphar’s engineering and construction division, is cGMP compliant, and once fully operational, the Julphar plant will produce 25 million bottles of suspension and syrup, 500 million tablets and 200 million capsules per annum. Equipped with state-of-the-art technology, it enables production of Julphar products not only for the local market but also across for the African continent.

Julphar has invested over USD 9 million in the construction of the plant and this strategically important launch took the company closer to bringing more accessible, reliable medicines in the region. It is the first step for Julphar in opening many more similar plants across the region to create a pharmaceutical manufacturing industry that is on par with the best in the world.

Fighting diabetes with ambitious manufacturing

In 2008, Julphar made an important strategic decision to become one of the Middle East’s leaders in offering a world-class treatment for both Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes. With an eye on this mission, it launched Julphar Diabetes, which today is the only regional company to offer a comprehensive portfolio of high-quality, affordable drugs that doctors and patients continue to endorse.

Progressing at a fast pace, in 2011, Julphar signed a partnership with International Diabetes Federation (IDF), ensuring IDF has a key partner in the Middle East region in the fight against diabetes. This also ensured that it could play a key role in the efforts to promote better diabetes care, management and education, to cut down diabetes rates in the MENA region, which are amongst the highest in the world.

In 2012, it became the only company in the MENA region to locally produce recombinant human insulin products, through the completion of its USD 136.1 million dedicated insulin production facility. Julphar Diabetes, which manages the manufacturing and commercialisation of the company’s diabetes products, has the facility capabilities to produce approximately 40 million vials of insulin per year. This makes Julphar the fifth largest producer of insulin in the world, and the only one in the MENA region.

Julphar also has plans to expand the insulin production plant to widen its product offerings and strengthen its market presence in diabetic care. Since diabetes afflicts sizeable sections of the Middle Eastern population, Julphar’s venture into bulk insulin production proved to be quite timely. Julphar’s first insulin production began eight years ago when insulin crystals were imported from European sources for the local production of Jusline®. It turned this around by producing the crystals in-house, thereby all set to make insulin more available and more affordable.

Today, Jusline® the Julphar brand for recombinant human Insulin, is primed be a major step towards diabetes care in the region. As of now, it has been approved by many MOH regulatory authorities and is registered in more than 14 countries worldwide.

Company Profile

Localised pharmaceutical research and manufacturing can play a crucial role in providing accessible and affordable medical care for every nation and region. The United Arab Emirates, as part of its economic diversification has not just focused on creating world-class healthcare facilities, but also ensuring support for the pharmaceutical sector.

Encouraged by the nation’s policy of creating optical opportunities in every field, Neopharma was set up in the year 2003 with the first manufacturing facilities planned on an investment outlay of over US$ 25 million. Today, this company is the pride of Abu Dhabi, hosting a world- class pharmaceutical manufacturing facility and a wide ranging product line.

Within just a few years of successful accomplishment of registration of the products in the UAE Ministry of Health, Neopharma set about on an unprecedented growth path and accomplished several milestones. Its manufacturing facility in Abu Dhabi is built on the concept of modular manufacturing, follows global benchmarks in manufacturing technology and has efficient control mechanisms in place, ensuring every product is manufactured with the highest quality standards.

Following the successful presence in the UAE market, Neopharma expanded its coverage to the GCC countries, Middle East, Asia, CIS markets, South America and African Countries. The company’s consistent efforts of maintaining and building the brand equity and quality over the years has resulted in appreciation of its standards across various international markets.

The organisation believes in positing itself as a globally admired and technologically-driven enterprise that delivers the best healthcare possible in the region and beyond. Its approach can be exemplified by the fact Neopharma was one of the first companies to capitalise the business opportunities and take the challenge of re-establishing operations in Libya in the recent past. It set up a dedicated Scientific Office with a team consisting of managerial and medical professionals who rebuilt Neopharma’s operations by promoting more than 35 pharmaceutical products, a move that was highly appreciated by both the medical fraternity and the local population.

In harmony with the motto of the Company “Local Inspiration and Global Aspiration”, Neopharma further explored the West African markets and initiated operations in the Federal Republic of Nigeria. Neopharma’s recent expansion projects include the establishment of a dedicated Cephalosporin manufacturing facility and an exclusive research & development centre which will be a pivotal hub for large research projects and interdisciplinary collaborations with leading international nanotechnology, biological and pharmaceutical manufacturers.

Following a well-planned growth path, the company continues to blend innovation and quality to provide worldclass pharmaceuticals. Forming alliances consistently to benefit the region, Neopharma also partnered with Merck Serono, a division of Merck KGaA - Germany, in the year 2012, to locally-produce Merck branded medication in the UAE for distribution across the Middle East.

As a part of community responsibility Neopharma has also paid great importance to protect the environment. It has received various accolades for being an environmentallyfriendly manufacturer and is the recipient of the GCC Environment Award for being the best institution committed to Industrial and Environmental Standards and the Environmental Performance certificate from Ministry of Environment and Water, UAE for outstanding efforts in environmental conservation and application of cleaner production techniques.

Recognising its achievements, Neopharma was awarded the prestigious Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Award in the manufacturing category and is also the recipient of the current European Good Manufacturing Practice Guidelines Certification from Belgium.

At present, as Abu Dhabi’s premier generic pharmaceutical manufacturing facility, Neopharma is in its 10th year of successful and fruitful accomplishment. It has a presence across 20 international markets and is working towards initiating operations in many more key markets.

Success Story
Creating greater local access in healthcare

Neopharma has been consistently taking steps towards making the region self-reliant for supply of essential pharmaceutical products. The epitome of its success has been the signing of a manufacturing agreement with the leading German manufacturer Merck Serono in the year 2012. This partnership involved manufacturing Merck’s branded medications at Neopharma’s facility for distribution across UAE and the rest of the Middle East markets.

Under the agreement, which was the first partnership of its kind, Merck Serono became the first multinational pharmaceutical company to work with a local manufacturer to cater to regional demand for key pharmaceutical products through export out of the UAE manufacturing base. Initially, two Merck Serono products are set to be manufactured by Neopharma: Euthyrox, a hormone therapy requiring a very stringent sterile environment for production and packaging; and Glucophage, a standard oral treatment for type 2 diabetics.

The company aims to start the production of both branded medications in 2013. With this partnership, Neopharma has ensured that the lead time for product-to-market reduces significantly along with increased availability of major treatments for the UAE and the wider region.

Company Profile

GlobalPharma is a key subsidiary of Dubai Investments PJSC, a company with investments in extensive fields across the UAE and the Middle East. Having over 20,700 shareholders and paid-up capital of USD 654 million, it is the largest investment company listed on the Dubai Financial Market (DFM). The group has rapidly expanded in diverse sectors and presently has 47 subsidiaries under its umbrella.

As one of its leading industrial investments, GlobalPharma is dedicated to offering international quality pharmaceutical products to the Middle East and global markets.Starting out as Dubai’s first pharmaceutical manufacturing company gave it a unique advantage and positioned it strongly to benefit from the vast opportunities offered by the booming UAE economy. This has enabled it to pioneer a manufacturing excellence model for the pharmaceutical business in the region. Driven by the aim to be socially-conscious and a commitment to improving the quality of human life, the company has innovatively spread its operations beyond the immediate markets of the GCC and Middle East to the vast continents of Asia and Africa. Some of its key export markets, which offer wide opportunities based on the large populace, are Saudi Arabia, Lebanon, Libya, Iraq, Kenya and South Africa, among others.

GlobalPharma started out with a clear business model, having three key areas of operation, including manufacturing and marketing its own brands, manufacturing and marketing the products of multinational pharmaceutical companies through ‘in-licensing’, and contract manufacturing products for other companies. Its core business of manufacturing its own brands constitutes more than 90 per cent of total sales turnover, thereby making it a self-reliant entity. Whereas, its in-licensing portfolio comprises products from leading international names such as Polichem, Switzerland, Bouchara Recordati, France; and Marco Antonetto, Italy.

Its flagship products include Gloclav (Amoxycillin and Clavulanate / Antibiotic), Emifenac 50 DT (Diclifenac / NSAID), Glovit (Multivitamin), Emicipro (Ciprofloxacin / Antibiotic), Emimycin (Clarithromycin / Antibiotic), Emilok (Omerprazole / Anti-ulcerant) and Glodip (Amlodipine / Antihyperstensive). As part of its contract manufacturing business, it also extends its manufacturing facilities primarily for Penicillin products to various companies in the region. Currently, GlobalPharma is in the final stages of a deal with one of the top-ranking multinational Pharmaceutical companies to manufacture their products range for GCC and other Middle Eastern countries.

GlobalPharma’s modern pharmaceutical manufacturing plant occupies 27,000 square metres in Dubai Investment Park. It manufactures a diverse rangeof over 60 products that include Anti-infectives, Analgesic / Anti-inflammatory, Gastrointestinal, Nutraceuticals, Gynaecological, Antidiabetics, Cardio-vascular and Respiratory.

The factory has two separate manufacturing units, for Penicillin and Non-Penicillin products, with the utilities in a separate block. Its capabilities extend to a wide range of dosage forms including tablets, capsules, dry powder suspensions and oral liquids. The facility is designed to meet international standards of cGMP, including GCC Ministries of Health, US Food and Drugs Administration (FDA) and UK’s Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA). The company ensures zero deficiencies in the final product through strict adherence to the highest quality standards across every process.

The annual capacity of the plant is 300 million tablets, 150 million capsules, and over 7 million litres of dry syrup and liquid, based on one shift of eight hours. Upgraded consistently, it is well-equipped to take the company through to the next generation of products.

Success Story
Making inroads into the Asian market, and beyond

When GlobalPharma planned to expand beyond its immediate regional markets, Asia presented a promising gateway to large markets. However, the presence of major international as well as home-grown players poised a unique challenge for a relatively new entrant in the sector. But GlobalPharma, through its focus on innovation and advancement in every aspect, was already well placed to compete with the best. In order to make inroads into this continent, the company laid out the strategy to enter nations where the pharmaceutical sector was in the initial stages of expansion and emerging fast.

By applying its pioneering manufacturing business model formed in the UAE and other Middle Eastern markets, GlobalPharma forayed into countries like the Philippines and Cambodia, which was soon extended to Sri Lanka and Afghanistan, with plans to extend further in the Caspian region. What’s more, GlobalPharma has future ambitious plans of exploring viable opportunities in the highly lucrative European and North American markets.

GlobalPharma is also eager to further expand its products portfolio in the fast growing disease areas like Obesity, Cardio-vascular and Diabetes and will soon enter new therapeutic areas like Oncology, Hormone Therapies and Parenteral products.
