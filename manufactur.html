Company Profile

As one of the most prolific metal on earth, aluminium has seen a formidable rise in application since the mid-19th century. Its high conductivity and resistance to rust made it an extensively used metal and more recently, the largest contract traded on the London Metal Exchange (LME). No wonder then that metals in general, and aluminium specifically, take centre-stage in the diversification of Abu Dhabi’s industrial base in line with the emirate’s Economic Vision 2030. Playing a major role in the realisation of these goals is Emirates Aluminium (EMAL), a Joint Venture (JV) between Mubadala Development Company (Mubadala) and Dubai Aluminium Company Limited (DUBAL).

EMAL is a state-of-the-art aluminium smelter complex, located at KIZAD (Khalifa Industrial Zone – Abu Dhabi) in Al Taweelah, strategically located halfway between Abu Dhabi and Dubai. It showcases the close cooperation between the two emirates in the UAE, aimed at diversifying the nation’s economy and ensuring its continued vibrancy in the future.

EMAL’s advanced smelter currently uses DUBAL’s DX Reduction Cell Technology to produce 750,000 tonnes of aluminium annually. This is slated to increase to 1.3 metric million tonnes by the end of 2014 upon completion of Phase II. Further on, the installation of the new generation DX+ Reduction Cell Technology and this projected increase in capacity will make EMAL one of the largest single-site producers of primary aluminium in the world.

Its aluminium complex is to be built in two phases on a 6 sq km site in KIZAD, an ambitious development that aims to be the regional hub for industrial manufacturing. Phase I of the EMAL smelter commenced operations on the 2nd of December 2009 and hit full production capacity on first workday of 2011. Its product mix comprises sow, standard ingots, sheet ingots and extrusion billets, two of which are traded on the London Metals Exchange (LME).

USD 5.7 billion was invested to build EMAL Phase I with an on-going USD 4.5 billion investment in Phase II of the smelter’s development, which started in 2011 and is on path to be completed in 2014. Today, these remarkable achievements have made EMAL one of the largest industrial projects in the UAE outside the oil & gas sector.

It achieved these with a purpose-built wharf at Khalifa Port to receive shipments of raw materials directly to the smelter and put in place an on-site gas-fired 2,000 MW power plant for reliable long-term energy supply. The results manifested rapidly through remarkable growth figures with EMAL currently supplying over 150 customer worldwide.

Its high quality aluminium, available in different formats, has high standards and certifications maintained throughout the manufacturing line. Furthermore, the power plant at the site will see a planned increase to 3,000MW by 2014. The economic challenge for any smelter is in designing and implementing technology to get the highest quality product using minimal electricity consumption. EMAL does this by utilising the most sustainable technologies available worldwide to reduce emissions in line with Environment Agency - Abu Dhabi (EAD) requirements to minimise its carbon footprint.

Its operational workforce currently provides direct employment for approximately 2,000 people, which is set to rise to 3,000 by 2014. The company has been consistently recognised for its achievements with several commendations including the prestigious DuPont Safety Award, GCC Industrial Project of the Year 2012 by MEED, and Dubai’s KAIZEN Gold Award on the occasion of World Quality Day.

From day one, EMAL has placed priority on its commitment to environment, community, safety and best practices. EMAL’s stance on the environment is underlined by a USD 200 million contract for the supply of its gas treatment centres (GTCs). The deal secures its best-practice technology to reduce both fluoride and sulphur dioxide emissions.

Further to this, EMAL’s power plant uses the cooling tower technology to reduce the temperature of the water used during the energy production process, which in turn helps safeguard the delicate marine eco-system of Al Taweelah. In addition, it also conducted a major wildlife translocation exercise in conjunction with the Environment Agency – Abu Dhabi (EAD) prior to starting Phase I construction. In terms of safety, EMAL achieved 5 million work hours without a single Lost Time Incident (LTI).

When it comes to developmental initiatives, EMAL has Emiratisation of the workforce at the core of its business development strategy. It enables opportunities through graduate trainee programs, technical trainings, and scholarships for local graduates with guaranteed jobs. Its engagement also involves open days for the local community and support for social, sports and cultural organisations as well as donations to charitable health organisations.

Success Stories
EMAL listed on London metal exchange

Established for over 130 years and located in the heart of London, the London Metals Exchange (LME) is the world centre for trading of industrial metals with more than 80% of all non-ferrous metal futures business transacted on its platforms. Investors value the LME not just as a vibrant futures exchange, but also for its close links to industry. EMAL placed emphasis on making a mark on the global stage from its early days and LME was the platform that it has set its sights on. The LME standards for product listing are among the most robust that including ISO 9001 certification and completion a full year of production.

In light of these quality checks, EMAL stood apart through its significant achievement of listing sow, a solid block of molten aluminium, with LME exactly one year after its smelter began producing the same. This accomplishment enabled it to ensure both consistent pricing and an international standard of quality. Further to this strategic move, EMAL also listed its standard ingot product on the exchange, thus sealing its reputation as a global business attracting customers around the world.

Foresighted upsurge of market-share in Europe

Global demand for aluminium for 2011 was estimated at 44 million metric tonnes and is projected to rise to 70 million metric tonnes by 2020. Amongst the different regions, the European Union (EU) as a whole imports approximately 60% of its primary aluminium requirements, thus offering excellent prospects for future market share.

EMAL’s robust global export plan placed emphasis on local, Middle Eastern, European, Asian and North American markets. Having achieved a significant customer base in Asia, it directed its focus on the European market to capitalise on the growing demand. Putting its strategy to work in the year 2011, EMAL exported 174,000 metric tonnes of aluminium to Europe and is expected to increase by 35% in 2013. The year 2011 was a landmark year for EMAL in many more ways as it hit the symbolic mark of one million metric tonnes of hot metal produced and also announced of the start of phase II. Currently, approximately 88 to 92 percent of aluminium produced in the UAE is exported to global markets and EMAL continues to be one of the two major producers of this metal in the nation.

Company Profile

Abu Dhabi National Oil Company (ADNOC), an UAEbased oil and gas company, and Borealis, an Austriabased provider of chemical and plastics solutions, established Borouge as a joint venture in 1998. Within a few years of operation, Borouge became a leading provider of innovative, value creating plastics solutions.

Borouge consists of two separate companies: the Abu Dhabi Polymers Company (Borouge’s Production Company) headquartered in Abu Dhabi, UAE, and Borouge Pte Ltd (Borouge’s Marketing & Sales Company) with the head office in Singapore and three logistics hubs in Shanghai, Guangzhou and Singapore.

Borouge is an excellent example of a petrochemicals company that is experiencing formidable growth within an incredible short period of time. Its journey of success was evident through the increasing number of the manufacturing plants. At the beginning, the company distributed Borealis plastics products through its sales and marketing units in different countries, a strategic move to best research the market and test the water of the industry. Once the potential was realized, building its own manufacturing plants was a logical next step.

The company started production at its first plant in Ruwais, Abu Dhabi in 2001 with a capacity of 450,000 tonnes per year (t/y) of polyethylene. In 2002, the first shipments of polyethylene left the Abu Dhabi plant to customers in the Middle East and Asia. By 2005, the company’s state-of-theart petrochemicals complex was expanded to manufacture a total of 600,000 tonnes of polyethylene per year. The complex consisted of an ethylene cracker and two Borstar® bimodal polyethylene units, each capable of producing 300,000 t/y of bimodal polyethylene.

In 2010, and as per its growth plans to strengthen its presence in the petrochemicals industry to meet the ever-increasing demand, the company tripled its annual production capacity in Abu Dhabi to 2 million tonnes per year, including polypropylene for the first time. The expansion in capacity was achieved under the multi-billion dollar ‘Borouge 2 Project’. The expansion project included an additional ethane cracker, a third polyethylene unit, an olefins conversion unit for the conversion of ethylene to propylene and two new polypropylene units, spearheading the company’s entrance into polypropylene production.

Stepping into the Chinese giant market to support the challenges and demands of China and Asia’s rapidly growing industries in April 2010 through inaugurating a compounding manufacturing unit in Shanghai was a milestone that signals the strong commitment of the company to be among the global industry players.

With the scope of its customers expanding in Asia, Borouge aims to be a leading plastics supplier to China in its strategic market segments of infrastructure, automotive and advanced packaging. The company has boosted its presence in China with launching {two logistic hubs and an academy Wire & Cable.

Unique proprietary processes and catalyst technologies like Borstar and Borealis Nucleation Technology (BNT) that were adapted enabled Borouge to provide a differentiated range of polyethylene and polypropylene innovative, value creating plastics solutions for infrastructure (including pipe systems, and power and communication cables), automotives and advanced packaging.

In addition, being present in key strategic locations throughout the Middle East, Asia-Pacific and Africa facilitates speed to market, on-time delivery and customer support. Borouge is also the exclusive regional provider of the entire Borealis product line.

With its base in the UAE and marketing & sales head office in Singapore, Borouge employs approximately 1,700 people representing more than 40 nationalities and serving customers in more than 50 countries across the Middle East, Asia-Pacific, Indian sub-continent and Africa.

Driven by its growth strategy and committing to meet the needs of its increasing customers for high quality products of polyethylene (PE) and polypropylene (PP), Borouge is currently expanding its polyolefin operations under the ‘Borouge 3 Expansion Project’ which will be completed at the end of 2013 resulting in an additional capacity of 2.5 million tonnes per year (t/y) being fully operational by mid-2014. The project includes an ethane cracker, two polyethylene units and two polypropylene units as well as a LDPE unit. Having entered the execution phase, 70% of the construction works at the Borouge 3 Project have been completed.

The company is also investing in plants and logistics hubs in Asia and an innovation centre in Abu Dhabi. The centre will focus on innovations for compounding as well as innovative plastics solutions in close cooperation with other local and international educational institutes and partnership with Borouge’s customers throughout the value chain. It came to support the company’s manufacturing facilities and focus on providing its customers with unique and innovative solutions that ensure durability, cost effectiveness, material saving, environmentally friendly and sustainability.

Committed to the principles of Responsible Care® and together with Borealis proactively, Borouge contributes towards addressing the world’s water and sanitation challenges through their Water for the World™ initiative.

Shanghai plant filing Borouge’s first patent of polypropylene solution

As a company focused on value creation through innovation, Borouge achieved a new step change in innovation by filing its first patent application in polypropylene (PP), a testament to its increasing capability in sales, marketing, product development and IPR protection.

Borouge’s first patent filed on May 22, 2012, involves a new PP blend that is used in appliances such as air conditioner parts. This PP blend is recognized with its reduced weight comparing to polymer materials conventionally used for the same purposes and without the need to cut new mould as shrinkage is very similar. The patented PP solution was made at Borouge’s Compounding Manufacturing Plant (CMP) in Shanghai and is used to replace Acrylonitrile Styrene (AS) and Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS) resins enabling the company to target a new area of PP applications.

The IPR protection Borouge has secured through filing its new patent would help enhance the innovativexs capabilities of the Company’s product development. It would also support the company’s marketing and sales activities, as well as the production of sustainable innovative PP solutions in China.

With the completion of the Borouge Innovation Centre in Abu Dhabi later this year, Borouge will be able to move a further step in innovation by producing more innovative solutions for the infrastructure, automotive and advanced packaging industries in close cooperation and partnership with Borouge’s customers throughout the value chain. Innovation will remain the main engine that drives Borouge towards ‘Shaping the Future with Plastics’.

Targeting Asia Growing Polyolefins Market

Borouge has invested approximately USD 60 million in a manufacturing plant in Shanghai. Resins are compounded in this plant specifically for customers in the growing automotive and appliance markets. The company is rapidly building up its technical and sales competence not only in China but also in the Asian region.

Borouge has been actively participating in China’s infrastructure projects across the country for applications in pipe systems, energy and communications networks. It supported the Beijing Olympics in 2008 and the Shanghai Expo in 2010 with advanced pipe technologies.

In India, Borouge is focusing on supplying its products to the end use industries. The company is following a gradual buildup strategy. While it maintains a network of agents in the country, it is increasingly complementing its indirect channel to market with direct sales. Borouge has a well-established operation in India with a sales office in Mumbai comprising approximately 20 people in sales and support functions across all Borouge application areas.

The Asian polyolefins market is expected to grow dramatically. These are attractive opportunities for Borouge and its new compounding manufacturing plant in Shanghai will help in optimizing its role in supplying this growing market.

Company Profile

The late H. H. Sheikh Rashid bin Saeed Al Maktoum, vice-president and prime minister of the UAE and Emir of Dubai (1958 – 1990), was among the early believers in transforming the UAE’s economy from an oil-based economy to a diversified economy by investing the wealth generated from the country’s oil resources in establishing the first-ofits- kind companies in each vital sector. Dubai Aluminium – Dubal – was founded in 1975 as an implementation of that vision. It was established as an aluminum manufacturer even though a downstream aluminium industry had not yet been established in the UAE. Thus, the company was founded to be international from day one.

At that time, Dubal was among the biggest basic industry development projects in the world. In November 1979, the first metal was produced; and in January 1980 commercial production began. Dubal’s plant comprised 360 reduction cells in three potlines with the capacity to produce 135,000 tonnes per annum, all of which was exported directly to the international market, initially mainly to the Far East and ASEAN region. In 1996, its metal had been shipped to the European market.

Today, after a series of successive expansion projects completed between 1991 and 2008, Dubal operates the world’s largest single-site primary aluminium smelter – in Jebel Ali – using pre-bake anode technology. Its facilities have grown to comprise a one million tonne per annum smelter (1,537 reduction cells in seven potlines), casting operations with the capacity to produce more than one million tonnes of cast products per annum in three main forms (foundry alloy, billets and high purity aluminium), a large carbon plant, a 2,350 MW power plant, port, storage and other facilities. In 2012, it produced 1,025,266 tonnes.

The company did succeed in exporting aluminum products to the world but also yielded distilled and potable water for its own needs and for the greater Dubai community by operating a 30 million imperial gallon per day desalination plant.

The growth of Dubal has been driven by a strategy focused primarily on organic growth. Since 2007, Dubal has begun to invest in core business opportunities where Dubal’s technical know-how and aluminium smelting expertise can be leveraged. The most significant development in this regard is Emirates Aluminium (Emal) at Al Taweelah, Abu Dhabi, in which Dubal has a 50 per cent share. Energizing of the 756 reduction cells in EMAL Phase I, with a total capacity of 750,000 tonnes per annum (since upgraded to 800,000 tonnes per annum), took place between 1 December 2009 and 31 December 2010 — with full production being reached four months ahead of schedule, yet within budget. Emal Phase II was announced in July 2011. A new 444-cell potline is being built which will increase Emal’s annual production capacity to 1.3 millon tonnes by the end of 2014. This will fulfill the overall objective of the development, which is to become one of the largest single-site producers of primary aluminium in the world.

With a view to securing part of its alumina requirements, Dubal has also made investments in green-field bauxite/ alumina projects that are in various stages of development. These projects are in Republic of Guinea in a joint venture with BHP Billiton, Global Alumina and Mubadala; Brazil in partnership with Norsk Hydro; and Cameroon with Hindalco and Hydromine. Dubal has also acquired a share in a calcined petroleum coke (CPC) development in China, thereby securing part of its CPC requirements.

To meet the needs of Dubal’s growing involvement in regional markets and to strengthen its brand internationally, offices have been established in Italy, South Korea, Switzerland and the USA.

In addition, Dubal is becoming a respected developer of advanced reduction cell technologies, following more than 25 years of efforts to develop its own smelting technology that can compete with similar technologies in the world market in terms of productivity, capacity and efficiency, while maintaining the highest standards of environmental conservation. These efforts have culminated most recently in two proprietary technologies.

DX Technology, where a demonstration line of 40 pots has operated at DUBAL’s Jebel Ali site since February 2008 and the operating amperage has increased from 340 kA to 380 kA, offering several benchmark attributes that provide significant advantages. DX Technology has been deployed in the 756 cells at EMAL Phase I.

DX+ Technology, an advanced version of DX Technology, where five new generation cells built in DUBAL’s pilot line have operated stably since August 2010, and the operating amperage has increased from 420 kA to 440 kA. DX+ Technology has been licensed to EMAL Phase II; and selected by Aluminium Bahrain for its Line 6 Bankable Feasibility study.

Success Story
Securing Growth

Driven by its vision to be among the world’s top five primary aluminium producers by 2015, Dubal has made investments in important greenalumina projects around the globe. In 2009, a joint venture with Norsk Hydro of Norway and Dubal was formed to build an alumina refinery ‘Companhia Alumina do Pará’ (“CAP”), in Brazil close to Alunorte refinery – the biggest in the world. Through these investments, Dubal aims to secure the raw materials required to fuel its future growth strategy.

The CAP refinery will have an initial capacity of 1.86 metric tonnes per annum through two lines, each one of 930,000 tons per year. The refinery’s capacity is expandable to 7.4 metric tonnes. DUBAL holds 19 per cent equity in the joint venture and additionally has secured 200,000 metric tonnes commercial alumina off-take for four years from Norsk Hydro.

Company Profile

On 24 November 1979, Dubai Cable Company “Ducab” legacy began with a single manufacturing factory in Jebel Ali Free Zone. The company is jointly owned by the Governments of Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

Thanks to the immense developmental activities undertaken at the Jebel Ali Free Zone, Ducab today enjoys a plethora of utility services and technical prowess. It has one of the most modern manufacturing units in the region and occupies an area of 590,000 square meters of land in Jebel Ali, Dubai and nearly 330,000 square meters in Mussafah, Abu Dhabi spread over 2 factories.

More than 115,000 copper tones equivalent of low voltage, medium and high voltage cables can be produced by Ducab’s plants. The company is emerging as the leader and the first choice of prestigious customers in the Middle East. The diversity of its products’ range has greatly contributed to the continuous growth and attracted a variety of clients locally and internationally. Currently, high voltage cables up to 400kV, medium voltage cables up to 33kV, low voltage power cables, control & auxiliary, wiring and lead-sheathed cables, low smoke and fume cables, cable components and accessories, as well as copper rod are the main products of Ducab.

Ducab was established to be an international company. Penetrating new markets and selling across regional markets are always within its strategy.

One of Ducab’s first international successes came when it secured a USD 3 million fire resistance cable delivery contract with the Delhi Metro, India in 2004. Ducab’s technical team successfully developed a range of fire-resistant (FR) cables that could withstand temperatures of 950° C for three hours surpassing the Delhi Metro project’s requirement of 750° C. The company will continue investing in India as it is a very promising market for cables manufacturers, given the large scale investment in infrastructural development.

In 2011, as a strategic step to increase its industrial capabilities and diversify its product lines, Ducab launched Ducab HV, a joint venture with the local water and electricity authorities in Abu Dhabi and Dubai. The newly formed venture came to offer Extra High Voltage infrastructural products (up to 400 kv) to markets in the GCC and beyond.

As a remarkable milestone, Ducab HV signed a USD 13.5 million contract to supply the Ministry of Electricity and Water (MEW) of Kuwait with 112 km of high voltage cables, the first contract signed for the export of the high voltage cables manufactured by Ducab’s new factory which was inaugurated in November 2011. The Ducab HV has already started providing the MEW in Kuwait with world-class high voltage cables of 132 kV and a diameter of 630 mm, which would require approximately 900 tonnes of copper.

As it moves to service international markets, focusing on traditional strengths of customer service and product quality will continue to drive the sale of UAE manufactured cables and allied products across the globe.

Given the undisputable quality of its cables, Ducab has been sought-after for high-performance cables for landmark projects locally and internationally. Some of its leading projects in the UAE include Burj Khalifa, Dubai Metro, Burj Al Arab, Atlantis, and Dubai International Airport.

Within the GCC, DUCAB has been involved in numerous major projects like the RAS Gas Project in Qatar, Al Manah Power Station in Oman, Bahrain City Centre and Bahrain Financial Harbour in Bahrain as well as the Kuwait National Petroleum Company in Kuwait.

Its commitment to quality and stringent testing procedures and its ardently belief in human capital development are what differentiate Ducab from its competitors. Cumulatively, Ducab’s team has over 10,000 years of cables & wires experience – a stunning figure. It has one of the lowest employee turnover rates in the region, which is testament to its ability to find, train and retain the best talent while ensuring they develop along with the organization.

Ducab’s success has been attested by accreditations and recognitions. The company has won a plethora of awards, including MRM-Manufacturing Excellence, ESMA- Quality Mark, ICD- Best Economic Value Creation. In addition, it has been recognized as a Superbrand four consecutive times, winning the Award in 2009, 2010, 2011 and now in 2012. In 2009, it was also honoured with the Mohammed Bin Rashid Business Excellence Award for their achievements in the manufacturing sector.

Company Profile

It was the year 1959 when the Dubai Refreshments Company (DRC) was incorporated in Dubai in 1959 by a Decree of His Highness, the Ruler of Dubai. This move was momentous as it was the first company to introduce soft drink manufacturing in the UAE. Over the decades, DRC has seen significant expansion of its operations, becoming one of the largest bottlers in the region and exporting to nations beyond.

The company is headquartered in Dubai, UAE where its current plant operates, providing it a strategically beneficial location as to efficiently target all its key local markets, while taking advantage of Dubai’s position as a major regional port in order to maximise its export business. DRC holds exclusive bottling rights for a broad portfolio of products under the agreement with PepsiCo.

Working together with PepsiCo, it has been a crucial partner in developing a strong brand identity as well as loyalty for Pepsi in the UAE, which is evidenced in the strong market share position of its products. DRC also differentiates itself through its ongoing commitment to excellence in customer service and in maintaining mutually beneficial relationships with all its stakeholders.

The emergence of the UAE as a regional hub for trade and commerce has been a driving force underpinning DRC’s success. As Dubai and the UAE have grown and developed, so too has DRC’s local customer base and ability to access export markets. In response to the growth in opportunity, DRC took the decision to build and operate a new state-ofthe- art facility known as the Greenfield Project.

Furthermore, DRC has also benefited from the infrastructure investments that have turned the UAE into an international hub, as these investments have allowed the Company to target a number of strategically important international markets efficiently. Having recognised the importance of this to a fastdeveloping region, the company commenced production with the first line in 1975 under the management of ‘Norcem AS’, a member of HeidelbergCement Group. Moreover, the development of an excellent infrastructure helped reduce transport times, thereby increasing its turnover.

Continuing its aim to provide the highest-standard products and best customer service, UCC obtained the ISO 9000 certification in 1994. What’s more, in the years 1980 and 2000, UCC added the American Petroleum Institute (API) Spec Q1 and the ISO 14001 certifications respectively, signalling its dedication to the environment.

In addition to the domestic market, with its proximity to the Saqr Port, UCC capitalised on the opportunity to serve the Middle East and East African markets. This provided it with a strategic advantage to import raw materials, machinery, spare parts and equipment. Based in the only Emirate in the UAE that produces oil well cement, white cement and lime, Union Cement Company (UCC) has emerged to be a major domestic supplier and exporter of cement and clinker.

When the company started out, its first and second production lines had production capacity of 750 tonnes per day each. Over the next two decades, this grew rapidly to meet local and regional demand. In 2006, UCC commissioned its milestone fourth plant, a 10,000 tonnes per day clinker production kiln that increased its capacity from 1.2 to 4.25 million tonnes per annum. In addition to maintaining consistently high production quality, the company offers a wide product range which includes ordinary Portland cement, high, moderate and low sulphate resisting cement and well cement. Moreover, UCC’s bulk tanker fleet offers the right competitive edge to deliver cement to its customers wherever needed. Showcasing formidable growth, UCC has taken its yearly sales from 275,000 tonnes in 1977 to over 3.0 million tonnes in 2008.

To date, its cement products have been used in several prestigious projects like the Dubai Golf Club, Burj Al Arab, and Abu Dhabi Mall, to name a few. As a sociallyresponsible organisation, UCC is also involved in a slew of social development activities and has commenced many environmental sustainability programmes. It also plays a positive role in the progress of the community though its Emaratisation initiatives and support for the national cadre. It was recognised for these efforts by the GCC Ministers of the Labor and Social Affairs in the year 2009 at the ceremonial function of the 26th conference.

Business wise, the new acquisition opened up new important sectors to the group including: high-barrier transparent and metalised film and labelling film, all sectors in which the former Radici Film gained a primary role over the years. Above all, Taghleef has now joined to its elite group of experts some of Europe’s finest minds behind the Film and BoPP industry in both R&D and Production.

And because it is a UAE company, where people come first, Taghleef Industries is also a worldwide leader in environmentally-friendly film manufacturing. The company has adopted the concept of bio-plastic (BoPla) film manufacturing, which uses a natural extract called PLA (Poly Lactic Acid) polymer as a base material for the film. All other raw materials involved in the manufacturing process are also from renewable resources. The new product line, branded Ingeo, is completely biodegradable and compostable; offering a wider range of end-of-life options, including recycling and industrial composting. To further compliment its environmental commitments, Taghleef Industries has come up with a new initiative in order to be able to quantify, monitor, compare and control the quantity of greenhouse gases emitted from their site and their products.

Company Profile

Gulf Craft, the Middle East’s and Asia’s leading fiberglass yacht and boat manufacturer, Driven by an entrepreneurial spirit, Mohammed Hussein Al-Shaali, a young businessman from the UAE, together with his 2 brothers, decided to follow their passion of the sea with the idea of constructing 15 to 18-ft fishing boats for local use. In 1982, the ambitious entrepreneurship consequently established Gulf Craft.

The 1990s witnessed major successes when Gulf Craft became a yacht manufacturer, not only broadening its products’ base but also its types of clients. The first yacht that was built by the company was the Adora 53 in 1992. In 1999, the company grew to a higher niche by launching the super yacht, Millennium 118.

In the early 2000s, Gulf Craft began looking at the world as its market place and that changed the culture of the company. It launched Majesty Yachts brand of luxury yachts in 2003 to increasingly serve the European market.

Over the last three decades, the company has succeeded to grow to become one of the most internationally renowned names in the building of various types of multi-decked motoryachts. Gulf Craft’s yachts and boats today are exported to over 40 countries around the globe. The superb delivery track record the company has shown over these years driven by the foresighted visionary management along with the UAE’s supportive foreign trade policy and central location are the main drivers that took the company’s wings to fly towards the international competitive market.

In addition, Technology has always been a decisive factor in the world of manufacturing. And for Gulf Craft, the use of technologies such as sandwich construction and vacuum infusion processes, water-jet drives engineered on large yachts, and gyro marine stabilizers, was an essential step in meeting the highest of market expectations.

The Majesty Yachts, a new brand introduced by Gulf Craft in 2003, has boosted the company’s position on an international stage. The company, through this brand, has not only succeeded to enter the European market but also has propelled into the international spotlight and began to be considered as a global player with a respected product range. Gulf Craft’s superyachts are continuing to head to European waters with flag ships like the Majesty 135 and the Majesty 125.

The company has shown a continuous strong performance, succeeding to increase its exports to reach 70% of its production. This has indeed made it one of the distinguished UAE Highflyers in its second edition.

Today, Gulf Craft employs 1,500 staff in its facilities located in 3 emirates of the UAE as well as a shipyard in the Maldives. With a product line ranging from 26 foot runabouts and sport fishing boats, to 135 feet luxury motor yachts, the company has an annual production capacity of more than 500 boats through its three brands Majesty Yachts, Oryx and Silvercraft.

Company Profile

When people started converting their furniture that were made from animal wool and palm trees wood to more manufactured and developed furniture items in 1970s, Abbas Ali Al-Hazeem, a young UAE entrepreneur, took the initiative of establishing and operating a company that would make its way in the furniture realm. In 1974, “International Foam and Furnishing Company” was established to produce polyurethane foam and sell furniture in Al Rashiyah area, Dubai. Within a short span of time, the company was recognized with a wide range of foam, one of the basic building blocks of the furniture industry. By mastering this essential product and through development and integration, the national Dubai-based enterprise turned into a multinational company that is not only specialized in foam production but also in the manufacturing of different sleep systems along with bedding accessories like pillows, comforters and sofa sets.

The foresighted industrial vision and strong business values embedded by the company’s cofounder established the fundamentals on the company’s structure to be international in an early stage. Today, Intercoil has 7 showrooms in the UAE, 2 showrooms in Oman, 2 showrooms in Kuwait, 3 showrooms in Saudi Arabia and one showroom in Qatar, Lebanon, Egypt, Iran and India. In addition, Intercoil’s products are present in 17 countries across two continents.

Committed to its expansion roadmap, Intercoil is in the way of boosting its geographical reach by setting-up subsidiaries and distribution points to new territories such as CIS countries, South-East-West Africa, Central Asia and Eastern Europe. These fast developing economic regions will contribute significantly to the overall growth of Intercoil.

The company’s success is not only presented through the regional distribution of its highly-competitive products but also by the creation of several world-class manufacturing organizations in several countries in the Middle East region. Arriving at the top is a relatively-straightforward journey but maintaining that position is the real challenge. This principle is implanted in the company’s strategy.
