Company Profile

The Abu Dhabi National Energy Company (TAQA) remains one of the most distinguished UAE-based global investors. The company was founded in 2005 when the Abu Dhabi Water and Electricity Authority (ADWEA) privatized its power and water assets. Even though the company has significantly grown in a remarkable fast pace and stepped into five different continents – North America, Europe, Africa and Asia, retaining its position as the premier energy company in the home market remains of such immense importance.

Today, TAQA is responsible for 98% of the emirate’s water and electricity needs. In total, it owns seven plants in the UAE, producing nearly 10,783 mega watts of power and 787 million imperial gallons of water a day of desalination capacity. With a combined total of over USD 23.4 billion in assets, TAQA enjoys a stable fiscal position, which does not only enable the company to operate with confidence but also to expand regionally and globally.

TAQA has divided its operations into three business lines, namely; downstream, midstream and upstream, since operating at a global level has been its primary goal, matching the company’s commitment towards becoming a global integrated energy provider. In the upstream sector, TAQA operates in the exploration and production of crude oil and natural gas fields, which take the company to the Netherlands, UK, Canada and the US.

Midstream operations in TAQA were designed to become the company’s logistics arm in terms of storage, transportation and processing infrastructure. This is demonstrated by the company’s growing portfolio of midstream assets in the UK and Dutch North Sea and the ongoing creation of the Bergermeer Gas Storage Consortium.

This business line enjoys increasing popularity because it handles end-user energy needs, from Water Desalination to Power Generation. Not only does it provide the UAE’s capital, Abu Dhabi, with 90 percent of its daily electricity and water needs, but it is also expanding globally with operations in India, Morocco, Saudi Arabia and Ghana.

To provide further flexibility for its operations, some of these business lines are operated through TAQA-owned local entities; TAQA North, TAQA Bratani, TAQA Europa and TAQA Energy.

In August 2007, TAQA stepped into North America by acquiring Northrock from Pogo Producing Company and amalgamated with TAQA North. The company has subsequently entered into agreements to acquire Pioneer Canada Ltd., a subsidiary of US-based Pioneer Natural Resources Company, and Calgary-based PrimeWest Energy Trust. Today, TAQA North has a diversified asset base with operations in both Canada (British Columbia, Alberta and Saskatchewan) and the United States (Montana, North Dakota and Wyoming).

TAQA Bratani, another subsidiary of TAQA and one of the fastest-growing exploration and production companies working on the United Kingdom Continental Shelf UKCS, was established to build a long-term future for TAQA’s growing North Sea portfolio which began when the group acquired Talisman’s interest in the Brae Complex which included the SAGE pipeline and onshore terminal. In 2008, TAQA acquired a 100% interest in the Northern North Sea Tern, Kestrel, Eider, Cormorant and Pelican Fields, a combined 26.73% interest in the Dana operated Hudson field and a 24% interest in the Sullom Voe Terminal. In addition, TAQA Bratani operates the Brent System on behalf of itself and co-venturers in the North Sea.

TAQA’s expansion into new markets partially through acquiring stakes in major operating companies in the field of oil and gas continued to flourish. TAQA Energy B.V. was established in Netherlands to explore and produce gas and condensates from wells located onshore in the Alkmaar region and offshore in the Dutch North Sea. In 2009, TAQA Energy acquired DSM Energie Holding, a Dutch oil production company.

In India, TAQA has sat up the expansion plans for its power plant in Neyveli in order to double its capacity from 250 Mega Watts to 500 Mega Watts. The plant is planned to be operational by 2015. Currently, the 250 Mega Watts lignitefired plant is supplying its entire electricity generation to the Tamil Nadu Electricity Board under a thirty year agreement. The plant is owned and operated by ST-CMS Electric Company Private Limited which is 100% owned by TAQA.

Regionally, TAQA holds strategic shares in a number of leading energy companies. In Saudi Arabia, it is a major shareholder in Jubail Energy Company which owns the Jubail Cogeneration Plant, a 250 MW plant that began providing energy to the SADAF Petrochemical Plant in 2005. In Oman TAQA acquired a 40% interest in SOHAR Aluminium including the 1,000 MW captive combined cycle power station. The Sohar aluminium power plant powers the company’s aluminium smelter operations which produce 350,000 tonnes of the metal each year.

The acquisition of Jorf Lasfar Energy Company, JLEC, Morocco’s first independent power producer, also provides a clear example of the way TAQA paves its way as a responsible global power company. While the acquisition itself serves a business purpose, the deal enabled TAQA to actually double the production capacity of JLEC, thus furthering the reach of the national electricity network in Morocco and helping millions of people make better use of energy in their daily lives. Because of the dynamic nature of the Moroccan economy, the expansion of JLEC plants has a direct positive effect in the central region of this North West African country. From a business perspective, JLEC emerges as an example of TAQA’s merger, negotiation and project management capabilities. Two new coal fired units of 350MW each are expected to become fully operational by the end of 2014.

In addition to its strong background backing, TAQA developed its own philosophy of financial stability and business growth. The company remains committed to the principle of diversifying its overall revenue mix to mitigate risk and provide greater stability and flexibility through lower commodity price cycles. Being a worldwide energy provider, TAQA’s commitment to continual and sustainable improvements in HSSE (health, safety, security and environmental performance), is demonstrated through a worldwide internal program to establish and maintain common HSSE procedures and standards. Today, almost all TAQA operations are either recommended for international certification (such as ISO 14001) or granted global recognition based on external audits (such as the WORK SAFE ALBERTA award).

Success Stories
Building Europe’s largest open access gas storage facility

The Bergermeer gas storage plant in Alkmaar in the Netherlands is one of TAQA’s marque projects after receiving approval from the Dutch government in May, 2012.

When the facility is completed in 2015, it will be one of the largest in Europe, storing up to 4.1 billion cubic metres of gas, which would heat 2.5 million households in the Netherlands. It will also double the Netherlands’ storage capacity and turn the country into a major gas hub in northern Europe.

The scale of the project in Alkmaar, 40 km north of Amsterdam, is immense. With project partner EBN, TAQA will invest EUR 800 million in drilling 14 new wells, building a gas treatment installation and constructing pipelines.

During construction, more than 1,200 people will directly work on the project.

Taqa: Increasing footprint in Africa

TAQA has increased its global footprint in Africa by buying a leading power company in Ghana. The Takoradi International Company operates the Takoradi 2 Power Plant in Takoradi, 200 km west of Accra. The facility has been converted from oil to gas in a bid to reduce CO2 emissions. Last year, TAQA received government approval and agreed new financing to expand the plant, which produces 15% of Ghana’s energy needs CHECK.

The project will increase output from 220 MW to approximately 330 MW without requiring additional fuel. This will add 50% more capacity without increasing CO2 emissions.

Company Profile

Dana Gas PJSC is the Middle East’s first and largest regional private sector natural gas company. It was established in late 2005 as part of an initial public offering on the Abu Dhabi Stock Exchange (ADX). There were 400,000 investors from over 100 nationalities making it one of the region’s most successful initial public offerings. The Company is headquartered in Sharjah, UAE and possesses a network of offices in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kurdistan Region of Iraq and Egypt. It currently has assets and projects in oil and gas exploration and production, processing, transportation and marketing in several countries and aims to play a major role in the rapidly growing natural gas business through the Middle East, North Africa and South Asia region across the entire gas value chain.

Dana Gas continues to expand its business interests in the gas sector throughout the region, including upstream exploration and production and downstream gas-related industries. The company is also building important strategic alliances and partnerships at both the regional and international level. Acquisition of strategically important assets in new and growing gas markets is an important part of Dana Gas’ objectives.

The Company has accomplished a number of significant business development activities including securing strategic positions in existing projects, entering into strategic alliances and developing new opportunities in related fields with first class partners.

In 2007, Dana Gas accomplished its most significant transaction to date acquiring Centurion Energy International Inc. Centurion had exploration and production interests in Egypt, Tunisia and Offshore West Africa. Centurion exited the interests in Tunisia and West Africa and the company was re-named “Dana Gas Egypt”. Dana Gas operates across three geographical locations: Egypt, the Kurdistan region of Iraq and UAE.

In Egypt, Dana Gas is 100% operator and currently produces from 11 fields in the Nile Delta and is 50% operator and producer, alongside Sea Dragon Energy, from one field in Upper Egypt. During 2011, Dana Gas Egypt produced 77.67 billion cubic feet of gas and 2.6 million barrels of liquids. The focus for 2012 was to drill 9 additional exploration wells, tie in additional production from Nile Delta gas fields and expand the capacity of the El Wastani gas plant in the Nile Delta region. In addition, Dana Gas has a 26.4% stake in an LPG extraction plant at Ras Shukheir on the Gulf of Suez that is expected to came on stream in the second half of 2012.

In the Kurdistan region of Iraq, Dana Gas holds a 40% interest in Pearl Petroleum Company (PPCL) with three other shareholders – Crescent Petroleum (40%), OMV (10%) and MOL (10%). Crescent and Dana Gas jointly operate the Khor Mor Field producing gas, condensate and LPG. The gas is supplied to two power stations in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq. In 2011, two LPG trains were commissioned, the first started LPG production in January and the second in June and the Company achieved an average production rate for the year of 23,700 boepd. Longer term plans are to increase production capacity to meet local gas requirements and for export regionally and to Europe.

Dana Gas has Sharjah Offshore Concession. The concession contains the Zora gas field, currently under development. The targeted production is 50 to 60 millions of standard cubic feet per day. Dana Gas also owns a major 600 million of standard cubic feet per day (MMscfd) of gas sweetening and sulphur production plant, and a major onshore and offshore gas pipeline system with a capacity of 1000MMscfd, through its wholly owned subsidiaries, SajGas and UGTC respectively. Additionally, Dana Gas owns a 35% interest in Crescent Natural Gas Corporation Limited (CNGCL), the marketing organisation. CNGC holds long-term gas off-take agreements with the electricity and water authorities and with major industrial corporations in the UAE.

Dana Gas is expanding its activities in all elements of the natural gas value-chain, including upstream exploration and production; through the midstream transmission and distribution of gas including LNG trading; and downstream into gas-related industries and petrochemicals.

Dana Gas seeks an active role as an agent of change and wishes to contribute to the development of the communities in which it operates. The company’s CSR programme is about directly contributing to economic and social development in a way that safeguards the natural resources and the environment whilst respecting the rights of each individual. The company has undertaken CSR activities since its establishment and prides itself in continuing along this path despite the financial crisis.

Dana Gas’s contributions focused on the environment, education, sports, health and sustainable community businesses. One of the programmes is “education on environment”, which works closely with Bee’ah, a public private company in partnership with the city of Sharjah on a comprehensive bilingual (Arabic & English) programme to educate the school children in the importance of caring for the environment, waste management and the three R’s: reduce, recycle and re-use. The work takes the form of both interactive sessions with pupils as well as the provision of digital tools to teachers enabling the company to reach over 100,000 students of grades 1 to 12 residing in the UAE. Dana Gas is also the sponsor of the “Dana Gas Chemical Engineer Chair” at the American University of Sharjah, where it works closely with the university to develop cutting-edge research in the field of oil and gas engineering.

Success Stories
catalyst for Kurdistan’s economic development

In April 2007, Dana Gas and Crescent Petroleum signed longterm agreements and a Strategic Alliance Protocol with the Kurdistan Regional Government of Iraq to appraise, develop, process and deliver 200 million cubic feet per day of natural gas supplies from the Khor Mor Gas Field urgently needed for power generation. Dana Gas, with its partners Crescent Petroleum, OMV (Austria) and MOL (Hungary), has invested approximately USD 930 million in the project, this involved the construction of 180km of pipelines and two LPG processing trains at the Khor Mor Field. The project will sustain two power plants to generate 1250 MW providing cost effective electricity for 4 million Iraqi’s, freeing the extra 300 MW of electricity to other provinces, saving Iraq USD 2.5 billion a year in imported fuels and creating 2000 new jobs.

The first phase of the project was accomplished within record time, highlighted by the servicing and completion of the wells, laying the pipeline across challenging mountainous and hazardous terrain and the installation of brand new processing facilities. 50,000 tonnes of equipment were imported, with the first gas production commencing on 5 October 2008.

Today, the Kurdistan region enjoys 24 hours per day of reliable electric power, as compared to barely two hours per day before the company’s investments. This electricity supply has powered rapid development of the regional economy and is a major factor in Kurdistan outpacing the development in the rest of federal Iraq.

The partnership will also appraise the potential of the Chemchemal Gas Field for development, as part of their strategy to advance regional economic benefits through development of the region’s substantial gas resources. Khor Mor and Chemchemal are major gas fields in northern Iraq.

Dana Gas – grabbing an opportunity in Egypt’s gas market

In 2007, Dana Gas acquired Centurion Energy International Inc. At the time, Centurion had exploration and production interests in Egypt, Tunisia and offshore West Africa. The acquisition greatly enhanced Dana Gas’s international standing and gave it entrance into Egypt’s gas market. Subsequently, Dana Gas has exited the blocks in Tunisia and West Africa, and has renamed Centurion to Dana Gas Egypt.

At the time, in Egypt, the company was involved in upstream activities in two exploration blocks in the Nile Delta region and an exploration block in Upper Egypt. Since the acquisition, the company has appraised, developed and delivered on their promise to expand their Egyptian operations. In 2008, it announced an exploration and development programme involving 19 new wells with an investment of $170 million. This aggressive drilling campaign proved successful, with eight discoveries in 2009 and a total of 16 onshore gas fields now discovered, and 11 of which are fully developed and producing. An oil discovery was made in the Upper Egypt region, which is now the first and only commercial oil production in the province. Dana Gas Egypt and Sea Dragon Energy share the operator role in the jointly-owned Kom Ombo concession where the Al Baraka oil field is located.

Company Profile

The GCC region is known as much for its rich energy resources as its expertise in the exploration and production of the same. The United Arab Emirates, as one of the most diversified economies within this region, has taken significant steps towards enabling sustainable growth of the energy sector at home and encouraging entrepreneurial ambitions, while sharing its capabilities with the world at large.

Among the pioneers in the nation’s thriving oil & gas sector is the Sharjah-headquartered Crescent Petroleum. It is the oldest private exploration and production company in the Middle East, with over 40 years of experience as an international operator in numerous countries including Egypt, Pakistan, Yemen, Canada, Yugoslavia, Tunisia, Argentina, in addition to its continuing operations at home and abroad.

It is part of the Crescent Group, one of the most progressive business groups in the Middle East & North Africa (MENA) Region, operating through more than 25 diversified companies across energy and non-energy sectors and has an international presence in over 20 countries across the globe.

Crescent Petroleum has been operating as a regional upstream oil & gas company in the UAE. The company began its activities in the early 1970s and was the first regional, independent, privately-owned Middle Eastern petroleum company to engage in the acquisition, exploration and development of petroleum concessions as well as the production and sale of crude oil, petroleum products and natural gas.

This forerunner traces its origins to Buttes Gas & Oil Co. International Inc., a wholly owned subsidiary and holder of a Concession granted in 1969 by the Sharjah Government, pursuant to which the Mubarek Field was discovered offshore Sharjah in the early 1970s. By the mid-eighties, the Crescent Petroleum Group was fully established, appropriately reorganised, and making its mark on the international energy scene.

Crescent Petroleum’s continued search for, and identification of, opportunities in the oil & gas sectors resulted in securing numerous international oil concessions worldwide. At the end of the 1980s it held concessions in Argentina, Canada, Yugoslavia, France, Tunisia, UAE, and in the 1990s, added to these with concessions in Egypt, Pakistan and Yemen.

For regulatory and other purposes, Crescent Petroleum has created special-purpose vehicles for each of its key operations. By adopting this structure it has enabled the company to achieve flexibility, transparency and efficiency of its operations. Crescent Petroleum has also been instrumental in establishing Dana Gas PJSC which is the first major private sector natural gas resource enterprise to be established in the Gulf Region and currently owns and operates assets in Egypt, the UAE and the Kurdistan Region of Iraq.

Crescent Petroleum’s current focus is upon energy opportunities in the Middle East, specifically within the Gulf region, but more widely also encompassing North Africa, the Caspian Sea region and South Asia. Because of this focus, and its long history of regional operating experience, it is intimately familiar with business in this region.

Its success in the energy industry across the region goes beyond the function of being technically, commercially and financially sound, but encompasses an in-depth knowledge and understanding of the cultural context within which business is conducted. Furthermore, Crescent Petroleum’s powerful network of relationships, both across the region and globally, provides it with a distinct advantage in concluding deals.

The particular area of focus for the long-term growth of Crescent Petroleum’s business is the development, marketing and utilisation of natural gas assets in the region. Crescent Petroleum has pioneered this role since the mid-1980s within this vital hydrocarbon rich region of the world, which contains over 40% of global gas reserves, by successfully implementing the first two cross-border pipeline deals in the region.

Crescent Petroleum has, over the years, built up a competent and internationally-experienced team of energy professionals, enabling it to operate projects which are challenging from the geological and engineering perspectives. Through its expertise and established logistical support base, the company has also been able to demonstrate its proven track record of project management on behalf of other oil companies in an efficient and costeffective manner.

Furthermore, it continues to participate in the global oil & gas industry with a particular focus on the region comprised of the countries within the Middle East, North Africa and South Asia (MENASA). As the world energy consumption projections continue to see a rapid upward trend, Crescent Petroleum is well-prepared to meet a significant percentage of the needs, while ensuring an optimal utilisation of resources.

Success Stories
Leading energetically from the capital of Iraq

UAE and Iraq have been natural trading and investment partners with trade between the countries dating back to historical times and growing year-on-year. Paralleling the close relations between the two nations, Crescent Petroleum is the only international oil company to have had a continuous presence in Baghdad for almost 20 years. The Company has been actively engaged with the Iraqi Ministry of Oil since the late 1980s, having carried out studies and training as well as completed a full field development plan for the Ratawi oil field in Basra province. Ratawi is one of the major super-giant fields with over 10 billion barrels of oil in place that are earmarked for speedy development to boost the oil production capacity of Iraq.

The company’s reputation can be gauged by the fact that it was one of the select international oil companies encouraged to visit Baghdad to negotiate petroleum development contracts for over ten giant undeveloped fields each with indicated reserves between one and ten billion barrels. In 2005, Crescent Petroleum signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Oil Exploration Company (OEC) of Iraq, a division of the Iraqi Ministry of Oil, which was one of the first agreements of its kind with a Middle Eastern petroleum company. The MOU covers technical co-operation and training and includes an exploration and appraisal study in southern Iraq. Crescent Petroleum, in partnership with its affiliate Dana Gas, is currently implementing a major integrated natural gas project in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq.

Furthermore, it has plans on the anvils for utilising currently flared gas and launching a Basra Gas City project on an even larger scale than the one in Kurdistan. As of now, its continuous presence has grown to include offices in Baghdad, Erbil and Basra, all staffed 100% by qualified and experienced Iraqis

Bridging the energy-rich region with South Asia

South Asia is a huge and growing energy market, while the GCC region has several proven gas reserves that could meet this need. Crescent Petroleum identified the potential of the enormous gas reserves of the North Field of Qatar to meet the needs of this important market.

Showcasing a major private sector initiative to focus on commercial fundamentals, Crescent Petroleum’s launched the Gulf-South Asia Gas Project (GUSA). It was the first workable scheme for producing, transmitting by pipeline and delivering natural gas from Qatar to Pakistan and eventually to other parts of the Indian subcontinent.

Crescent Petroleum is the developer and lead sponsor for this project. The Company has successfully advanced the GUSA Project to its present state of pre-implementation maturity including completing a detailed route survey for the whole pipeline route from source in Qatar to its final destination in Pakistan.

It comprises two main parts: the upstream development to produce the gas at the source in Qatar and the pipeline, which will transport the gas to Pakistan. The former part includes the development of a sector of the North Field of Qatar to produce natural gas of up to 1600 MMscfpd of pipeline quality gas for export to the Bridging the energy-rich region with South Asia Pakistan markets. While the pipeline between Qatar and Pakistan, being 1,186 kilometres in distance, supports the economics of a large diameter pipeline project.

The company’s has to-date invested over US$ 30 million in this significant project, which carries enormous environmental benefits, regionally and globally. It has successfully completed the engineering design of the pipeline, while the routing of the pipeline based on a detailed onshore and offshore route survey is currently under progress.

Company Profile

Abu Dhabi National Oil Company (ADNOC) was established in 1971 as the investment arm of the Government of Abu Dhabi in the exploration and production of crude oil and gas, oil and natural gas processing, chemicals and petrochemicals, maritime transportation, and refined products distribution. After four decades of excellence in operations, ADNOC has become one of the world’s largest oil exploration and production companies.

ADNOC’s position is dependent and closely linked to the strategic role the United Arab Emirates plays in the global oil and gas scene. The UAE is the world one of the world top 10 crude oil producers and so exporters. As of 2009, the UAE’s proven oil reserves were estimated to be 97.8 billion barrels. Abu Dhabi leads the other emirates, which make up the UAE federation, with 92.2 billion barrels, followed by Dubai with four billion barrels, Sharjah with 1.5 and Ras Al Khaimah with 100 million barrels.

The Supreme Petroleum Council (SPC), chaired by His Highness Sheikh Khalifa Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, President of the UAE and Ruler of Abu Dhabi, formulates and oversees the implementation of Abu Dhabi petroleum policies.

ADNOC is given over 60 per cent of the petroleum and gas projects in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi. ADNOC’s efforts in the exploration and production field have concentrated on assessing undiscovered reserves and optimizing hydrocarbon recovery by improving the reservoir management. And in order to do so, there have been several mutual cooperation and joint ventures with leading international companies where ADNOC has been and will continue to be an ideal strategic partner.

Over the last few years significant achievements were made in the expansion and development of gas fields to meet increased demand from industry gas users and gas injection requirements in order to enhance the oil and condensate recovery from the producing fields. ADNOC is committed to sustainable development.

ADNOC has subsidiary companies working in the various fields of the oil, gas, and petrochemical industry as well as crude oil and gas transport and services. Many of these companies comprise joint ventures, partnerships and contractual co-operations with international companies from the United States, United Kingdom, France, Portugal, Austria, Germany, Japan, China and more. These subsidiary companies form the ADNOC Group of Companies comprising ADNOC’s overall activities. In exploration and production of oil and gas, there come Abu Dhabi Company for Onshore Oil Operations (ADCO), Abu Dhabi Marine Operating Company (ADMA-OPCO), Zakum Development Company (ZADCO) and National Drilling Company (NDC). In gas processing, there come Abu Dhabi Gas Industries Ltd. (GASCO), Abu Dhabi Gas Liquefaction Company Ltd. (ADGAS), Al Hosn Gas and ADNOC Linde Industrial Gases Company Ltd. (ELIXIER). Abu Dhabi Oil Refining Company (TAKREER) and Abu Dhabi National Oil Company for Distribution (ADNOC Distribution) are categorized under refining and distribution of petroleum products. Under petrochemicals, there come Abu Dhabi Polymers Company Ltd. (Borouge) and Ruwais Fertilizer Industries (FERTIL). Petroleum Services Company (ESNAAD) and Abu Dhabi Petroleum Ports Operating Company (IRSHAD) are involved in logistics. And Abu Dhabi National Tanker Company and National Gas Shipping Company Ltd. (ADNATCO-NGSCO) are engaged in maritime transportation.

The UAE crude oil exports are estimated at 2.8 million barrels per day. But in line with ADNOC’s mission of exploiting oil and gas reserves in order to secure adequate and reliable supplies, the company continues to develop its exploration & production and refining infrastructure with plans to raise Oil production to 3.5 million barrels per day by the year 2017. In the Gas sector, Abu Dhabi embarked and successfully overcame the challenges of developing the first unconventional sour gas field. The Shah Gas field will start production in 2014 adding to the overall gas supplies in Abu Dhabi.

A closer look at how these subsidiaries operate and work gives a glimpse of the philosophy behind the company and its group’s business model. For example, at the downstream level, ADNOC Distribution has a domestic, regional and global network of distributors and retail outlets selling oilbased auto-lubricants and greases that comply with the most top state-of-the-art international standards and health, safety and environment preservation measurements. Most significantly was ADNOC’s exclusive deal with German auto maker BMW’s dealers for supplying lubricants. Also, Borouge has established an international network of hubs and offices to follow up and handle its products of polypropylene and polyethylene to end clients. Success Story
Gemasolar – a revolution in the CSP sector

In October 2011, Torresol Energy, a joint venture between Masdar and SENER – the leading Spanish engineering and construction firm, held the official inauguration ceremony of the 19.9MW concentrated solar power (CSP) plant, Gemasolar.

Gemasolar is the first commercial plant in the world to use molten salt thermal storage in a central tower configuration with a heliostat field - an efficient system that improves the dispatchability of electric power from renewable sources.

By operating for a total of 6,450 hours a year at full capacity and producing a net total of over 110 GWhe per year, Gemasolar can supply electricity to a population of 27,500 households in the South of Spain.

This annual production of Gemasolar (110GWhe) is the equivalent of the energy generated in a conventional thermal plant burning 89,000 tons of lignite or the converted energy of 217,000 oil barrels. Therefore, the plant is expected to save more than 30,000 tonnes of CO2 emission a year.

Torresol Energy also recently announced commissioning of the Valle 1 and Valle 2 plants - two identical 50 MW parabolic trough plants, located in Cádiz, in the South of Spain.

Success Story
Smooth-sailing in Singapore

ENOC’s expansion over the years has been driven by attributes of proactively identifying problems, seizing new growth opportunities, taking calculated risks and achieving business excellence. In the year 1999, ENOC established a formidable international presence by starting its trading operations in Singapore, becoming the first Middle Eastern oil company to do so.

Horizon Singapore Terminals Private Limited (HSTPL) is situated on Jurong Island, the petrochemical hub of Singapore and the world’s top bunkering port by volume. The strategic location of the terminal on the southwestern quadrant of Singapore provides for smooth marine traffic movements for tankers. Horizon Singapore Terminals Private Limited is also an integral component of the global pricing mechanism for crude and refined petroleum products in the region.

ENOC Singapore has grown from strength to strength and has made rapid strides and achieved recognition as a Global Trader under the Global Trader Program (GTP) from the International Enterprise Singapore and was included among the top ‘Singapore 1000 company’ for the last five years.

Company Profile

Dragon Oil was incorporated in Ireland in 1971 and moved headquarters to Dubai, UAE in 1998. Since then it has become an independent international oil and gas exploration, development and production company. Emirates National Oil Company Limited (ENOC), a company of the Government of Dubai, owns approximately 54% of the company’s ordinary share capital. Dragon Oil is registered in Ireland with a primary listing on the Irish Stock Exchange and, since 6 April 2010, in accordance with recent changes to the UK listing Regime, has been designated as a ‘premium listing’ on the London Stock Exchange.

Dragon Oil has been evaluating targets that fit its criteria within Africa, Central Asia, the Middle East and selectively south-east Asia in order to create a diversified balanced portfolio of exploration and development assets for the Group.

The company was established to be international since day one. In 1999, a Production Sharing Agreement was signed with the Government of Turkmenistan to re-develop oil and gas resources in the Cheleken Contract Area for a term of 25 years from 1st of May 2000 and an exclusive right to negotiate an extension of not less than 10 years. Thus, Dragon Oil Turkmenistan was established as the operator and granted a production license for that purpose. The Cheleken Contract Area, in the eastern section of the Caspian Sea, offshore Turkmenistan is the principal producing asset of Dragon Oil.

Dragon Oil has been working in Turkmenistan for more than 12 years. More than USD 2.5 billion has been invested in expanding the oil production in the Cheleken Contract Area, making Dragon Oil one of the largest foreign investorsin Turkmenistan.

The Group is producing from a significant number of new and old wells and has an aggressive development program comprising the drilling of new wells and an ongoing workover program. The average daily gross field production has increased from approximately 7,000 barrels of oil per day (bopd) in 2000 to over 67,600 bopd of average daily gross production in 2012 with the 2012 December average gross production of 73,500 bopd.

As of 31st December 2012, Dragon Oil had proven, probable oil and condensate reserves of 677 million barrels and 59 million of oil and condensate contingent resources, 1.5 trillion cubic feet of gas reserves (corresponding to 250 million barrels of oil equivalent) and 1.4 trillion cubic feet of gas resources.

As part of its expansion strategy, in October 2011, Dragon Oil signed a farm-in agreement with a wholly owned subsidiary of Cooper Energy Limited for a 55% participating interest in the Bargou Exploration Permit, offshore Tunisia. The Permit is located in the Gulf of Hammamet in the Mediterranean Sea and covers an area of 4,616 square kilometers with predominantly offshore exploration prospects.

In 2012, in a consortium of companies Dragon Oil was awarded an exploration, development and production contract (EDPSC) for Block 9 in Iraq’s fourth bidding round. Block 9 is located in the Basra province. In January 2013, the Iraqi Ministry of Oil and the Tender Committee signed the final service contract for the block with the consortium (Kuwait Energy at 70% and operator and Dragon Oil at 30%); this marks the commencement of exploration activities by the consortium in Block 9.

In November 2012, a consortium of companies comprising Dragon Oil, Kuwait Energy, Turkiye Petrolleri A.O. (TPAO) and the Ghazanfar Group, submitted a bid for two blocks and later, in December 2012, was selected as the winning bidder for two blocks, Sanduqli and Mzar-i-Sharif, of the Afghan-Tajik Phase 1 Oil and Gas Tender. The consortium has been invited to enter into negotiations with the Afghanistan Ministry of Mines for the exploration, development and production activities in the two blocks, which are expected to take place and conclude in first quarter of 2013.

Dragon Oil has been looking for opportunities in countries with significant hydrocarbon resource in its regions of interest that represent potentially attractive diversification opportunities for the Group.

Success Story
Empowering the Turkmen national human capital

Dragon Oil has over 1,400 employees in Turkmenistan of which in almost 1,200 are Turkmen nationals, making the group the single biggest employer in Hazar, the town closest to its operations in the Cheleken Contract Area. The employer status and importance for the community impose big responsibilities on the Group to be a dependable, ethical and conscientious partner.

In Turkmenistan, developing our the company’s employees’ skills and abilities is the core priority of its HR strategy. Dragon Oil has developed and implemented a Training Strategy and Plan for both professional and skilled workers, some of which are targeted, on-the-job training, in-house and external trainings offered to the people that will gain the most benefit from it, both in-house in the field, at the headquarters in Dubai and other locations worldwide.

In 2010, Dragon Oil established a new training centre, the Centre of Excellence, at its site in Hazar to provide training and development to its local employees. In 2011, 52 in house and onsite and external courses were organized by the training team at the Centre of Excellence.

Over the past few years, the Group has invested significant time and money in a number of training and development initiatives and programmes. This includes in-house and external training courses for developing the key potential and talented employees covering their technical and soft skills.
