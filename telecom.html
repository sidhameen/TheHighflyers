Company Profile

Technology has eased the ways for people to stay in touch and connect across borders. The United Arab Emirates, with its policy openness and sharing, has been at the forefront of both the telecommunication as well as the digital revolution in the region. For nearly 40 years, Etisalat has helped the UAE sustain its position as the region’s main hub for business, trade and foreign investment by providing reliable and high-quality services. It has been the key to powering its home country into the top ten nations list for adoption of technology by providing the latest advancements first.

Emirates Telecommunication Corporation (Etisalat), was established in the year 1976 as a joint venture between international Aeradio Limited, a British Company and local partners. Today, it is the Middle East’s leading telecommunications operator and one of the largest corporations in the six among the GCC countries.

Starting off by unleashing the potential of technology as a pioneer, Etisalat launched the Middle East’s first mobile network in the year 1982. The next year saw its ownership structure change for stronger foundations with the UAE government holding 60% of the company and the remaining 40% publicly-traded. This combination between public and private sector made the organisation moved quickly toward the modern innovation in telecommunication fields.Continuing its track-record of innovation, it introduced the Middle East’s first GSM service introduced in the UAE in 1994, followed by rollout of internet services across the country in the next year, another first in the region.

Etisalat’s international acquisition programme began in earnest in 2004 when it won the second mobile license and the first third-generation (3G) mobile license in Saudi Arabia. From thereon, it continued to surge forward by acquiring a stake in a green-field operator in Nigeria in 2007 and investing in Excelcomindo, one of the leading mobile service providers in Indonesia, closely followed by operations in several other nations in the Middle East & Africa (MEA) region and beyond. While in the UAE, in 2010, the company propelled it as one of the first five countries in the world with a 3D television service.

Etisalat is a pioneer in next-generation networks for both fixed-line and wireless services and it has deployed enough fibre-optic cable in the UAE to reach from the earth to the moon eight times. It currently owns the Middle East’s fastest fixed-line broadband network with speeds of up to 30 megabits per second (Mbps). As an industry leader in innovation, Etisalat has trialled the world’s fastest mobile broadband service with speeds up to 300 Mbps.

This technological expertise has helped Etisalat to capture significant market share as it expands across the region, most notably in Egypt and Saudi Arabia, where the introduction of mobile broadband services, including video call and mobile television, has changed the market dynamics and provided affordable Internet access for millions. The conglomerate is also in the process of launching 4G mobile services in the UAE and Saudi Arabia, and today operates the Middle East’s largest LTE network.

Keeping its leadership intact in complementing fieldsis Etisalat Services, the holding company for the various services and support functions offered through individual business units. These include e-Facility management (e-FM), e-Real estate (e-Re), Etisalat Academy, Ektibar Card Systems and Tamdeed Projects, among others.

Etisalat has been named ‘Best Overall Operator’ in the Middle East ten times since 2006 and “Best Wholesale Provider” four times in the last three years as well as three prestigious wins at the 2012 Global Mobile Awards. It has won numerous accolades for its innovative marketing, being awarded for having the “Best Brand”, “Best Customer Service” and “Best CSR Programme”.

One of Etisalat’s core values is service to the community, a commitment to give back to improve the lives of people and to protect the environment for future generations. It supports, sponsors and participated in a wide array of social, economic, and environmental initiatives across the Middle East, Asia and Africa, working closely with governments, other companies, and foundations.

It also builds products and services for those with special needs in the firm belief that all members of society deserve the same freedom of communication. Caring for the shared resources, Etisalat has pioneered several advanced “green” technologies and is a regional leader in providing environmentally-friendly information and communication solutions. These include smart building technologies, the latest Machine-to-Machine (M2M) solutions and the deployment of alternative power within its regional networks. Its futuristic nationwide fibre-optic network in the UAE is expected to reduce carbon emissions by over 80 percent and energy consumption by over 70 percent.

Surging ahead in a world in which technology extends the reach, through its values of energy, openness and enablement, it is actively developing newer and more advanced networks that enable people to develop, to learn and to grow. The organisation is rated Aa3 by Moody’s, AAby Standard & Poor’s and A+ by Fitch Ratings, giving it some of the highest ratings for a telecommunications company anywhere in the world. Etisalat’s market value currently stands at approximately US$22 billion with annual revenues of over US$ 9 billion.

Showcasing its continued success, Etisalat reported net profits of US$3.6 billion for 2012, ranking it as one of the most profitable telecoms group in the world. This blue-chip organisation with operations in 18 countries across the Middle East, Africa and Asia benefits an estimated 2 million people from regular work, including 53,000 who are directly employed by it. Having witnessed rapid expansion, Etisalat has positioned itself as one of the world’s fastest growing operators, with worldwide subscribers surging around 3,475 percent from 4 million in 2004 to 141 million in 2013.

Success Stories
Bringing state-of-the-art telecom to Sudan

Etisalat was one of the key participants in the remarkable transformation and achievements witnessed in the Sudanese telecommunication sector. In the year 2006, it joined other founding partner companies to launch Canar Telecom, a fixed-line telecom services operator.

By offering innovative and high quality services, Canar has steadily increased its customer base in the fixed telecom market to enjoy 61.5% overall market share in fixed-line in Sudan. It grew its customer base by over 50% in 2007 with continued upward trends and its coverage saw an increase of 122% compared to 2006.

Furthermore, Canar’s fibre optic backbone extends to 3.070 km nationally and 850 locallyin the major cities to serve mobile operators, ISPs and corporate and business clients across the country. To further expand its services, Canar has launched Sudan Landing station in May 2007 to provide international connectivity linked with 122,000 km of submarine cables, making Sudan, East Africa’s Internet gateway to the entire world.

During 2009, fibre optic links between Sudan and Ethiopia were rolled out as part of overall plans to expand the Canar fibre network across all neighbouring countries. This resulted in maximising use of its submarine landing station, which is an integral part of the operator’s wholesale offer in international connectivity for the region.

Reflecting its confidence in the company, Etisalat raised its stake in Canar during 2007 from 37% to 82% by investing close to USD $159 million. Continuing the innovative approach of its partner, Canar is also one of the first operators in Africa to use an NGN network core.

Positive dynamism in Afghanistan

Etisalat believes in contributing to all aspects of development by adopting practical and effective ways of bringing progress to the world at large. An example of this is its launch of Etisalat Afghanistan in 2007 as its 100% owned GSM operator to support the revival of the nation. It has rapidly become the fastest growing telecommunications service provider of the country with an active base of 3 million subscribers.

The company aims to be at the forefront of its market based on quality, innovative services and competitive pricing, with unsurpassed coverage in every province in the country. Since 2007, company has invested US$ 300 million in Afghanistan. It has also signed a deal with Afghanistan Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (ATRA) for conducting of a Rural Telecom Development project in 17 rural districts in 9 provinces by building and operating 23 BTS sites.

In line with company’s long standing resolve of partnering in the development of human capital and skill levels of local Afghan employees, the company launched the Employee Succession Plan to train and develop Afghan nationals into future senior management positions. It is also the official mobile phone company of Afghanistan national cricket team.

Etisalat Afghanistan operates out of Kabul and as of March 2010, the company achieved 24 percent market share in 27 provinces of Afghanistan. The operator, driven by its group, is providing unmatched connectivity to its customers through roaming agreements with 120 operators in 78 countries.

Company Profile

Thuraya Telecommunications Company was formed in April 1997 by a group of eight major national telecommunications operators and investment houses as a Mobile Satellite Services (MSS) operator headquartered in Dubai. A total capital amounting to USD 500 million was raised, paving the way for an agreement to be signed with Boeing to manufacture Thuraya’s first satellite.

The company continued on building its foundations by raising USD 475 million through a consortium of financial institutions in 1999. In the year of 2000, Thuraya became the Middle East’s first mobile telecommunications satellite when it launched its first satellite from the Pacific Ocean. After the launch of the second satellite in 2003, its subscribers hit 100,000 users. In 2008, a third satellite was launched, expanding the network coverage across Asia and Australia.

Thuraya makes a clear example as a UAE-based company that succeeded to not only become an international telecom company but also grew to be one of the top five international satellite telecom operators. Its voice and data solutions are used in more than 140 countries across Europe, Africa, the Middle East, Asia and Australia. The success keys of Thuraya, in my opinion, are represented in three elements: the increasing immensity of its coverage area through a successive launch of satellites, the uniqueness and modernity of its solutions and products and the focus of its marketing strategy on specific targets.

Thuraya succeeded in developing competitive innovative telecom solutions and products that are mainly via satellite mainly to people working in remote locations where terrestrial networks are useless. This has also contributed to the international success of the company. An example of its recent products is ‘the Thuraya XT-DUAL’ – a dualmode satellite phone, offered in March 2012. This handheld featured both GSM and satellite capabilities. Earlier on August 2009, Thuraya offered a satellite phone described as the toughest phone in the world ‘the Thuraya XT handset’. The uniqueness of these high-tech products generated more demand for Thuraya’s products from local and international markets; a strong market response was received as a result.

In the satellite broadband market, Thuraya has also made major inroads with the introduction of Thuraya IP and IP+, plug and play satellite broadband devices. As a recent example of the company’s international success, in July 2013, AVIWEST, a company headquartered in Rennes, France that provides digital video equipment and solutions, received certification from Thuraya to use Thuraya IP and IP+ broadband terminals on its equipment for live streaming .

In line with providing competitive solutions for specific markets, Thuraya has launched satellite voice, data and fax terminals: SF2500™, Seagull 5000i and Seagull 5000. These devices were designed mainly for small and medium-sized fishing vessels, leisure and surveillance boats operating in harsh maritime environments. Its robust exterior can withstand extremely high temperatures and humid conditions. The Indonesian Fisherman Association was an early adopter of the SF2500™. Its advanced GPS navigation system allows sea-vessels to document and transmit critical location and time information, optimizing costs and fleet operations.

The company’s tendency to continue improving its services is also a success key that should always be considered to stay healthy in the telecom competitive market. In addition, knowing the localized market and its changing requirements are significant for a company that plans to go abroad. Thus, Thuraya established a ‘Point Of Presence’ (POP) and ‘Meet Me Point’ (MMP) in Singapore to enhance the performance of the IP in Asia. The POP allows IP consumers, especially media broadcasters streaming services from remote locations to newsrooms and data centers across Asia.

Thuraya has concluded roaming agreements with more than 350 mobile GSM operators around the world, making it the only satellite operator to offer roaming between satellite and GSM. In July 2012, the company launched its roaming service in the USA in partnership with the American mobile operator, T-Mobile. This service allows Thuraya customers to roam seamlessly on the nationwide T-Mobile network that reaches 96 percent of Americans.

As a dynamic mobile satellite services operator, Thuraya continues to innovate and develop specialised communications solutions to meet the needs of its growing customer base.

Company Profile

Al Yah Satellite Communications Company PrJSC (Yahsat) is a private joint stock company fully owned by Mubadala, an investment arm of the Government of Abu Dhabi that represents the initiative within Mubadala’s ever growing portfolio of information communications technology interests that drives forward Abu Dhabi’s economic diversification strategy. The first fixed satellite operator in the UAE, it was incorporated in 2007, designed to meet the region’s satellite needs, based on extensive interaction with customers and research into their communication needs.

The company aims to lead satellite communications in the region and for the UAE government as well as the commercial sector. It has already expanded to more than 140 countries in the Middle East, Africa, Europe and South West Asia. The company have already launched two satellites at a total investment of US$1.9bn.

Yahsat launched its first satellite, Y1A in April 2011, blasted into orbit from Kourou, French Guiana in partnership with a consortium composed of EADS Astrium and Thales Alenia Space that manufactured the Y1A multi-purpose satellite; and with Arianespace, the experienced international launch services provider.

Y1A, which has a projected product life span of more than 15 years, delivers a range of Yahsat’s commercial and government satellite service offerings. YahLive that provides Direct-to-Home (DTH) television capacity and services to more than 64 countries across the coverage area. YahLink supports telecommunication services and communication links for applications such as corporate data networks, Internet trunking and GSM backhauling. The first satellite provides communications to the UAE Armed forces, as well as commercial links to corporate customers and TV broadcasters. It provides the first secure Ka band communications capacity (YahSecure) within the coverage area, enabling state-of-the-art infrastructure for military communications. Beyond the UAE, YahSecure enables Yahsat’s service division, YahService, to provide support for governments in the areas of administration, defense and emergency management.

The second satellite Y1B was launched from the Baikonur Cosmodrome, Kazakhstan on April 24th, 2012. The Y1B weighed more than 6,000 kg and features a spacecraft power of 14kW and has a 15-year design lifetime. It was equipped with a commercial multi-beam Ka-band payload, which will be used to provide a variety of government and commercial applications, including “YahClick”, a new reliable and affordable satellite broadband service for the Middle East, Africa, and South West Asia The two satellites offer a wide portfolio of voice, data, video and internet connectivity solutions designed to accommodate the demand for emerging applications in the satellite industry including satellite broadband, HDTV and various other satellite services.

Control of the satellites are managed by a team of trained UAE-national satellite controllers based out of the Yahsat control station in Al Falah, Abu Dhabi. Yahsat is now cosnidering to launch as one way of growing organically.

Company Profile

Technology is innovating and re-inventing itself at a remarkable pace, as it shapes the future pathway for human progress. As one of the few fields where global collaboration and knowledge-sharing are paramount to growth, several key technology hubs have emerged across the world. Amidst these, Advanced Technology Investment Company (ATIC) is committed to ensuring that Abu Dhabi becomes a major hub in the global technology network by supporting and driving its innovation.

One of the key driving forces for ATIC is the Abu Dhabi Economic Vision 2030, which provides a long-term strategy for achieving the primary goals of a safe and secure society alongside a dynamic open economy. Geared towards making it a reality and supported by the UAE’s friendly economic ties, ATIC was formed in 2008 as a specialist investment company mandated to focus primarily on the global advanced technology sector. It is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Mubadala Development Company, a multi-industry global conglomerate established by the Government of Abu Dhabi as a catalyst for the emirate’s economic diversification.

ATIC has a clear and single purpose: to deliver superior financial returns to its shareholders by responsibly and sustainably investing in, and building, leading technology companies around the world. Towards this, it has till date delivered significant strategic initiatives in industry, education and research to build solid foundations for future prosperity.

One of its first successes in the field came just within the second year of its operations when ATIC and Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) launched GLOBALFOUNDRIES during March 2009 with Dresden’s Fab 1 as its anchor. Focused on serving customer needs at the leading edge, it is geared towards collaboration on specific R&D projects like cool silicon and linear collider.

Moving further by investing into yet another promising venture, ATIC invested in Calxeda based in Texas, USA during the year 2010. Calxeda is at the forefront of innovations looking to revolutionize energy efficiency in data centers through the development of ARM-based ultra-low power solutions for servers. Calxeda EnergyCore Server-on-a- Chip (SoC) products offer intelligent power optimisation and can deliver a reduction of up to 90% in overall power consumption.

Another one of ATIC’s investments in this field is Intermolecular, which pioneers new collaborative approaches that accelerate R&D, innovation and time-to-market for partners in the semiconductor and clean energy industries.

ATIC’s approach is fundamentally different to that of other technology-focused investment organisations. Recognising that real change takes time, and that value-creation is more about sustainable wealth generation than short-term profit creation, it is committed to a long-term investment horizon.

This patient capital approach ensures that its investments are strategic and transformational rather than tactical and opportunistic. As a result, it enables them to deliver superior returns to both its portfolio companies and its shareholder. At its core, the company’s strategy comes down to three core assets – its patient capital philosophy, its perspective and its people.

ATIC works closely with the full spectrum of educational institutions in Abu Dhabi and the UAE to raise awareness of and interest in science and technology among young people. One of its key initiatives is Al Nokhba, which is empowering elite young Emiratis with the global experience needed to lead in the advanced technology-driven industries. Towards this goal, 20 elite Emirati students were awarded Al Nokhba Scholarship for Advanced Technology in 2012. ATIC is also involved in TECH QUEST, a youth engagement initiative designed to recruit, inspire and develop the UAE’s best national talent as well as a placement partner for the Higher Diploma in Electronic Systems Engineering Technology (HDESET) at Abu Dhabi Polytechnic.

ATIC also benefits from the insights and experience of a diverse network of expert advisors from around the world. Its team, including the Board of Directors and outside advisors, shares a strong culture and a common vision which underpins the success of the company. At present, ATIC’s potential scope is global to be focused on leading technology companies and centres in Europe, the USA and Asia, underpinned by its shareholder’s AA+ rating.

Success Story
Global success from the Silicon Valley

ATIC’s patient capital philosophy and responsible investing have resulted in GLOBALFOUNDRIES becoming a key player in its own right. Through aggressive investments made in technology and capacity and strategic engagements with leaders like ARM, Qualcomm and STMicro, the company has grown from strength-to strength.

In September 2009, ATIC acquired Charted Semiconductor and integrated it with GLOBALFOUNDRIES, thus creating the first truly global foundry in the world. Recognising its commendable effort, it was chosen as the GLOBAL M&A deal of the year for excellence in deal-making by the Global Mergers and Acquisitions Network in New York 2012.

As of now, GLOBALFOUNDRIES has eight fabrication facilities, of which six are in Singapore, one in Dresden, Germany, and one in New York State, USA. The network encompasses approximately 184,000 square meters of clean room space and last year produced in excess of 4.5 million wafers. The company serves more than 150 leading industry customers including most of the top 20 IC companies and employs approximately 10,000 employees spanning three continents across 12 locations.

In March 2012, ATIC acquired full ownership of GLOBALFOUNDRIES and in the next month its fabrication plant in Malta, New York, which is currently the largest commercial capital expansion project in the USA, came online. Today, GLOBALFOUNDRIES is the world’s second largest independent semiconductor foundry and ATIC is all set to take this to success to the next level with up to US$ 10 billion in committed investment for the future.

Company Profile

SmartCity was established in October 2006 as a conglomerate promoted by Dubai Holding member, TECOM Investments, a global leading Business Park and Free Zone developer. SmartCity was founded in accordance to the diversification strategy of strengthening TECOM’s position on the international arena, and was given the mandate to develop and manage Knowledge Industry Townships worldwide. TECOM’s decision to expand its operations and diversify its services internationally was made in order to enhance its shareholder value, add other revenue sources and growth markets, and to leverage existing corporate expertise in the development and management of Business Parks.

SmartCity has taken Dubai Internet City (DIC) as its main headquarters, which has played a vital role in its journey of success. The company can utilise the strong infrastructure provided within TECOM entities, as well as the flexibility provided by Dubai legislation to support the business environment. Based on the successful models of Dubai Internet City and Dubai Media City, SmartCity develops and provides an Infrastructure and environment that supports the growth of knowledge-based companies. It provides many value-added services for companies including a onestop- shop of Business Support services, a range of Retail, Hospitality, Residential and Recreational facilities, cuttingedge ICT Infrastructure, excellent After-sales Support, and enhanced and easy Government Services.

The company has had a significant impact on the economies in which it was established through the creation of vibrant knowledge-economy communities, which are anchored by leading global, regional and local names in the industry to facilitate knowledge transfer and job creation. SmartCity also creates a global network to assist its business partners in tapping into various markets, opportunities and talent. SmartCity Kochi, along with SmartCity Malta, are the first two projects to be part of the global network of knowledge-based industry townships that SmartCity seeks to develop. This global network fosters productive linkages and taps into synergies between knowledge-industry centres worldwide.

SmartCity has also signed two agreements with two different Governments to invest over USD 800 million. It won the Best Investment in Europe’ award at the World Investment Conference in France, La Baule in 2009 for its investment and development of SmartCity Malta.

Committed to its values of integrity, collaboration, and excellence, SmartCity has set its sights on creating a global network of self-sustained business townships to foster the knowledge economy.

Success Story
Taking knowledge-based communities to Malta

The UAE’s trade policy and business environment have enabled the country to prosper with a spirit of Free Trade and Open Economy, contributing greatly to the achievements of SmartCity. Business partners and governments around the world recognise the UAE-based SmartCity as a trustworthy partner which can build the same economic opportunities in other regions as well.

As the biggest Foreign Direct Investment in Malta, SmartCity has significantly contributed towards the promotion of Malta as the destination for knowledge-based companies from across the globe to cater to the European and North African markets. To simplify business relations with Malta, the UAE has signed the Avoidance of Double Taxation Agreement with the Government of Malta, which has enhanced trade, investment and business opportunities between the two countries. This agreement has had a positive impact on TECOM Business Parks’ clients.

Company Profile

The journey of success of e-home automation initiative began in 1999 when Khalifa Al Jaziri, a young UAE entrepreneur and a co-founder of the company, received an email in his (Junk Mailbox) about the new concept known as smart homes. Khalifa did not treat that email as we all would do. He read it and believed on that concept and its applicability in the region. Thus, he decided (along with his brother Ahmad) to take action by establishing a company in 2003 that provides home automation systems – the first of its kind in the region. Since this was a relatively new concept here, Khalifa began attending a variety of conferences and exhibitions to disseminate the idea of his business and started automating houses of his family, relatives and friends to gain practical experience and proving the success of this model.

The increasing demand for these solutions required the search for products that are tailored for the region since most of them were UK and USA-customized. Thus, establishing an R&D unit in the UK to develop the company’s own software and a unit in China to develop its own hardware came as a strong indication of the company’s vision of becoming a leading e-home technologies provider in the Middle East. This has given the company the ability not only to provide customized solutions for the UAE but also for other markets like MENA, Levant and CIS.

In-house tailored systems for home automation allow a variety of interfaces, including Windows Media Center, voice control and smartphones the control of audio and video equipment, lighting, heating, ventilation and air conditioning, motorized curtains and irrigation systems around the property as well as home security. Simply, it became possible to communicate with the property through a mobile, computer or voice commands without the need to be present.

Within a few years of operation, e-Home Automation has significantly grown from two employees to more than 100 employees, from one office in Dubai to seven offices in UK, China, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait and Egypt. The company developed its own corporate governance without even knowing the existence of such a term at the beginning. It also expanded its product base to include solutions like e-Masjid, e-office and e-health center.

Indeed, the journey of this company’s success was not an easy ride. But thanks to those who listen and appreciate the entrepreneurial spirit – the top with no doubt is H.H. Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid represented by Mohammed bin Rashid Establishment for SME Development that was there to help in every step of the company’s growth.

Due to its performance, the company gained several awards including the best business in both IT and Electronics sectors in the Mohammed Bin Rashid Small and Medium Enterprise Awards for Young Business Leaders ceremony and the best SME in the UAE which is at the GCC level.

Success Story
Automating homes around the region

The number of home automation companies has exponentially grown in the last five years in the GCC. This is mainly due to the increasing advancements in smart phone technologies and people’s demand asking for “modern lifestyle” houses. However, through its own R&D unit, the company has been able to offer tailored solutions that are more suitable to the regional uses. Thus, e-home automation has succeeded to position itself as an innovative e-home technologies provider rather than a mere distributor of others’ solutions.

As a result, e-Home Automation has established an incredible track record of projects not only in the UAE but also around the GCC region. Entering the Saudi market in 2008 was a very important move that gave the company an access to around 28 million people. In addition, opening a branch in Yarmouk, Kuwait contributed to the growth of the company’s success and reputation.
