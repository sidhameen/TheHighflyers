Company Profile

The International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies are guided by the objectives and principles to provide help and assistance to the most vulnerable groups without discrimination as to nationality, race, colour, religious beliefs, political and intellectual affiliations. The UAE Red Crescent Authority (RCA), as the only entity in the country representing the societies, is based on the same underlying rules laid down in international conventions. It is the nation’s main relief and aid agency, which is independent, but takes a lead role in the government’s humanitarian activities.

The UAE RCA was founded in 1983, and became the 139th member of the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies in 1986. Outside the UAE, the agency responds to emergency situations, as well as undertaking health programmes and humanitarian projects. It also undertakes continuous medical care initiatives within the Emirates to help the underprivileged.

Some of the key aid programmes that it has been involved in include the mission to help those affected by the Haiti earthquake, medical surgeries in Somalia, delivery of relief supplies to Sichuan province in China in the aftermath of a devastating earthquake and supply of essential medical and food to Myanmar within three days of Cyclone Nargis.

It is also actively involved in reconstruction projects, the most prominent among these being the Skeikh Zayed City in Kabul. Sheikh Zayed City, a project that built 200 homes on a 60 acre area in Kabul, the capital of Afghanistan. The cost of phase I of the development was USD 4.5 million, while phase II, is currently in progress, involves building the infrastructure for the city at a cost of USD 2 million, and includes roads, sewerage, water and electricity supplies. Recently, the UAE RCA also started construction on the Mazraq-2 compound for those displaced by conflict in northern Yemen. These compounds can house over 7,000 displaced and consist of 1,000 tents, providing food, healthcare and education.

In the UAE, the authority as part of its regular initiatives provided USD 354.000 in medical assistance to needy people of limited income. It was disbursed to 148 beneficiaries in Abu Dhabi, Bani Yas and the Western Region to cover costs of surgical operations overseas, medicine, wheelchairs and prosthetic hearing aid and devices for hearing impaired people whose cases are not covered by medical insurance.

Amongst the key areas of focus that the UAE RCA is involved overseas are social infrastructure and services, water supply and sanitation, health and education. In the year 2009, the UAE Red Crescent Authority (RCA) gave a total of USD 122.9 million, making it the third most significant donor from the nation in financial terms. Around 76 percent of these funds were spent in Asia, most notably the Middle East which received 58 percent, while sub-Saharan Africa received nearly 18 percent of total funds. At least 55 countries received funding from the agency in support of a number of sectors, with the largest contributions going to social infrastructure and services and multi-sector programs.

Till date, the UAE RCA has supported projects in more than 100 countries, and occupies a significant place in the UAE non-profit sector. It continues to be one of the first aid agencies on ground in times of unforeseen crises. Through its experienced team, the agency also is actively involved in helping economies of developing countries and nations marred by conflict.

Success Story
Flying in aid to Haiti

The devastating Haiti earthquake on 12 January 2010 claimed over 200,000 lives and displaced over 1.5 million people. The UAE Red Crescent Authority (RCA) immediately responded to the humanitarian call for help initiated by the International Red Cross and Red Crescent. It started implementing various programmes to support those in need with the first phase of the programme providing supplies and aid worth over USD 136.000, making it a principle contributor to this cause.

The second phase, valued at over USD 245.000, included sending a cargo plane with about 45 tonnes of food and essential material to Haiti. Additionally, the UAE RCA flew in about 100 tonnes of foods and support products through the Dominican Republic. It also continues support on ground through medical delegations and healthcare camps. As an agency dedicated to serving humanitarian causes worldwide, its timely initiatives in Haiti not just bough relief to those affected by the calamity, but also helped in rebuilding the nation.

Company Profile

As a nation that believes in growth and prosperity for everyone, UAE and organizations from the nation have been major contributors to alleviating global poverty. One of the major names in this field is the Abu Dhabi Fund for Development (ADFD), which was founded in the nation’s inception year of 1971 with the vision to be a model institution in providing development assistance that alleviates global poverty. The fund helps developing countries achieve sustainable economic growth and reduce poverty by providing financial resources, forging partnerships in the public and private sectors, and adopting international best practices to ensure aid effectiveness.

ADFD is an autonomous institution owned by the Abu Dhabi Government and to date, it has provided extensive financial facilities for building and launching vital developmental projects in various sectors across the world. The fund’s activities since inception include interests in land investment, agriculture, livestock & fisheries, water & electricity, housing, transportation & storage, industry & mining, social & health services, tourism & hotels, telecommunications and studies & technical aids.

ADFD also contributes to commercial projects that are inclined towards development with the aim to provide jobs and stability in local communities of developing countries. It has carefully chosen a wide range of sectors that are essential for the growth and economic progress of beneficiary countries. Moreover, its developmental initiatives are focused on developing countries that suffer from economic challenges. The fund also embraces the process of sustainable development in a balanced manner around the world.

ADFD also manages grants and loans provided by the UAE Government by overseeing the administrative duties, quality of the project and completion cycles. In the last three years, ADFD and the UAE Government provided concessionary loans and grants to launch major development projects in countries most in need worth over AED 7 billion across 50 projects. These projects included agriculture and land fertilization projects, construction of water and electricity power plants, industrial and mining project, and the provision of transportation and housing as well as facilities for healthcare and social services.

The fund has also made investments at more than AED 900 million through several economic vehicles including holding companies, investment portfolios and private equity. These investments are part of the ADFD’s aim to expand the provision of concessionary loans.

ADFD and Abu Dhabi Government have previously implemented and are currently financing projects in the Middle East and North, Eastern and Central Africa, as well as South, Central and Northern Asia. To date, their efforts have spanned 321 projects across 58 countries with loans and grants totaling AED 28.2 billion, and equity participation valued at AED 902.1 million. To date ADFD its present capital stands at AED 8 billion.

The ADFD seeks to continuously improve its results by identifying and activating best practices by strengthening cooperation with various national, regional and international institutions. Based on this principle, ADFD plays a key role as active member in the Coordination Group which was established in 1975. In addition, it recently participated in the Busan High Level Forum on Aid Effectiveness (HLF 4), signed a MOU with the Central American Bank for Economic Integration (CABEI) to coordinate co-financing of development projects and entered into an agreement with Asian Development Bank (ADB) to co-finance projects in the developing countries.

Endorsing its effectiveness, ADFD was bestowed the 1st place at the Stevie Awards for International Business in the category of Non-Profit or Government Organizations, surpassing some of the most advanced countries in the world.

Abu Dhabi Fund for Development (ADFD) shows its commitment to national social and humanitarian needs by regularly supporting activities and programs implemented by humanitarian agencies and social institutions. ADFD is led by the visionary leadership of the UAE and is continuously steered towards making a significant difference to the world and its people, cutting across national and regional boundaries.

Success Story
Generating and transmitting progress

Taking the onus of spurring growth in the Arab region, ADFD participated in the construction of a reinforced concrete dam across the river Nile near Merowe, some 350 km north of the Sudanese capital Khartoum. The dam is about 1.1 km across the river with a total length of 9 km and 60 metres in height. The project includes all the necessary civil works; the power plant to accommodate 10 generators each with a capacity of 125 megawatt and the electromechanical works. The works also include the transmission lines and the connection to the national grid.

The project is aimed at utilising the huge hydropower potential of the country in generating electricity to meet the increasing demand for power in order to assist the economic growth of the country. The project generates about 1250 megawatts of electricity that will be utilised for domestic, industrial and agricultural purposes. ADFD stepped in with its strength and acumen to cover the financial gap in the electromechanical works, thereby creating a developmental success worth emulating.

Company Profile

Education is the most effective tool to break the cycle of poverty, a belief held by His Highness Sheikh Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice President and Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai.

It was with this belief and desire to give children, regardless of their nationality, creed or religion, the opportunity to become positive contributors to the society, that His Highness launched Dubai Cares in September 2007. It was also launched with aim of helping achieve, by 2015, the United Nations Millennium Development (UN MDG) Goals 2 and 3 of guaranteeing universal primary education and promoting gender equality, respectively. Dubai Cares is also helping forge a global partnership for development, as indicated by UN MDG 8.

Since inception, the organization started building the pillars of success that paved its way towards further expansion. It has partnered with international aid agencies, foundations and NGOs including American Near East Refugee Aid (ANERA), Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, buildOn, Care International, The End Fund, Global Alliance for Improved Nutrition (GAIN), Global Network, Idara-e-Taleemo- Aagahi (ITA), Médecins Sans Frontières, Microsoft, Oxfam, Partnership for Child Development (PCD), Plan International, Room to Read, Save the Children, Scholastic, Teri Press, UNICEF, UNRWA, Water Aid and World Food Program.

Through its strategic partnerships with credible organizations, Dubai Cares achieved its initial target of reaching 1 million children in its first year. Today, in its fifth year of operation, Dubai Cares is reaching 7 million children in 28 developing countries across the globe with its integrated primary education programs.

Dubai Cares also commits significant resources towards Monitoring, Evaluation and Learning (ME&L) activities that do not only ensure alignment with its vision and values, but also become a source of valuable information on the performance of programs.

An important achievement of Dubai Cares was its transition from supporting to designing programs. This transition became evident at the launch of the USD16 million WASH-in-Schools initiative in Mali in 2010 where Dubai Cares, in coordination with 5 implementing agencies (UNICEF, OXFAM, CARE, Save the Children and WATERAID), designed a framework for collaborative program implementation and Monitoring, Evaluation and Learning (ME&L). As part of this program, Dubai Cares is reaching 660,955 children in 726 schools.

Another innovative program is its Home Grown School Feeding program in Ghana. Launched in 2012, this 4-year program will improve the education, health and nutrition of over 320,000 primary school aged children and the livelihoods of over 80,000 rural households. Based on the principle “healthy children learn better”, the program will not only benefit school children, but marginal rural communities as well since the commodities required for producing the nutritious meals for school children will be procured from local farmers, prepared by local caterers and distributed by local service providers. This creates a self-sustaining cycle in which students receive the nutrition they need and household incomes increase.

These programs have promoted Dubai Cares as a flexible and innovative donor within the industry and paved the way for new partnerships.

Success Story
Reducing Hardships in Gaza Strip

With Gaza facing humanitarian and economic hardships that led to increasing poverty rates, Dubai Cares launched a school feeding program to address the rising malnutrition rates of 80,000 children. With a limited diet and often times an empty stomach, children are unable to absorb the material being taught at school. If their situation is not improved, children eventually drop out of school as they are unable to keep up with the syllables. Even if small in size, the daily food ration of one 60g fortified date bar provided by the Dubai Cares’ program with the World Food Program enhances children’s nutritional intake and energy levels. Children are now more motivated to come to school regularly as they are guaranteed a food intake and can perform better in their studies. Other than the children, different stakeholders such as local markets are also being impacted as the ingredients for the date bars are purchased from local suppliers. The program is bringing benefits on many fronts, from combating short- term hunger to improving the cognitive abilities of children and also supporting local economic activity.
