Company Profile

The ambitious forays of the UAE Government as well as investors from the nation have a common thread binding them, in that they are keenly focussed on identifying future trends while ensuring economically diverse investing. This is all the more true when it comes to the Abu Dhabibased Mubadala, one of the world’s leading multi-national investment and development conglomerates of the present times.

Mubadala - the Arabic word for ‘exchange’ - was established by the Government of Abu Dhabi in the year 2002 as a principal agent in the diversification of Abu Dhabi’s economy. Within a span of just over a decade, it has successfully marked its presence in some of the most challenging sectors including aerospace, metals, information and communications technology, oil and gas, renewable energy and semiconductors.

In line with the Abu Dhabi Economic Vision 2030, the company’s mandate is to generate sustainable, long-term returns for its shareholder, while also delivering strong economic benefits for Abu Dhabi and the United Arab Emirates. Its investment activities focus on reducing Abu Dhabi’s reliance on oil and oil-related products by diversifying the Emirate’s industrial base to develop a multisector economy.

Mubadala is building upon Abu Dhabi’s competitive advantages to grow businesses, in partnership with established international organisations in diverse priority sectors. Many of these investments have seen Mubadala expand its presence in emerging markets, in recognition of the long-term opportunities and growth potential that they offer.

Mubadala’s activities also extend to the establishment, construction and provision of social infrastructure in Abu Dhabi, particularly in the sectors of healthcare and real estate, in support of the Government’s development strategy and the wider growth of the private sector in the UAE.

The company’s investment philosophy is centred on the founding of world-class partnerships. Its current partnerships encompass a broad range of initiatives and areas of joint cooperation with some of the world’s leading businesses and are always based on a shared vision and collaboration.

Its stellar list of international partners includes Airbus, Boeing, The Carlyle Group, Cleveland Clinic, EBX, GE, Imperial College London, Pramerica and Total among others. The common thread that runs through all Mubadala investments and assets is the opportunities they enable for current and future generations, from employment in entirely new sectors to the educational and healthcare infrastructure to support the nation.

In terms of individual sectors, Mubadala has a stake in companies that have continued to deliver formidable successes. An example of its world-class approach is evidenced in the information and communications technology field, where its key assets comprise renowned names such as Al Yah Satellite Communications Company (Yahsat), Bayanat, Damballa, Emirates Integrated Communications (du), Etisalat Nigeria, Injazat Data Systems and Prodea Systems. It has also invested in conglomerates such as Advanced Micro Devices (AMD), EMI Music Publishing that hold a highly regarded position in their respective industries.

The common approach that runs through all Mubadala investments and assets is the opportunity provided to current and future generations. It consistently provides career opportunities to young UAE Nationals through its Emiratisation strategy with an ever-increasing proportion of Emiratis working for the company in both managerial and engineering roles.

Keeping an eye on a sustainable future for the planet, Mubadala is also advancing the development, commercialisation and deployment of renewable energy solutions and clean technologies, establishing Abu Dhabi as a global centre of excellence in the renewable energy and clean technology sector. It also holds a stake in London Array Limited, the world’s largest offshore wind farm, which will be generating enough electricity to power nearly half a million homes.

Mubadala’s success is reflective of the prudence of UAE Government’s its policies and initiatives, which have included a number of steps to accelerate the process of economic diversification and growth in recent decades. Complementing its financial acumen, Mubadala has one of the highest credit ratings in the Middle East and is recognised for its ongoing commitment to transparency as well as adherence to robust corporate governance and compliance practices.

Having established itself with a single project in the regional energy sector, Mubadala is now an active investor in more than 12 industries around the world with over 33,000 employees. As of 2012, its portfolio stood at more than USD 50 billion, increasing above 15% compared to the previous year. Further on, its total revenue for the year 2012 was over

USD 8.5 billion, showcasing an increase of more than 12% over 2011. Most significantly, till date, Mubadala has created over 10,000 jobs locally and made significant contributions to the GDP and the development of Abu Dhabi, which continues to be its key mandate.

Success Stories
Creating a booming healthcare sector with international partners

Mubadala is contributing to a thriving healthcare sector by providing world-class facilities for the residents of the UAE and the wider region. It is focused on addressing the region’s most pressing healthcare needs through the creation of specialist facilities. This is a task that it is achieving in collaboration with internationally-recognised medical organisations such as John Hopkins Medicine International, Imperial College London, and Cleveland Clinic. The goal for the company at every step in this sector is to bring the highest standards of clinical expertise to the region and reduce the need for patients to travel overseas for treatment.

One of its most important projects in this area is the Imperial College London Diabetes Centre (ILCDC), a one-stop, state-ofthe- art, out-patient facility that specialises in diabetes treatment, research, training and public health awareness. It currently has two centres – one in Abu Dhabi and another in Al Ain. The purpose-built centres, operated in partnership with the UK’s Imperial College London, provide the highest level of specialised patient care, from first diagnosis to the management of all complications associated with diabetes, and has offered diabetes prevention, education and treatment to more than 160,000 people.

ILCDC holds the Joint Commission International (JCI) Clinical Care Program Certification (CCPC) in Diabetes Management and JCI Accreditation for Ambulatory Care. Showcasing its expertise is the fact that it is the first facility in the Middle East and the third globally to hold JCI’s highly-acclaimed CCPC for the clinical management of diabetes. As a wholly-owned subsidiary of Mubadala, ILCDC is enabling easy access to global standards in healthcare for the region and beyond.

Rising high as a global aerospace partner

The UAE is fast becoming an aerospace hub that offers a range of world-class services for the global aerospace industry, with capabilities including turnkey maintenance, repair and overhaul, aircraft and component financing, composite aerostructures manufacturing and flight training.

In line with its goal to establish the aerospace sector in Abu Dhabi, Mubadala is creating a network of companies that leverage best practice manufacturing processes, an integrated supply chain, and a high caliber workforce. One of these companies is Strata, an advanced composite aerostructures manufacturer based in Al Ain that designs, develops and produces key aircraft components – including wings and empennages – for industry leaders such as Boeing and Airbus.

Furthermore, it is also focusing on the global aerospace sector to accelerate innovation and growth in this crucial field. Mubadala’s global network of aerospace businesses delivers world-class performance, creates innovative products and provides valueadded services to its customers. The list of its key aerospace assets includes prominent names such as Abu Dhabi Aircraft Technologies (ADAT), Italy-based Piaggio Aero SpA, Sanad, Switzerland-headquartered SR Technics and Strata, among others.

The unifying factor amongst all Mubadala aerospace companies is the commitment to developing local human capital, which is vital to the future of Abu Dhabi’s aerospace industry and overall diversification plans. In total, Mubadala’s wholly-owned companies in the aerospace sector employ over 4,000 people, with an additional 2,500 jobs expected to be created in the next five years, of which around 60% are designated for the next generation of UAE Nationals.

Company Profile

Al Ahli Holding Group (AAHG) is an Emirati corporation with a colorful portfolio of over 30 companies covering various sectors including Construction and infrastructure, Real Estate, Basic Materials, Technology, Transportation & Logistics, Trading and Marketing, Lifestyle and Fitness, in addition to Entertainment and hospitality.

This multi-disciplined portfolio is the fruit of a 40 years evolution that begun in 1968 when Nasser Khammas, a young entrepreneur from the Emirate of Fujairah, started his first business in the retail industry by establishing Al Ahli chain of supermarkets.

From the early beginnings, Nasser Khammas always looked at, and was open to, innovative ways to expand and diversify the activities and sectors of his businesses, and since then, AAHG’s expansion was evident and limitless, penetrating multiple business fields and entering new markets.

Today AAHG employs around 8,000 people, and although it is based in the United Arab Emirates with offices in Dubai, Abu Dhabi and Fujairah, it has representing offices operating in Germany, Spain (Madrid), Panama, USA (Los Angeles & New York), Mexico, Nicaragua, Colombia, Costa Rica, Venezuela, Peru, Brazil, Argentina, Angola, Nigeria, South Africa, Egypt, Hong Kong and India, In addition to Oman and Qatar.

AAHG’s involvement in the construction sector commenced in the early 1990s by establishing Emirates General Contracting. It has successfully delivered a variety of projects including industrial, residential and commercial buildings within the UAE.

As the construction industry thrived in the UAE and the gulf region, AAHG’s construction and infrastructure industries rapidly grew and the need to establish new companies that specialize in the different segments of the construction sector became indispensable. As a result, the Gulf Spring Contracting and Al Bawadi Contracting were born.

The demand for building materials also grew, and AAHG realized a huge gap in the market and quickly seized the opportunity by establishing the Emirates Engineering Services and Factories which specialize in design, fabrication, and erection of pre-engineered steel buildings as well as hot rolled steel structures, and Al Ahli Aluminum, one of the leading aluminum, glass and cladding contractors in the UAE today.

Committed to his strategy of diversifying the group’s activities, Mr. Khammas continued to establish strong presence in a wide range of new business fields, including the establishment of the state of the art Al Ahli Printing Press in 1996, equipped with latest High-Tech printing machines able to cover the growing printing demands in the UAE and Middle East back then.

Entering the new millennium, the economical and technological growth in the UAE reached unprecedented levels, and new industries and technologies were highly in demand. That’s when Al Ahli IT Solutions joined the extensive portfolio of the group consulting and outsourcing solutions to an impressive client base. Al Ahli Plastics industries soon followed manufacturing almost unlimited variety of quality plastic products that were highly in demand, and within less than two years, the company had to expand so fast it reached a production capacity of 20.000 Tons per annum.

During the following years, AAHG did not only maintain its momentum, but it enlarged its enrollment in different business fields faster than ever establishing a wide new range of companies such as Al Ahli General Transport, Arabtec Consultants, Al Dhiyafa Residence, Al Ahli General Trading, Al Ahli Advertising and Distribution and Artline Advertising to name a few.

Soon after the announcement of DUBAILAND® project, AAHG launched the Dubai Outlet City, a US$1 billion mixed use development covering approx 10 million sq ft featuring retail, offices, hotels, furnished apartments, a conference centre and other facilities. The well-known Dubai Outlet Mall is the centerpiece of this first of a kind concept in the Middle East hosting over 800 premium and top fashion brands in individual and multi-branded stores.

After reaching this huge local success, It was only a matter of time before AAHG started looking for a global presence through long-standing relationships and partnerships with several international companies, which opened new doors for new markets, business and industries altogether.

Al Ahli Holding Group truly believes in the private sector’s responsibility to contribute back to society, and that’s why Al Ahli Holding Group was one of the first private sector companies in the UAE to create a fully dedicated Corporate Social Responsibility division.

The CSR division of Al Ahli Holding Group focuses on entrepreneurship and on helping, training and empowering the youth to start their own sustainable businesses. Today, CSR Al Ahli Holding Group has succeeded, with the generous help of the governments and partners, in implementing programs in Argentina, Jordan, Brazil, UK and Lebanon changing the lives of 200 youth from (UAE, Jordan, Argentina, Brazil, South Africa, Colombia, Greece, Lebanon, India and UK).

Other local and international initiatives by CSR Al Ahli Holding Group included The Global Youth Forum that was held in both UAE and Colombia gathering over 500 international youth and more than 50 top international speakers, and the CSR in Action program in UAE, Lebanon and Jordan. An initiative set to develop and enhance the practice and awareness of CSR activities among practitioners and students, and was the catalyst behind more than 20 new CSR initiatives currently serving the communities in UAE, Lebanon and Jordan.

Company Profile

The Abu Dhabi Investment Council is an investment arm of the Government of Abu Dhabi. Its headquarters are situated in Abu Dhabi, an ideal location for its worldwide operations and place it in close proximity to its local investments and senior government officials. The positive UAE business environment and infrastructure, and the double taxation treaties entered into by the government, have aided the Council in attracting successful professionals from over 30 nationalities to the organisation, as well as supported the Council’s operations globally.

In April 2007, the Council started its operations and was given the responsibility of investing part of the government’s surplus financial resources through a rich portfolio of highly diversified asset classes and active investment management strategies, targeting positive capital returns. Investment mandates implemented included global and regional Public and Private Equities, global Mainstream Hedge Fund strategies and global real estate and infrastructure. The Council has managed to grow through sound and prudent investment strategies in spite of challenging market conditions. Since its inception, The Council has entered into a number of well-planned international investments that enriched its portfolio, leading the organisation towards its ambitious goals, including the purchase of a strategic stake in the renowned Chrysler Building in New York.

Although the Council invests globally, there is a strong focus on investing in the local economy in line with Abu Dhabi’s 2030 vision of building a diversified economy. Some of its significant investments include substantial stakes in prominent organizations such as the National Bank of Abu Dhabi, Abu Dhabi Commercial Bank, Union National Bank, Al Hilal Bank, Abu Dhabi National Insurance Company, Abu Dhabi Aviation Company and Abu Dhabi Investment Company (Invest AD).

By creating an environment which is both disciplined and innovative, The Council aims to become one of the world’s foremost effective and efficient investment institutions, capable of managing assets and generating high and consistent returns over the long-term, while sustaining prosperity for the future.

Company Profile

National Holding (NH) originated in 1999 when Emirates International Investments Company (EIIC), the parent company of the group, was established in the emirate of Abu Dhabi. Within the last five years, NH has strengthened its position as a Highflyer after successive investments in the international arena. These investments were made by the five diversified companies that are operating under the umbrella of the holding company: EIIC, Bloom Properties, Exceed Industries, Rise General Trading and Petromal.

EIIC is the investment arm and the parent company of the group. As a result of its continuing hunt for strategic investment opportunities, the company currently holds active investments across a wide range of asset classes. In 2007, it acquired significant shares in Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank (ADIB). In conjunction with the bank, it went abroad by acquiring a majority stake of National Bank for Development in Egypt, an acquisition that has given the group a stronger presence in the MENA region. In addition, EIIC holds longterm strategic investments in regional and global companies including the French multinational mass media and telecommunication company Vivendi.

NH moved to property sector in 2001. It rebranded its real estate department by launching Bloom Properties in 2008 with a focus of creating thriving master-planned communities in major cities across the region and beyond. Locally, Bloom manages 30 residential and commercial towers in addition to several retail centre and residential compounds. On the international arena, it holds properties in Algeria.

To further enhance its standing and competitive position in local, regional and global markets by identifying and responding to strategic investment opportunities, NH stepped into the industrial sector in 2002. A diversified manufacturing industrial company Exeed Industries was established as the industrial arm of the group. It consists of fifteen entities spanning building materials and fibers, agriculture business and international investments. Exeed has a strong presence in Algeria, owing a copper and aluminum-cables factory Cablet El Djazair. In addition, Exeed owns National Integrated Industries Complex in Jordan, manufacturing a range of electronic home appliances with a target on South Africa, Eastern Europe-CIS, and the Iran markets.

RISE General Trading, the trading arm of the group, is divided into engineering services, project management, consulting, and national travel and tourism services. RISE is operating locally and plans to expand its reach to the MENA region.

National Holding has recently launched an oil and gas company Petromal which is now divided into four distinct business units: Upstream (oil and gas producing assets), Downstream (refining and petrochemicals), Petroleum Services (oil field services and engineering & fabrication) and Minerals. Since inception, the company formed two joint ventures with the UK’s Premier Oil. It has also partnered with Egyptian Mud Engineering Chemicals (EMEC) to form EMEC E (Emirates), a partnership that marks the company’s entry into the oil field services market. . The Joint Venture covers the United Arab Emirates, Algeria, Iraq, and Yemen.

Through establishing diversified companies, the group has successfully left its footmarks on multiple sectors and across a wider area, efficiently making the group as one of the UAE-based international highflyer.

Success Story
Investing in Saudi Arabia’s energy sector

Macroeconomic fundamentals of Saudi Arabia are positioning the country among the investment destinations in the world. These characteristics are consistent with the investment strategy of MENA Infrastructure that did not rethink of acquiring a 15% equity stake in the 4,000MW gas-fired Qurayyah Independent Power Project (QIPP). QIPP is currently being developed in Saudi Arabia in partnership with ACWA Power and Samsung C&T.

In June 2011, MENA Infrastructure Fund was part of the winning ACWA Power led consortium that was announced preferred bidder for the development of the project. The project will deliver almost 4,000 MW of electricity to Saudi Electricity Company (SEC) under a 20 year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) commencing on 30 June 2014. Samsung C&T is the EPC contractor. The twelve gas turbines are provided by Siemens. When complete, Qurayyah IPP will be the largest combined cycle gas-fired power plant in the world.

MENA Infrastructure also invested in a heavy fuel oil power plant “Rabigh IPP 2” 175km north of Jeddah, Saudi Arabia. The plant is the fourth project in SEC’s IPP program and is projected to have the capacity to produce 1,813MW.

Company Profile

The Arab world has a significant number of family businesses that have evolved into the modern times as multinational groups making a significant global imprint. In the UAE, the highly advanced trade policies as well as friendly ties with national around the world have enabled family-owned businesses to thrive beyond the borders. These factors have played a crucial role on the operation and growth of Thani Investments, a large privately-owned business house from the UAE.

The group, from its headquarters in Dubai, has expanded into more than ten countries within just over a decade through four diversified business units. These are oil & gas, property development, mining & mineral resources and commercial trading & investment.

Thani’s emphasis on building long-term growth and profits through developing relationships in the Middle East have paid rich dividends. Initially, it was primarily focused on commercial trading and property development sectors. However, in response to the changing dynamics of the global economy and to re-balance risk across its portfolio, the group subsequently ventured into the oil & gas and mineral resources sectors through wholly-owned subsidiaries.

Thani Emirates Petroleum, the first independent Arab oil & gas company to successfully venture overseas, is engaged in the exploration and production of crude oil and natural gas. It has built alliances with global technical partners in pursuing enhanced oil & gas development projects. These partners include CNPC, Petronas, Sinopec, OMV, PetroVietnam, Anadako Petroleum, Tullow Petroleum, Itochu Corporation and Sojitz Corporation. Its interests span Sudan, Mauritania, Tunisia, Algeria and Senegal. The company is also exploring in the emerging markets of Yemen, Libya and Indonesia.

Thani Emirates Resource Holdings Limited (TERH), the first independent Arab junior resource company, is mandated to develop, through discovery and acquisition, a geographically-diversified, predominantly gold portfolio. It currently owns significant mining concessions in Egypt, Eritrea, Yemen and Pakistan, and has formed an equal stake, strategic alliance with AngloGold Ashanti Limited (AngloGold) to explore, develop and operate gold, precious and base metals mines across the MENA region.

BENAA, the group’s property development and real estate business, holds interests in both completed developments and ongoing development sites in the commercial, retail, hospitality, leisure and residential sectors. It has proposed development projects in respect of 85 million sq. ft. of developable land. In particular, the company is the lead partner in a joint venture with the Al Oula Group, one of the largest property developers in the KSA, to deliver Dubai Golf City in Dubailand. The project comprises 55 million sq. ft. of developable land with a potential completed value in excess of AED 20 billion. Its other investments include Al Bustan Centre and Residence, Arabian Heights and the Dubai Wholesale Plaza.

Thani Commercial Ventures, its commercial trading business has diverse commercial and agency interests ranging from shipping, electrical & power and equities trading. Its shipping activities include acting as commercial agent in Qatar, Oman and Kuwait for over US$ 2 billion of VLCC and LNG vessel sales by Mitsui & Co. and Hyundai Heavy Industries. It has also supplied approximately 4,000 km of medium voltage cabling, partnered with BSES Ltd. for a major GIS substation project and supported ABB in its marketing initiatives.

Thani’s vision is one of building long-term differentiated businesses which capitalise on their skills and enhance their leadership position. This unique value-based proposition was evidenced in 2007, when Thani Investments became one of the first privately-owned companies in the GCC to successfully issue a US$100 million Musharaka Sukuk. Its pioneering role brought Thani the ‘Best Sukuk Structure by a Private Corporate Issuer’ recognition at the London Sukuk Summit in 2008. Founded on the values of integrity, passion and energy, Thani currently has over 1,000 employees working to build a long-term growth story for Dubai and the nation.
