Company Profile

In a region that grows at exceptional pace backed by massive infrastructure investments, Emaar Properties stands tall as the developer of world-class masterplanned communities. A Dubai-based Public Joint Stock Company, which is listed on the Dubai Financial Market (DFM), Emaar is not just a regional success story, but a globally-renowned property developer and provider of premier lifestyles.

Emaar, the developer of Burj Khalifa, the world’s tallest building, and other iconic real estate assets, has been shaping landscapes and lives in the UAE since the company’s inception in 1997. Extending its expertise in developing master-planned communities internationally, today, it has a significant presence in several key global markets. Simultaneously, the company has developed competencies in hospitality, leisure, shopping malls and retail.

Emaar has numerous real estate projects in its primary market of Dubai including established communities and developments in various stages of completion. Key among these is the integrated lifestyle destination Downtown Dubai, which is the world’s most visited lifestyle destination and is planned to include The Opera District. The established communities of Emaar include Dubai Marina, Arabian Ranches and Emirates Living, which comprises Emirates Hills, The Meadows, The Springs, The Greens, The Lakes and The Views, preferred by its cosmopolitan residents for their elite lifestyles.

Emaar is also partnering in implementing Mohammed Bin Rashid City, a new city within Dubai, and with Dubai Holding has unveiled Dubai Hills, the first project in the master-development. Leveraging the robust growth of Dubai’s economy and the strong investor confidence in the company’s commitment to delivery and world-class project development, Emaar launched The Address Residence Sky View, The Address The BLVD, and The Address Residence Fountain Views. Some of the other new project launches by Emaar include Panorama at The Views and CASA an architecturally-stunning Moroccan style villa community as part of Emaar’s extension of Arabian Ranches.

The developer is renowned globally for landmarks that include Burj Khalifa, The Dubai Mall and The Dubai Fountain – all set in Downtown Dubai, described as ‘The Centre of Now,’-and also featuring The Address Downtown Dubai, The Palace Downtown Dubai, The Address Dubai Mall and Souk Al Bahar. Downtown Dubai is also home to one of the world’s finest boulevards, the spectacular Mohammed Bin Rashid Boulevard.

Globally, Emaar has a collective presence in several markets spanning the Middle East, North Africa, Pan-Asia, Europe and North America. It has highlighted its commitment to project delivery by handing over homes in its masterplanned communities in Turkey, Lebanon, Egypt, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, India, Pakistan and Canada, and commercial offices in Syria.

Emaar’s international projects in Egypt include: Marassi, Mivida and Cairo Gate as well as a ‘city within a city’ development, Emaar Square, at its flagship Uptown Cairo in 2012. Making a mark in the MENA region as a premium developer, it has established a presence in several other countries. Emaar’s other developments around the region include Amelkis, Saphira and Tinja in Morocco; Samarah Dead Sea Resort in Jordan; The Eighth Gate project in Damascus, Syria; Emaar Square in Turkey and Beit Misk in Lebanon. In Saudi Arabia, Emaar is developing King Abdullah Economic City, a mixed-use project located on the Red Sea coast, which is the largest private sector-led development in the region and has the potential to create 1 million jobs.

Emaar Middle East, a subsidiary that is focused on projects in the Middle East, has unveiled several projects in key growth markets. These include Jeddah Gate, a masterplanned mixed-use project in Jeddah’s new downtown; Al Khobar Lakes, a luxury lakefront development; and Emaar Residences at Makkah Clock Tower.

As part of its strategic partnerships, Emaar has joined hands with The Turner Corporation, the USA’s leading building services provider, to form Turner International Middle East Ltd to jointly tap regional growth opportunities. In 2011, Emaar launched a wholly-owned subsidiary, Dawahi Development, envisaged as the next-generation pan-Arab developer of ‘value housing’ projects within full-service community developments.

Beyond the region, in India, the company also has Boulder Hills, a world-class leisure and residential community in Hyderabad; the Commonwealth Games Village in Delhi; Mohali Hills in Punjab and The Palm Springs and The Palm Drive in Gurgaon, to its name in India. Emaar has also pioneered the development of master-planned communities in Pakistan with Canyon Views in Islamabad, and Crescent Bay in Karachi, Pakistan, and has handed over the first homes in Canyon Views.

Emaar has also made extensive forays in the global hospitality and leisure sector with The Address Hotels + Resorts, the group’s 5-star premium hotel brand, signing management agreements to operate The Address Masai Mara in Kenya and The Address Marassi Golf Resort and Spa in Marassi, Egypt, as well as in Emaar Square, Turkey.

Emaar Properties recorded full-year 2012 net profit of US$ 577 million and a total revenue of US$ 2.243 billion, highlighting the company’s strong financial fundamentals. Reinforcing the success of the company’s focus on strengthening its recurring revenue streams, Emaar’s shopping malls, retail, hospitality and leisure businesses contributed US$ 1.115 billion to the full-year revenue in 2012. While the contribution of Emaar’s international operations to the total revenue was US$ 344 million, representing 15 percent of the total revenue.

Marking the clear differentials that Emaar brings to property and lifestyle development, the company holds five Guinness World Records: The Tallest Building and Tallest Manmade Structure Ever for Burj Khalifa; Largest Shopping Centre for The Dubai Mall; the Highest Restaurant from Ground Level for At.mosphere; and the Single Largest Acrylic Viewing Panel for Dubai Aquarium & Underwater Zoo. True to its stature, the company has won several local and international accolades, commending its inimitable achievements across the property and lifestyle development spectrum.

Company Profile

In the midst of beginning a new dawn for a young country, Arabtec was founded to contribute to the architectural renascence in the early 1970s. From building three cold stores in Dubai, Abu Dhabi and Muscat and student accommodations in Al Ain to the construction of the world’s tallest building, the Burj Khalifa, and the iconic seven-star hotel, Burj Al Arab, Arabtec’s success has indeed gone beyond imagination.

Today, Arabtec specializes in construction projects that include high-rise developments, hotel interiors, residential complexes, office blocks, commercial and industrial projects, major airport developments, stadiums, drainage, sewage Treatment Plants, offshore oil and gas installations, cinemas, entertainment facilities, communities, and luxury villas. The company has grown across different sectors and geographic markets.

The 1980s period did not only witness the accomplishment of a number of residential and commercial projects in the local market but it also saw the company’s expansion into a new segment and commencement of forming a client base. In 1984, Arabtec delivered its first turn-key project that involved both construction and design “The Abu Ghazaleh Building” in Sharjah. It has also won numerous contracts to construct automobile distribution centers, commercial and residential building for Al Futtaim Group. In addition, the long involvement with the oil and gas sector commenced in 1980s when Arabtec began executing a residential and office development on Mubarraz Island, laboratories and control centers, jetty projects and the West Mubarraz oil and gas processing plant for Abu Dhabi Oil Co. (Japan) Ltd. (ADOC).

The company’s meticulous selection of partners is one the important factors that secured its well-standing position through the depression that marked the construction market in the late 1980’s when it engaged a Swiss company, Cablex, to assist it in a number of 132KV oil-filled cable installations for Dubai Electricity and Water Authority with a collective value in excess of USD 16.3 million. These projects carried Arabtec safely through the tough period.

By the 1990, Arabtec’s name became synonymous with class, excellence and superiority. The company began exploring different horizons, making its marks on the aviation world - the Emirates New Technical Centre and the expansion of Dubai Airport - that played a key role in the company’s rise to the top of the construction field in the UAE.

Arabtec continued to play a major role in redefining the skyline of Dubai. In conjunction with Samsung and Six Construct, it was involved in the construction of the world’s tallest building, the Burj Khalifa, which stands 828 metres tall. Nearby, the Burj Lake Hotel, now called the Address Hotel, has recently been completed, in a joint venture with Six Construct. This earned recognition in the world as The New Hotel of 2008. Recently, Tourism Development and Investment Company (TDIC), a master developer of major tourism, cultural and residential destinations in Abu Dhabi, has awarded the construction of the Louvre Abu Dhabi, set to open in the Saadiyat Cultural District in 2015, to Arabtecled joint venture with Constructora San Jose SA and Oger.

Arabtec’s commitment towards becoming a leading provider of quality construction and engineering services locally, regionally and globally has driven it to successfully expand its geographic footprint. Al Waab City in Qatar, Lamar Towers in Saudi Arabia, and Karachi Financial Towers in Pakistan are a few examples of Arabtec’s completed projects in the neighboring countries. In 2011, Arabtec and Combined Group Company PLC Joint Venture were awarded to build University Colleges in Kuwait, a contract valued at USD 408 million.

Globally, Arabtec has gone through a stiff competition to win the bid of Okhta Centre project, with a contract value of USD 2.7 billion, over European and Russian contractors. Due to its extensive experience in building quality projects and its successful record of implementation of similar projects, Gazprom Neft, the oil arm of Russia’s national gas economy and St. Petersburg’s mayor office were convinced to award Arabtec this iconic contract. The project comprises a 400-metre high tower having the shape of a flame that resembles the logo of Gazprom; three podium buildings as well as massive underground structures and special external works.

With a portfolio that contains varied high standard eyecatching projects like Burj Al Arab, Sky Gardens Tower, Al Fattan Marine Towers, Tiara United Towers, The Infinity Tower, Ocean Heights, Lamar Towers, Nation Towers, Silverene Twin Towers and the prestigious Burj Khalifa, a workforce of over 70,000 employees worldwide and state-of-the-art plant and equipment, a well-established and firm senior management team that has been dynamically dedicated to the company through the years, Arabtec can unquestionably win any competition in the realm of construction.

Company Profile

Embodying the culture, values and vision of Abu Dhabi, Al Maabar is a leading real estate developer of luxury mixed-used properties in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region. Headquartered in Abu Dhabi, Al Maabar is a partnership between the emirate’s six leading real estate and investment powerhouses: Mubadala Development Company PJSC, Aldar Properties PJSC, Sorouh Real Estate PJSC, Al Qudra Holdings PSC, Reem Investments PSC and Reem International LLC. Within just five years since its inception in 2007, Al Maabar has become synonymous with prestigious landmark mixed-use destination developments that are unique in design and destination. Currently, its international real estate portfolio includes strategic landmark developments across the MENA region – two in Jordan – Marsa Zayed in Aqaba and The St. Regis ® Amman and The Residences at The St. Regis Amman , Bab Al Bahr, in Rabat, Morocco and Al Waha in Tripoli, Libya.

For Al Maabar, international partnerships that open the doors for long-term prosperity and positive change for its partner countries and communities, lies at the core of its business model. To achieve this, Al Maabar’s investment strategy involves considerate and careful evaluation of potential markets and their long-term economic, social and cultural sustainability. By exploring new destinations beyond the boundaries of Abu Dhabi, Al Maabar seeks to enhance existing landscapes and deliver real value to the rich cultures and communities of its partner countries. In line with the vision of Abu Dhabi 2030, Al Maabar exports the vision of its leadership and supports the Emirate’s overall economic diversification strategy.

Meanwhile, Al Maabar’s forward thinking commitment to social, environmental and economic responsibility has not only made the company a welcomed partner in partner countries, but has also resulted in driving the company’s leadership position in MENA’s mixed-used real estate development market. Meanwhile, its shareholders’ support ensures long-term investment sustainability and access to the extensive combined experiences of its shareholders, positions Al Maabar as an attractive partner that is wellpositioned for future growth.

Marsa Zayed, named in memory of the late Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, is Al Maabar’s flagship project in Jordan and the largest mega mixed-use real estate development project to take place in the country and the MENA region. Situated in the historic gateway to the Levant, Marsa Zayed encompasses 3.2 million square meters of land area in the heart of Aqaba, including 2 kilometres of newly developed, prime waterfront property between the coast of the Red Sea and majestic mountains of Aqaba.

Developed by Al Maabar’s wholly owned subsidiary Aqaba Real Estate Development Company, Marsa Zayed will feature high-rise residential and office towers, hotels, retail spaces and commercial facilities including shopping centres, cafés and fitness centres. Once completed, Marsa Zayed’s 30,000 residential units, eight hotels with 3,000 rooms and marina facilities with 300 berths for yachts and boats, will make this Aqaba downtown property a leading commerce, tourism and residential attraction in Jordan.

The first project phase includes the award-winning residential properties of Al Raha Village and the Sheikh Zayed Masjid. Al Raha Village, received the “Best Mixeduse Architecture” Award, while the Sheikh Zayed Masjid, which takes its architectural inspiration from Abu Dhabi’s Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, was presented with the “Best Public Service Architecture” Award at the world-renowned International Property Awards Arabia 2012. Sales at Al Raha Village, the first of Marsa Zayed’s properties are expected to begin in early 2013 and the property is on track for completion in 2016.

Touting a signature address in the capital’s enviable Abdoun District, The St. Regis ® Amman and The Residences at The St. Regis Amman overlooks Jordan’s capital. Being developed by Al Maabar Abdoun Real Estate Development Company, a wholly owned subsidiary of Al Maabar, the St. Regis properties carry the world-renowned Starwood brand, offering a premier lifestyle that will set the standards for luxury in Jordan. The first St. Regis properties to be launched in Jordan will showcase two 16-story towers connected by an external façade. The hotel tower will feature a 260 guest rooms, including 86 suites and 40 serviced apartments, while the second tower will feature Jordan’s first private branded residences. Exclusive boutiques featuring prominent international brands, along with four restaurants, a signature spa, fitness centre, a café, pool, business centre and ultra-modern meeting rooms are among the planned amenities.

In Morocco, the new trendy downtown comes to life with the Bab Al Bahr, an integrated city featuring luxury commercial, residential, retail, leisure arts and cultural developments. Bab Al Bahr, which will house 1,700 residential units, sits between the two ancient cities of Rabat and Salé where Morocco’s Bouregreg River meets the Atlantic Ocean, overlooking the Hassan Tower, Kasbah des Oudayas and the Great Mosque of Salé.

This project was initiated in 2010 by Bab Al Bahr Development Company (BBDC), a joint venture between Al Maabar and the Morocco-based Bouregreg Agency. By 2012, 70 percent of Bab Al Bahr’s available phase one Marina and River front residential properties had been sold. An architectural innovation that reflects the region’s ancient art forms and Arab-Andalusian lifestyle, Bab Al Bahr is expected to become an iconic landmark of Rabat, following its completion in 2016.

A combination of commercial, retail, hospitality and residential units in the heart of the Libyan capital, Al Waha comprises a 20-story luxury hotel, 23-story office tower and “market” space with a hypermarket, restaurants and retail shops that spans across 40,000 square metres. Al Waha, which has been designed to pay tribute to Libya’s rich cultural heritage and landscapes, particularly the Oasis town of Ghadamis,will feature the country’s first Rotana property.

A joint venture development between the Libyan Investment and Development Company (LIDCO) and Al Maabar, Al Waha has been a long-time vision of Al Maabar. Seeing Libya’s significantly untapped market potential early on, Al Maabar pioneered in the country’s real estate market, setting the trend for glamorous mixed-use landmark properties.

Success Stories
Bab Al Bahr revival through dedicated preservation

About 70 percent of the Bouregreg Valley’s historical landmarks are located within Bab Al Bahr development site, and as part of its efforts to preserve and protect these historical landmarks, Al Maabar has been working closely with local community partners such as Morocco’s Bouregreg Agency. In order to achieve this, Al Maabar has assigned a team of specialist archaeologists to provide their expertise throughout the project duration, so that Bab Al Bahr continues to deliver unique cultural returns, economic and social benefits for the local community.

Being developed as an integrated city that joins the two ancient cities of Rabat and Salé, Bab Al Bahr will bring about a Renaissance in the country’s capital, while increasing pedestrian crossings for the residents, businesses and visitors of the new community that continues to flourish every day.

Given the sheer size and scale of Bab Al Bahr (292,000 square metres), which includes seven districts – Jewel, Cultural, Salé Wall, Arts, Service, Marina and River front Districts, built around the Central District, it’s inevitable that this development will become Morocco’s new trendiest downtown. The country’s first tramway will offer passengers a delightful tour of downtown Rabat, which showcases museums, art galleries, boutiques and the future Rabat Grand Theatre, which is being designed by Zaha Hadid, the internationally acclaimed Iraqi architect who designed The Aquatic Centre to host the 2012 London Olympics and the Dubai Opera house.

Creating a positive economic impact in Jordan

With the strategic aim to ensure long-term economic and social returns for the local Marsa Zayed community, Aqaba Real Estate Development Company has been working in close collaboration with the Aqaba Development Corporation (ADC) and Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority (ASEZA), both responsible for driving Aqaba’s economic growth.

Marsa Zayed is already supporting the enhancement of the Jordanian economy through job creation, talent development, knowledge transfer, economic stimulus and foreign exchange gains.

In 2010, the Aqaba Real Estate Development Company signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Jordan’s National Company for Employment and Training, the organization overseeing the implementation of the National Training and Employment Project (NTEP), which supports the efforts of the Ministry of Labour to reduce the country’s unemployment rate especially among the youth population by providing job training. Upon completion, the Marsa Zayed development is predicted to generate approximately 15,000 jobs for Jordan.

In 2012, Al Maabar signed a five-year agreement with the Jordan River Foundation (JRF), a non-profit nongovernmental organisation chaired by Her Majesty Queen Rania Al Abdullah of Jordan with the mission to help Jordanians to realise their full economic potential and overcome social challenges. To date, economic development programmes aimed to stimulate the local economy, while increasing the involvement of local authorities in development projects have been implemented, in addition to those that encourage the development of Aqaba’s community and increase socioeconomic empowerment amongst the youth segment. Significant milestones have already been reached with more than 500 enrolled programme participants, including 150 Jordanian youth.

Company Profile

Headquartered in Dubai, DAMAC Holdings is a regional business conglomerate that has grown from a specialist catering company into a multi-billion dollar corporation. As one of the largest private corporate organisations in the region, it has expanded its presence with offices in the Middle East, North Africa, CIS States, Europe, Far East and the Subcontinent.

The organisations maiden venture in catering has grown from strength-to-strength to become the largest catering services provider in the Middle East serving over 150,000 meals per day. It manages projects in a number of markets including the Middle East, Africa and CIS. In addition to servicing construction campsites, army camps, educational institutions, 5-star hotels and onshore/offshore locations, the division specializes in providing ancillary services like camp managements, maintenance and manpower supply.

DAMAC Properties, the largest of the group companies and its greatest success was established in 2002 as a residential, leisure and commercial real estate developer. Within a short period of time, its focus on construction and contract delivery resulted in fast expansion into North Africa, Jordan, Lebanon, Qatar and Saudi Arabia.

As the region’s largest private developer, DAMAC Properties quickly established a pedigree and a proven track record for delivering some of the most luxurious residential, commercial, retail, leisure and hospitality developments in the Middle East.
