Company Profile

Civil aviation connects cities and countries, people and places, and is central to the growth of nation’s economies and the world at large. Dubai as a tourism, trade and lifestyle hub hosts over 200 nationalities that thrive in this multicultural metropolis, and Emirates is at the very heart of it, as an airline that is truly international in stature. It is a unique company and an ambitious success story, in that it draws its strength from the open skies environment at its main operating base, Dubai International Airport, where it competes with over 120 other scheduled airlines.

Emirates, in a span of under two decades, has turned into one of the world’s fastest growing airlines, mirroring the growth of the nation from where it took wings. Starting with just two aircraft in its formative days in 1985, the airline now boasts a fleet of nearly 200 fuel-efficient aircraft. Throughout the company’s history, upgrading and expanding the fleet has been a continuous programme and its business strategy has charted the growth of its global network of routes which today spans six continents.

Today, Emirates flies to over 125 destinations in 74 countries and no fewer than 15 new destinations were added during the year 2012 alone. Starting with Rio de Janeiro and Buenos Aires on 3rd January 2012 and including Dublin, Dallas Fort Worth, Seattle, Lusaka, Harare, Ho Chi Minh City, Barcelona, Lisbon, Erbil, Washington DC and Adelaide, finally concluding the year by commencing services to Lyon and Phuket in December 2012. Furthermore, in line with this increase in destinations and to facilitate aircraft upgrades of many routes, coupled with additional frequencies around the network, Emirates received delivery of 33 aircraft during 2012, a record for the airline.

The secret to the airline’s business success is to provide the best service in the industry through inter-linked, complementary array of businesses. These include Emirates SkyCargo, an award-winning international cargo division; a full-fledged destination management and leisure division including Emirates Holidays, Arabian Adventures and Wolgan Valley Resort & Spa, among others; EmQuest - an airline IT developer and Emirates Engineering – an advanced aircraft maintenance provider.

The airline’s growth has been consistent over the years and it has been making waves at every step of the way. In 2001, Emirates demonstrated its confidence in the industry’s future growth by announcing the largest order in aviation history, valued at USD 15 billion. While in 2005, it announced the largest-ever order for the Boeing 777 family of aircraft – 42 in all – worth USD 9.7 billion. At the 2006 Farnborough Air Show, Emirates signed a Heads of Agreement for 10 of Boeing’s new 747-8F aircraft, to be powered by General Electric’s GEnx jet engines.

Emirates still regards the Airbus A380 as its flagship aircraft and the ultimate symbol of its pioneering spirit, continuing to expand the fleet and the routes it serves. Already flying this fleet of over 30 iconic double-deckers to Amsterdam, Auckland, Bangkok, Beijing, Hong Kong, Jeddah, Johannesburg, Kuala Lumpur, London Heathrow, Manchester, Melbourne, Munich, New York JFK, Paris, Rome, Seoul, Shanghai, Singapore, Sydney, Tokyo and Toronto, the airline has plans in the pipeline to increase the destinations even further as new aircraft currently on order are received.

Testimonial to its stature as an innovative leader, it is also the world’s largest operator of the Boeing 777 and celebrated the unveiling of the 1,000th Boeing 777 in March 2012 at the manufacturer’s plant in Everett, Washington State. This landmark aircraft became the 102nd to join the airline’s 777 fleet.

Emirates SkyCargo, the freight division of Emirates, continues to be a significant force in the global air cargo industry, expanding its fleet or freighters by taking delivery of four additional Boeing 777 freighters during 2012. During the 2011/12 financial year, Emirates SkyCargo carried almost 1.8 million tons of cargo, demonstrating its resilience and ability to grow revenues against the industry norm. Contributing 16.2 per cent of Emirates’ total transport revenue, Emirates SkyCargo continues to play an integral role in the company’s expanding operations.

SkyCargo also continues to win awards, again scooping several prestigious accolades during 2012, including Cargo Airline of the year, Best African Cargo Airline and, for the 24th consecutive year, Best Middle East Cargo Airline at the Cargo Airline of the Year Awards, as well as being named Air Cargo Carrier of the Year for the sixth time at the Global Freight Awards.

In May 2012, the Emirates Group, which includes dnata one of the world’s largest air services providers, posted profits of US$ 629 million for the financial year ending 31st March 2012. It was the 24th consecutive year of profits amidst unprecedented economic pressure and record high fuel prices. Further to this landmark achievement, Emirates’ position as the industry-leading A380 operator was reinforced with the opening of the world’s first purpose-built A380 concourse on 1 January 2013 at the Dubai International Airport.

Part of the strategic plan by Dubai Airports to support Emirates’ ambitious growth, the new concourse is also tipped to bolster Dubai’s ambition to become the largest international air hub in the world by 2018. Moreover, it will become the nerve centre of Emirates’ A380 operations and as the company’s growing fleet of popular double-deckers touches more points around the globe, it brings new levels of environmental sensitivity. In addition, its enhanced facilities and features will provide a new level in the passenger experience of tomorrow.

Continuing its financial success, in March 2013, Emirates issued a one billion dollar Sukuk on NASDAQ Dubai, the Middle East’s international financial exchange. Reinforcing the strength of its business, the Sukuk was sold successfully to investors from around the world.

As the fleet and destinations grow, Emirates’ recruitment drive also shifts up a gear, with road shows and recruitment events worldwide as the airline strives to maintain the cosmopolitan nature of its multinational, multilingual crew. During 2012, the number of pilots working for the airline grew to over 3,000 with a current figure of some 16,000 cabin crew from over 130 nationalities. In total, over 67,000 staff are employed across the entire Emirates Group. In the financial year 2011/2012, Emirates carried 34 million passengers and 1.8 million tonnes of cargo.

Smarter technology to enhance the customer experience

Creating the ultimate passenger experience remains of paramount importance to Emirates. No wonder then that the brand constantly leads the way with technological innovation, from the inflight entertainment to provision of on-board connectivity, which have proven to be one of keys to its continued success.

Its multi award-winning “ice” digital widescreen provides passengers with over 1,400 channels from which to choose. All new Boeing 777 aircraft joining the fleet come equipped with some of the industry’s widest personal HD TV screens going up to 27-inch in First Class. The screens are also enhanced by Emirates’ new touch-based Graphical User Interface (GUI).

For business travellers and those who need to stay connected whilst in the air, the latest technology offered by the OnAir system allows passengers on board any A380 to use their own mobile phones to make and receive phone calls and text messages inflight, in addition to using the OnAir WiFi service. The first recorded A380 inflight call was placed to China while the aircraft cruised at 11,500 metres flying from to Dubai to Munich.

During 2012, the airline also drove the further development of a line-of-business application named Knowledge Driven Inflight Service (KIS), operating on Windows 8 HP ElitePad business tablets, enabling Emirates cabin crew to continue to deliver extraordinary customer service to passengers.

Flying high, the environmentally-friendly way

Operating a young fleet with an average aircraft age of less than 80 months makes a significant contribution to Emirates’ ability to maintain its leading position in the industry in terms of fuel efficiency. Among the many phenomenal achievement of the company is the fact that even as Emirates has grown leaps and bounds, its fuel-efficiency rating is some 22% lower than the IATA (International Air Transport Association) global fleet average.

Emirates participates in the INSPIRE (Indian Ocean Strategic Partnership to Reduce Emissions) Programme, a partnership created in March 2011 between air navigation service providers and a select group of airlines with the aim of using best practices to reduce the fuel burn and carbon emissions associated with aviation.

Evidencing its commitment, Emirates conducted three test flights – Dubai to Brisbane, Perth to Dubai and Dubai to Durban. Using a Boeing 777-300ER, the airline’s most fuel-efficient aircraft, the flights involved a number of operational and navigational efficiency improvements that included minimising on ground delays and utilising expedient taxi-and runways, among several others. The first two test flights produced a combined total saving of over five tonnes of fuel and 16 tonnes of CO2. The third flight to Durban resulted in savings of nearly two tonnes of fuel and six tonnes of CO2 emissions.

Company Profile

Amid the real estate renaissance and due to the unprecedented growth of UAE residents and visitors in the end of the nineties, a hospitality company “Jumeirah Group” was established to meet the increasing demand for hotels and resorts not only in the UAE but also in the region. With hard work and commitment, Jumeirah Group has successfully built a world-class portfolio of prestigious hotels and resorts within a short span of operation.

Jumeirah Group’s journey of success commenced with opening Jumeirah Beach Hotel in Dubai in 1997. In the following years, a number of landmark properties in the UAE were launched under the group’s management : Wild Wadi Water Park and Jumeirah’s flagship hotel “Burj Al Arab” in 1999, Jumeirah Emirates Towers in 2000, the Emirates Academy of Hospitality Management in 2001 and Madinat Jumeirah in 2003.

Building on its rich expertise in launching and managing luxurious hotels and resorts, Jumeirah Group successfully established its own Arabian traditions of luxury in hospitality that enabled it to fly high on the international sphere.

In 2001, the international expansion plans started out in London with the launching of Jumeirah Carlton Tower and Jumeirah Lowndes Hotel. The selection of these hotels came after a careful evaluation process that resulted in acquiring them. For instance, Jumeirah Carlton Tower was chosen based on its strategic location as it is situated in the heart of fashionable Knightsbridge and is within walking distance of the prestigious Harrods and Harvey Nichols department stores, and luxury designer boutiques lining Sloane Street. The hotel was voted ‘4th Best UK Business Hotel’ at the Conde Nast Traveller Reader’s Travel Awards 2010 and in the top three Best Hotels for Service in the UK in the Conde Nast Gold List 2011.

Effectively and uniquely managing the properties is the first priority of the group which was commercially important for the validity and credibility of the brand. “STAY DIFFERENT” trade mark – a commitment by Jumeirah Group to ensure that every guest is an individual and is different – was launched to reconfirm that the individuality of each guest as well as each hotel and resort. And so far, it has been achieving these goals in Dubai and London developments. As it takes on new hotels, the company is committed to demonstrate to its owners that they made the right choice. The group is currently looking at expanding within the region and within Asia. Its ambitious expansion plans to grow its portfolio of luxury hotels and resorts worldwide are well underway, with projects currently under development in the UAE, Jordan, Qatar, Kuwait, Egypt, Morocco, India, Indonesia, Thailand, China, Azerbaijan and the Caribbean.

Joining Dubai Holding, a global investment holding company headquartered in Dubai, in 2004 has given the group support to continue its international expansion strategy. By 2005, Jumeirah has established itself as a leader in hospitality business operating highly successful hotels in Dubai and London. At that point the company decided to embark on the path of international expansion on a hotel management basis (common industry practice followed by other leading hotel companies), and signed its first letter of understanding for a hotel in Shanghai. This was followed by launching Jumeirah Essex House in New York.

The company has also expanded to include luxury serviced apartments and residences under the brand ‘Jumeirah Living’ with launching Jumeirah Living World Trade Centre Residence in 2008. Recently, successive expansions have been witnessed locally and internationally.

Even though it is much newer than renowned brands that have been around for decades, Jumeirah Group has successfully developed its awareness on a global basis. However, when it comes to the luxury end, Jumeirah is certainly up there in recognition with companies like Four Seasons, Shangri-La and the Ritz-Carlton. It is recognized in that market space by the travelers who use hotels at that level.

Company Profile

The United Arab Emirates (UAE) has risen within a short span of time to build a highly commendable civil and commercial aviation history. One of the most innovative and iconic names in this sector is Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA). The company, formed in 1976 by a Royal Decree, initially started off with a capital of AED 2 million. By the year 2011, it successfully transformed itself into a major group with a commendable international presence and an annual turnover in excess of AED 1.8 billion.

Starting with just two aircraft, the company gradually grew to expand its operations and even form two subsidiaries. As part of His Highness the late Sheikh Zayed Bin Sultan Al Nahyan’s policy of encouraging national companies, the company was transferred to full public status on 16 May 1982. This resulted in the establishment of a National Joint Stock Company with a capital of AED 60 million with the government retaining 30% of the capital. By 1983, its fleet size increased to a total of 34 aircraft, flying over 38,800 hours annually, an average of over 100 hours per day.

In the year 1985, ADA received the authorisation to operate fixed wing aircraft and to conduct third-party aircraft maintenance, while it was awarded its first fixed wing aircraft contract in 1991. Following this, the group continually expanded its operations across the region and beyond. Presently it has, to its credit, numerous completed contracts throughout the Middle East as well as a several global ones including a prestigious fire-fighting contract in Spain. ADA also provides two critical, round-the-clock health and rescue services: medical evacuation service (medevac) for all the oil companies based in Abu Dhabi as well as search and rescue support for the UAE Armed Forces.

In order to expandits service scope, the group formed Royal Jet, an international luxury flight services provider, in the year 2003 as a joint venture with the Presidential Flight Authority (PFA). Royal Jet is a pioneer of luxury executive flight services in the Middle East and has achieved phenomenal growth with a significant international presence. It has won several commendations including ‘World’s Leading Private Jet Service’ at the World Travel Awards for five consecutive years. Its current fleet of aircraft includes six Boeing Business Jets, making it the world’s single largest independent operator of this jetliner.

Its second subsidiary, Maximus Air Cargo, is the largest all-cargo airline operator in the UAE. It was established in 2005 and has rapidly become a key player in the region and was voted ‘Air Cargo Operator of the Year’ at leading regional industry awards. Headquartered in Abu Dhabi, Maximus also has a European office in Stansted in the UK and operates a fully-owned fleet of eight freighter aircraft. It excels in a number of key market sectors including operating scheduled cargo services on behalf of renowned airlines like Etihad, Iberia and Air France. The airline is also a market leader in managing oversized, heavy and highrisk loads.

Reflecting Abu Dhabi Aviation’s commitment to humanitarian causes, Maximum continues to be the exclusive air relief support partner for the UAE Red Crescent. In addition, the airline is also a founder member of Care by Air, a relief initiative.

ADA also provided six helicopters to serve as air ambulances in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA). This was the result of the excellent market reputation as well as the trust and confidence that the company instils. This agreement proved that the Middle Eastern aviation sector is capable of delivering world-class air ambulances for emergency medical service purposes across the region.

ADA has also operated one Bell 212 helicopter in support of long-term contracts for a major oil company in the Republic of Yemen. Additionally, the company has an on-going fire-fighting contract in Spain. The company’s marketing department actively pursues new business opportunities worldwide.

Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) has been an innovative and forward-moving group. One of its advancements is utilising specialist under slung load techniques for seismic surveys for oil and other natural resources. Since its inception, ADA has flown over 770,000 hours in helicopters and over 35,000 hours in fixed wing aircraft. Recognising its achievements, Bell Helicopter awarded the company with a plaque representing 700,000 helicopter hours.

Since its inception, ADA is now approaching the highlylaudable milestones of 1,000,000 flight hours in helicopters and over 55,000 hours in fixed wing aircraft. Sustaining these successes requires a high degree of dedication, commitment and professionalism, which is why ADA has made these the hallmarks of its team of pilots, engineers and support staff. In the recent past, ADAmoved to a larger purpose-built facility at the Abu Dhabi International Airport in order to substantially expand its capabilities, while exploring greater challenges globally. For the year ending 2011, the group’s operating profit reached US$ 127 million, up 13%, while net profit increased by 22% to US$ 76.46 million.

The motto at Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) continues to be ‘The sky is the limit’, as it believes that there is always room for expansion in the multifarious aviation sectors. An example is the area of training that has a new simulation centre under construction, which will serve not only its employees, but also the UAE and the region.

As important to ADA’s success has been an ever-increasing engagement with its strategic partners in the Oil &Gas industry, locallyand internationally, as well as in the formation of established partnerships, most notably with Mubadala and Agusta Westland. As it expands further, it continues to strive to provide aviation services that set the standard, both regionally and globally. The company continues to pursue joint ventures with manufacturers in order to enhance its offerings and capabilities, while planning several projects in line with the country’s Vision 2030.

Success Stories
Flying high in Spain

Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) has a commendable track-record of undertaking high-risk aerial support not just in the UAE, but also beyond national borders. Its helicopters are configured to perform a number of specialised tasks. One of its longest-running contracts in this field is with the Catalonia region of Spain. The company has been undertaking fire-fighting services, as and when required, in the region since the year 1996. Spain’s dry climate and wooded terrain make it highly vulnerable to wildfire outbursts, especially during the hot summer months. This was demonstrably severe in the year 2009 when ADA successfully managed to contain several wildfires.

ADA uses Bell 212 and 412s helicopters fitted with highly-effective Isolair ventral tank and snorkel systems for these critical tasks. These are regularly seen in action in Catalonia, scooping up and delivering water to quench the flames.

Enabling aerial care across Saudi Arabia

In the year 2010, Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) signed an agreement with the Saudi Red Crescent Authority to provide six helicopters to serve as air ambulances.This agreement further strengthened commercial relations between the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA) and the UAE, while achieving significant growth for ADA in this key market. It also showcased the trust and confidence in ADA’s capability to provide international standards in services needed to meet the requirements of Saudi Arabia’s Emergency Medical Services Programme.

As part of the contract, ADA provided six fully-crewed helicopters, comprising four Bell 412s and two AW 139s as air ambulances, to be stationed at the two main bases of Saudi Arabia - Jeddah and Riyadh, providing roundthe- clock coverage.

This agreement represented an important milestone in the company’s global expansion strategy. Most importantly, this HEMS (Helicopter Emergency Medical Services) operation in KSA contributed greatly towards saving many lives through timely support, in particular during the Ramadan and Hajj seasons.

Company Profile

Aviation is the backbone of any destination that aims to be a strategic hub for business, trade, tourism and commerce. More so in the case of the UAE, which has the vision to be the centre of the world as a link between economies globally. Etihad Airways, the national airline of the United Arab Emirates, plays a commendable role in this aspect, while building strategic international partnerships. Set up by Royal Decree in July 2003, Etihad Airways commenced commercial operations in November 2003 and has gone on to become one of the fastest growing airlines in the history of commercial aviation. It has, in a short span of just under a decade, established itself as the world’s leading airline.

Soaring ahead with its foresighted plans, the airline unfailingly reflects the best of Arabian hospitality - cultured, considerate, warm and generous and continues to enhance the prestige of Abu Dhabi as a centre of hospitality between East and West.

By June 2006, the airline grew to 30 destinations in 30 months. In 2008 Etihad carried more than 6 million passengers, a 30 percent increase from 2007, and flew to over 50 destinations. Continuing this trends, by 2009 the airline had achieved a target of over 60 destinations including Melbourne, Astana, Istanbul, Athens, Larnaca, Chicago, Cape Town and Hyderabad.

While its main business is the international air transportation of passengers, Etihad Airways also operates Etihad Holidays and Etihad Cargo. Supporting these is a global contact centre organisation, which is present in four locations, employing over 500 people who speak 11 languages altogether.

Furthering its global ambitions and enabling innovation in the aviation sector, Etihad Airways started investing significantly in major airlines globally. On December 2011, it announced the purchase of a 29 percent stake in airberlin, Germany’s second largest airline. In January 2012, it acquired a 40 percent stake in Air Seychelles Ltd. as part of a strategic partnership. The agreement, the first-of-its-kind in Africa, includes strategic measures to encourage Air Seychelles’ long-term commercial growth as well as a comprehensive codeshare agreement. In the same year, Etihad Airways and Aer Lingus signed a historic interline and codeshare agreement alongside a 2.987 percent equity investment in the Irish carrier.

Etihad Airways also acquired an equity stake in Virgin Australia Holdings of just over five per cent, to be increased to a maximum of 10 per cent in the future. This investment in Virgin Australia’s domestic operations significantly strengthened the 10-year strategic partnership forged by the two carriers in August 2010.

The airline’s success can be perceived by its continued growth and formidable numbers. It achieved a 31 percent increase in its Q2 2012 revenues to US$1.25 billion with passenger numbers of 2.55 million, up 34 percent over the same period in the previous year.

Recognising its achievements, it has received a range of awards that reflect its position as the leading premium airline brand in the world. These include ‘World’s Leading Airline’ at the World Travel Awards in 2009, 2010 and 2011 as well as three prestigious commendations at the 2012 Skytrax World Airline Awards.

The group also focuses on sustainability through the adoption of a stringent environmental policy, partnership with the Masdar future energy initiative and a fuel-efficient fleet. It also supports the community via a wide range of training opportunities locally and is also a major sponsor in the world of sports globally. These include Etihad Stadium, F1 Etihad Airways Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, Harlequins Rugby Football Club and Manchester City Football Club.

Currently, Etihad Airways serves 86 destinations in 56 countries with a fleet that is one of the youngest in the world, comprising 67 passenger and cargo aircraft. It employs more than 9,800 staff representing over 126 nationalities.

Success Story
Partners in German aviation growth

When Etihad Airways set out to innovate the global aviation industry, it chose Germany for its first equity investment, keenly aware of the potential of the European market and historic significance of this nation’s engineering excellence. In December 2011, it acquired a 29.2 percent stake in airberlin – Europe’s sixth largest airline and Germany’s second biggest carrier – thereby becoming its single largest shareholder.

Under the partnership, airberlin now operates daily flights from Berlin and Düsseldorf to Abu Dhabi, and additionally operates daily services from Abu Dhabi to Phuket in Thailand. Etihad Airways codeshares on all airberlin flights to/from Abu Dhabi and beyond the four German hubs, providing passengers with access to an additional 976 flights per week. This, and several other codeshare agreements, give Etihad Airwards a combined network of 384 destinations, more than any other Middle East airline, thus making it the UAE’s modern day success story in the skies.

Firmly entrenched at the core of the group’s business strategy are innovative thinking, outstanding experience, deep regional insights and exceptional commitment. Keeping up with the times, as a socially-responsible name, MANAFA LLC imbibes eco-efficiency and ethical environmental policies in every aspect of its operations.

The group showcases the success of the liberal and the business-friendly regulatory environment of the UAE as well as the distinction of the nation’s infrastructure and financial practices. MAAFA LLC has ensured that the support of these key elements has brought forth efficiency in business, both in the UAE and regionally.

Company Profile

Waha capital stands as one of the notable and distinct investment holding companies in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi, both in the sectors it invests and engages in and in being the largest and most diverse public investment holding company listed in the Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange. The group now stands at the threshold of significant planned growth as it looks to double its assets over the next five years.

The company started in 1997 as Oasis Leasing with a simple but winning formula. Demand for aircrafts, among other big ticket assets like ships, vessels and infrastructure components, was expected to grow rapidly not only in the GCC and MENA region but across the globe. At the time, there were no regional players that provided customers with a one-stop solution for their varying aircraft and big ticket assets leasing needs. Oasis Leasing (now named Waha Leasing) competently occupied this carefully selected niche with clear success.

Waha Leasing is now at the core of Waha Capital’s business model and growth drivers. Waha today owns leases, manages or has a financial interest in a portfolio of more than 100 aircrafts valued at over USD 4.3 billion, putting the company at the leading edge to provide regional and international airlines with the right solution at the right time. Waha Leasing’s list of clients currently includes top league airlines such as Emirates, Etihad Airways, Qatar Airways, Air Canada and Malaysian airlines. Through offering a diverse range of leasing options that suit the needs of regional and internal clients, Waha Leasing and Waha Capital aim to play a vital role in realising the Abu Dhabi 2030 economic vision, which regards aviation as a major pillar of Abu Dhabi’s economy and sees the Emirate as a key global player across the different sectors of the aviation industries’ international value chain.

Because Abu Dhabi’s economic strategy is ambitious, the Waha team have constantly been looking for opportunities to contribute to the Emirate’s and the nation’s economy in a unique and value-adding way. This drive was the key to the company being listed in the Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange (ADAX) in 2001 and to the strategic decision of turning Waha into an Investment Holding Company in 2008.

Along with Waha Leasing, Waha established three other subsidiaries targeting key growth sectors locally and internationally. Seeing the growth in demand and the need for modernisation and improvement across the Oil Supply Vessels industry (OsVs) in the GCC and Caspian region, Waha established Waha Maritime to make strategic investments and utilise the opportunities in this niche as well as other niches in the marine and shipping industry. Taking advantage of its healthy financial situation, Waha Marine expects to make a difference in the regional (OsVs) scene by being able to invest in companies and assets, help in modernising fleets, and introduce new and competitive technologies into those highly important assets in the oil industry.

The company made its first successful investment in 2008 by acquiring a significant stake in GMMOS Group, a major industrial holding company operating in the Marine, Oil and Gas and Crane-hire sectors across the high-growth Gulf and Caspian markets. Waha also established Waha Financial Investments (WFI), a company that intends to provide specialised banking services through the establishment of financial institutions, acquisitions and joint ventures. Its primary purpose is to support the company and Abu Dhabi government in achieving their goals through providing various financial products and services. Waha Capital, through its financial services arm, has been able to arrange significant deals for the UAE armed forces and is given mandate to arrange similar, large-scale finance for the Army and other government bodies in Abu Dhabi and the UAE.

In spite of its regional focus, Waha is also keen to contribute to the internal, organic growth of the Emirate of Abu Dhabi as it actualises its ambitious economic vision. Waha Land, a specialised subsidiary aiming at multi-purpose property development, was established in late 2008. Almarkaz, a mixed-use industrial project located strategically along the railway line, is intended to link the entire country by 2014. The project will be built in three phases and will cost USD 4 billion.

The word synergy is at the heart of the structure and management approach of Waha Capital. In spite of its diversified nature and strong international presence, Waha’s top management asserts that all business units and subsidiaries contribute to the success of the overall group, conducting projects and operations that are linked to the core areas in which Waha operates. This can be demonstrated in the case of Waha Financial Investments, where Waha realised that many of its government and corporate clients in Waha Leasing, both regionally and internationally, need assistance with arranging financing for their Asset Leasing needs. WFI helps to arrange this financing through its network and leverages its capital wealth and its ownership of its assets to achieve this objective.

Success Story
Thinking differently in times of turmoil

In the last couple of years, the entire globe went through an unprecedented financial and business downturn that has not been experienced for almost a century. It was natural, therefore, for many businesses to suffer in these challenging conditions, while a few businesses were able to flourish. Driven by its quest for innovation and ongoing ability to identify unique niches to cater to in all its areas of operations, Waha Capital, through its Waha Leasing subsidiary, was among these few, through entering a totally new and highly growing segment in the middle of the global crisis.

Seeing the long-term upwards trend in global air travel in general, Waha Leasing realised that the global financial conditions and the increasing ability of new segments of the world’s population to afford flying is creating a new opportunity, namely low-cost airlines. Such airlines rely heavily on using smaller aircrafts, a more financially feasible strategy to ensure the success of their business model. Taking these facts into account, Waha have made a highly strategic move by acquiring a 50 percent stake in AerVenture Holding, a global company focusing on owning and leasing Airbus A320 family aircraft.

Established in 2006, AerVenture currently has a total asset base of USD 2.4 billion. Its fleet consists of 22 aircrafts with firm orders of 32 more aircrafts. AerVenture is now managed in partnership between Waha Leasing, a subsidiary of Waha Capital, and AerCap Limited N.V AerVenture, a global leader in providing aviation solutions for operators and carriers. This partnership helps leverage the expertise of Aercap and expand the global reach of AerVenture, particularly in emerging regions such as the Middle East and Africa.

Waha Capital, through its different subsidiaries, has proven that it can take a lead in creating new business opportunities in the time of crisis. The AerVenture Holding deal provides only a glimpse at one of the pioneering deals made by Waha Maritime, Waha Land and Waha Financial Investments.
