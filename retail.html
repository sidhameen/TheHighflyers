Company Profile

The roots of trading and business in the emirate of Dubai date back to several centuries in history. In the recent past, the formation of the United Arab Emirates propelled it ahead as the nation’s hub for ambition with a plethora of family business houses transitioning into global conglomerates.

Substantially noteworthy among these is Al-Futtaim Group, a name that is often evoked as one of the gems of the nation’s diversified economy. It was established in the 1930s as a trading business by the late Hamad Bin Majid Al-Futtaim, one of the most prominent businessmen in Dubai and the Gulf of those times. The group’s business journey started with a shift by the founder from the traditional pearl diving and trading model that dominated the region’s economy to new lines of trading, investments and import activities influenced by the shifts in demand for natural pearls globally.

Today, Al-Futtaim Group is one of the most progressive regional business houses, headquartered in Dubai, United Arab Emirates and present across the globe with varied ventures. The group significantly expanded its business operations in recent years through a strategic acquisition plan and entered a number of new territories, increasing its footprint beyond the GCC and greater Middle East to encompass South East and North Asia, Australasia and Europe.

Managing its growth tactically, it is structured into eight operational divisions; automotive, electronics, engineering and technologies, financial services, general services, joint ventures, real estate & hospitality, and retail. The success of Al-Futtaim is attributed to proactively managing change whilst upholding the values of integrity, service and social responsibility. The majority of its businesses, built on a portfolio of world-leading brands, dominate their respective sectors.

Its historical precedents have set the pace in capitalising on emerging opportunities around the region. Rapid development throughout the 1940s and 50s saw it establish itself regionally as an integrated commercial, industrial and services organisation, positioning itself one of the leading business houses in the lower Gulf region.

One of the group’s key features is the belief in decentralisation, giving the heads of the operating companies a high degree of functional autonomy and authority. This, in turn, provides the group with essential flexibility, while presenting individual employees with a clearly defined work culture and sense of responsibility.

The group currently comprises a diverse range of strategically-positioned operating subsidiaries and associate companies, structured to keep the group ahead of local competition, whilst matching the pace with the ever-evolving global business scenario. The group’s continued investment in world-class systems technology is clear evidence of its commitment to maintain leading-edge performance and service delivery.

Al-Futtaim Group represents many of the world’s leading brands both in the UAE and the wider region. These names include Toyota, Lexus, Honda, Volvo, Chrysler, Jeep and Dodge, Panasonic, Toshiba, Sanyo, Alcatel, IKEA, Ace, Marks & Spencer, Toys R Us, Seiko, Raymond Weil, Westar, Kolber and Minato Pearls, in addition to its in-house brands that include Plug-Ins retail stores and AFTRON consumer electronics.

The group has also formed several subsidiaries in the GCC nations, while also exporting its expertise as well as unique concepts beyond the region, from Singapore to Pakistan to Egypt as well as Europe and CIS.

In Singapore, Al-Futtaim Group formed a presence through the acquisition of Robinson and Company (RCL), world-renowned for its department and specialty stores such as Robinsons, John Little, Principles, and Fat Face, among others in Singapore and Malaysia. This move established the group as a significant presence in one of the most buoyant economies in the South-east Asia region. Furthermore, it has been involved in automobile engineering sector in Pakistan for over three decades and continues to form new industrial collaboration agreements.

The latest, most innovative development undertaken by Al-Futtaim is the Dubai Festival City - a ‘city within a city’ that covers 1,600 acres and stretches along 4km of the Dubai Creek shoreline, built at an investment of over USD 2 billion by the group. This waterfront destination showcases prestigious retailers, an international gourmet culture, a golf course and an entertainment retreat.

Located at the heart of Dubai Festival City is the mall that hosts over 600 shops including 25 flagship stores, over 100 restaurants, cafés and bistros, a cinema complex, and a vast entertainment centre. Surging forward with its approach to expanding its success beyond home, the group launched its Festival City concept in Doha and Cairo, which are currently progressing in phases.

As part of the commitment to the communities in which it operates, Al-Futtaim believes in training and developing nationals. By equipping talented individuals with the skills required in the commercial workplace, it effectively builds rewarding careers for nationals at every level. It has a longstanding policy of positively recruiting indigenous nationals throughout its businesses and has a clearly structured programme in place designed to attract and retain talented nationals. It also invests considerably in the sustainability of the planet in the form of energy-efficiency and eco-friendly measures.

At present, Al-Futtaim Group employs in excess of 40,000 people and operates through more than 100 companies bearing its name, continuing a proud tradition with its everexpanding footprint spanning beyond the GCC and Greater Middle East to encompass South East and North Asia, Australasia and Europe.

Success Stories
Festivities for everyone around the region

The Festival City brand was formed to represent a type of inspirational lifestyle achievable through the creation of mixed-use urban communities. The flagship development for the brand in Dubai drew inspiration from international destinations such as St. Mark’s Square in Venice, Darling Harbour in Sydney, Covent Garden in London and the Croissette in Cannes.

It showcased, yet again, the fact that Al-Futtaim Group as a conglomerate consistently delivered iconic experiences and landmarks. As the owner, developer and operator of the globally-acknowledged Festival City brand, it chose Cairo, Egypt and Doha, Qatar as the next destinations for this successful ‘city within a city’ concept.

On 5th September 2010, Al-Futtaim signed a QAR 6 billion joint venture agreement to construct this state-ofthe- art entertainment and retail complex. October 2011 marked the ground-breaking for the Doha Festival City, owned and developed by Bawabat Al-Shamal Real Estate Company (BASREC), which comprises four primary shareholders including Al-Futtaim Real Estate Services, Qatar Islamic Bank (QIB), and private Qatari investors. Doha Festival City is the largest super-regional, mixed-use development currently under construction in the Middle East and is located 15km north of downtown Doha.

IKEA, the Al-Futtaim franchise brand, which forms part of the city’s offerings, opened its doors in March 2013. Doha Festival City upon completion in 2016 is slated to span almost 335,000 sq mt with nearly 270,000 sq mt set aside for retail space. Acknowledging its uniqueness, it was awarded the prestigious honour of “Best Future Project” at the Global RLI Awards 2012 held in London.

Furthering its success, the group also launched the Cairo Festival City, a master-planned community covering over 3 million sq mt, comprising residential communities, a retail resort with over 300 shops and services, over 100 restaurants, premium office space, an automotive park, and a school. This city is slated to be completed by 2018 with several phases already operational.

The strategic approach of Al-Futtaim Group has seen the Festival City brand become the mainstay for leisure, shopping, residential and entertainment in the three nations that it is currently present in, creating a unique success for the region and occupying a place-of-pride.

Breakthrough category in consumer electronics

As the innovation in gadgets and in turn, the demand, saw a manifold increase in the 1990s, Al-Futtaim Group saw an opportunity to apply its expertise acquired in the retail sector to spin-off its own brand. The year 1994 saw the group launch AFTRON, offering consumer electronics and home appliances that offer high-quality in the mid-priced segment, which has seen a rise across the region and beyond.

AFTRON was started with the aim to provide better quality of life to the customers by delivering range of futuristic products that are reliable, practical and affordable. The business model involved outsourcing manufacturing to renowned names at world-class facilities with stringent checks for quality and reliability.

Starting with increasing availability in the UAE at all leading electronic stores, it formed an exclusive showroom in Dubai as well as distribution and export divisions. Expanding rapidly, the brand has quickly established itself as preferred name. Today, AFTRON is distributed and serviced in more than 30 countries in GCC, Middle East, South Asia, Africa and CIS.

Furthermore, the group is also driving its ‘Go Green’ philosophy with introduction of environmentally- friendly and safe-to-use products. Some of the key AFTRON products that manifest this belief include air conditioners that consumer 30 per cent less, food waste disposers that reduce household food waste by up to 40%, CFC-free refrigerator and energy-saving lamps.

Success Story
Al Majid Group - A pioneer in international partnerships

Al Majid group relationship with General Electric (GE) of the USA is one of the oldest and big lesson from the UAE’s values of hardworking and perseverence in business could push business beyond boundaries. Juma Al Majid the Groups chairman recalls how he began an association with General Electric in the 1950s. He says he came across refrigerators produced by the company — at the time not available in Dubai — at the Al Qusaibi showroom in Bahrain.

Sensing an opportunity, he pretended to check the refrigerator, while picking up the guarantee card with the company’s details. He contacted GE once back in Dubai. But his first deal with GE was not a great success. He bought 200 air conditioners directly from the GE dealer in Lebanon at a discounted price, as the order was quite a substantial one in those times.

However, it took him two years to sell all the stock as there was no proper electricity supply then. But things changed for the best as the UAE started developing with first class infrastructure and electricity. By the 1970s, his company was the second-largest in terms of sales of GE products, including up to 700,000 air-conditioners a year. Even today GE is among the main supplier of electronics in Jumal Al Majid Home Appliances Department.

S.S. The extensive diversification of the S.S. Lootah Group is further evidenced in its interests in education, healthcare, trading, interior design, food and hospitality and IT solutions. Some of the well-established ventures in these fields include Dubai Medical College, Dubai Pharmacy College, Islamic School for Training and Education, Lootah Technical Centre, Al Islami Foods, Jawhara Hotels and Apartments, and Lootah IT Solutions to name a few.

Presently, S.S. Lootah Group has a workforce of over 10,000 multinational staff, which it continues to steer through entrepreneurship and sustainable development. Keeping up with the UAE’s growing importance on the global stage, the Group continues to bring its expertise and innovative approach to newer sectors.

The group’s operations range from motor vehicles and real estate development, to home electronics and computer operating systems. Their manufacturing base covers the production of a wide range of fibreglass (FRP) boats as well as bitumen-based membranes for use in construction.

It is headquartered on Sheikh Zayed Road in Dubai’s busy southern corridor, but also runs a network of offices and showrooms that stretch across the UAE and into the Gulf and the Middle East. Company Profile

Damas is the Middle East’s leading international jewellery and watch retailer. Founded in 1907 in Syria, the company was lured by the UAE’s prospective business and moved to Dubai in 1955 where it found its headquarters.

Damas traces its origins back to when Mohammed Tawfique Abdullah – grandfather of now Damas shareholdersstarted crafting, designing and customizing jewelry in Syria. Abdullah’s business migrated to the United Arab Emirates in 1955 when his son opened, in Dubai, a goldsmith shop under the name Al Abdullah Jewellery Traders LLC (AAJT), which showcased the Damas jewelry brand for the very first time.

The group opened its first retail whole sale gold operations in the emirate in 1970, By the end of 1985, the Damas Group had expanded with several retail stores throughout the UAE and in 1988, the Group launched its first branded jewellery line under the Harmony brand.

currently the Damas brands – which are spread across various jewelry types including diamonds, gemstones, gold, and pearl- are Boudour, Farasha, Farha, Fior, Fulla, Harmony, Hayati, Jawaher, Kiku, Legacy, Manthourah, Nakshatra, and Solitaire. Other product offerings include Damas watches, an articles collection, and a men’s collection of primarily bangles and cufflinks.

Damas operates around 450 stores distributed across 18 countries, most of which are located in the GCC region. The company has grown from a UAE-based retailer into a global brand name, and is also the retailer for a large portfolio of renowned global luxury brands.

Damas sells jewellery and watches through four main store formats, which are uniquely positioned to address different consumer segment needs. Specifically, the Les Exclusives Stores offer high-end luxury products; Semi- Exclusives Stores offer stylish and sophisticated products for the discerning consumer; and the Damas 22K and 18K Stores offer trendy, fashionable and stylish products and brands at competitive prices. In addition to the four principal store formats, Damas also operates other outlets such as exclusive watch stores and Mono-brand boutiques.

By 2000, the Group had established retail operations in Qatar, Bahrain, Oman and Jordan. In 2002, the Group expanded its operations to Kuwait and Saudi Arabia, and by 2004, the Group had further strengthened its presence within and beyond the Middle East.

As it strived to become a leading multinational in the jewellery and fashion industry and a house to international brands, Damas has had a number of celebrities endorsing it as their ambassador among these are Esha Gupta and the Arabic pop singer Nancy Ajram. The group has won several awards for its dedicated quality and reliability, among these are Dubai Quality Appreciation Award Program (DQAP). It has also won the Tahtian Pearl Trophy competition in 2006 organised by Diamond Trading Company (DTC)- DeBeers and Perles de Tahiti. Damas was also listed on NASDAQ Dubai in 2008

Success Story
Damas: Making a success in Saudi Arabia

In 2002 Damas expanded and opened another retail store in Sharafiya, Jeddah of Saudi Arabia to spread their Dubai success to the rest of the region. The retail shop was named Damas Saudi Arabia Company Ltd and was operating as a subsidiary of Damas Jewellery LLC.

Damas International in Dubai announced in April 2011 that a subsidiary had acquired a controlling stake in its joint venture operation, Damas Saudi Arabia Company. The officials said in a statement that the Damas KSA stake was purchased for an undisclosed sum by Damas Jewellery, increasing its stake to 98 percent. The acquisition illustrated the optimism the Damas International had for their jewellery business in the kingdom. The Saudi market is a core market for Damas, and the acquisition was to help provide a platform for the group to service this key market more fully.

Company Profile

The rise of small businesses at the end of the nineties and in the early of the new millennium has significantly contributed to the rising need of new business consultancy companies in the UAE. Dubai Consultancy (DC) as one of the leading consultancies in the region was established in 2005 in response to the overworked small businesses that had more obligations than time. With a small team of professionals, the company began building its clients’ base of different businesses in different sectors.

Since inception, Dubai Consultancy has realized that a consultancy company’s success is determined by its success in operating as a unified team. Dedicated hard working and commitment are greatly considered when it comes to selecting an employee at the company. In addition, it has been following strict guideline in order to build its competitive team. Thus, treating each employee with respect and listening to their input on how to continually improve the company’s service goals, not tolerating discrimination of any kind and encouraging all in problem solving creativity and having an open door policy which encourages interactions, discussions and the exchange of ideas have become the norms of Dubai Consultancy that were indeed behind its local and international achievements.

Within a short span of time, DC managed to leave its remarkable footprints across the different segments of consultancy from business advisory and consultancy to investment promotion, project development and event management in several countries in Asia, Africa and Europe.

Being a part of a prominent site that posses an outstanding reputation in the international market and accessible to diversified businesses, Dubai Consultancy’s business and financial professionals have gone through an extensive expertise that enabled them to take the company’s operations beyond the region’s borders. From its headquarter in Dubai, DC reached clients in the Middle East, North Africa and South Asia region. Within the events management segment, the company has successfully staged the Ghana Trade and Investment Exhibition 2008, the UAE-France Partnership Forum 2007, the UAE-Singapore Business Forum 2006, Iraq Development Expo and Tanzania Mission – Dar Es Salaam.

In addition to the service base and geographic market expansion, its customer base’s growth was also evident. It has grown today to include companies, government and non-government organizations.

Success Story
Guiding local manufacturers to West Africa

In 2008, Dubai Consultancy enhanced its engagement in the African continent when it organized the Trade and Investment Exhibition – Ghana and Senegal. The event aimed to create business opportunities for the Middle East and Asia in West Africa through Ghana as a centre of investment in the continent.

In collaboration between Dubai Consultancy and Dubai Export Development Corporation, the government authority responsible for promoting exports from Dubai, Dubaibased manufacturers were supported to participate in the exhibition. These companies were encouraged to tap the growing opportunities in the developing nations in Africa and use Ghana as a centre of the investment in the continent.
