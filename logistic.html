Company Profile

Dubai, as the trading hub of the UAE, is at the crossroads of world trade routes. From time immemorial, this ambitious and enterprising emirate has been the meeting point and the regional centre for traders across generations. Hence, it is no wonder that one of the world’s leading names in the global maritime industry was born out of Dubai. DP World was formed in September 2005 with the integration of the terminal operations of the Dubai Ports Authority (DPA), which was focused on the UAE ports of Rashid and Jebel Ali, and DPI (Dubai Ports International), which had been set up to export this success internationally.

DP World’s experience dates back to the year 1999, when DPI was established to apply its expertise to managing ports in the Middle East, India and Europe. Its first project was a collaboration at Jeddah Islamic Port (in 1999) on the management and operation of the South Container Terminal (SCT). In 2003, SCT was the first terminal in the country to exceed 1 million TEU (twenty-foot equivalent container units). DPI .continued to expanding through successful operations at the ports of Djibouti (2000), Vizag, India (2002) and Constanta, Romania (2003).

In January 2005, DPI transformed its network with the strategic acquisition of CSX World Terminals (CSX WT), the international terminal business of CSX Corporation, giving it a strong presence in Hong Kong, China, Australia, Germany, Dominican Republic and Venezuela. In yet another significant step, DP World also acquired CSX WT’s strong project pipeline, which included the 9-berth Pusan Newport (PNC), South Korea.

In February 2005, DP World signed an agreement with the Cochin Port Trust (CoPT) to construct, develop and operate an international container transhipment terminal at Vallarpadam, Kochi, India. Closer to home, in the same year, it was awarded a 30-year concession to develop and operate the container terminal at the Port of Fujairah, in the UAE. In November that year it announced agreements to develop new container terminals at Yarimca, Turkey and Qingdao, China.

DP World’s next giant leap forward came with the acquisition of P&O in March 2006, expanding its portfolio of terminals and adding P&O Maritime Services to the group. The combined container throughput of both companies for 2005 was more than 35 million TEU across terminals from the Americas to Asia. This grew to nearly 42 million TEU in 2006. This deal made the company a top-three global port operator and was an historic moment that formed the base for further international growth over time with an aim to achieve progress for all.

DP World is an apt example of the Dubai model of adding services that go “beyond the gate” in response to shipping lines and shippers seeking greater efficiencies across the whole supply chain. Its London Gateway project, a prime example taking this model global, is a port centric logistics solution with corridors into the UK hinterland. The new 3.5 million TEU deep sea container port located just east of London on the north bank of the Thames, due to open in Q4 2013, has the potential to transform logistics operations in the UK at the heart of the UK’s largest consumer market – the south east.

It is adjacent to what will be the largest rail connected logistics park development in Europe at some 9.25 million square feet. The vast majority of deep sea imports enter the UK through south east ports, yet only 10 per cent of warehousing is in the region. By having these at the point of entry DP World’s strategy will ensure that significant significant supply chain savings are made through reduced transport costs.

On other continents, DP World has also developed a container train to improve the connectivity between the ports and hinterland in the Indian Subcontinent. It operates a container freight station outside the port gates in Mundra (India) and has invested in and operates inland depots in northern Europe and Vancouver, Canada.

It has stayed true to its vision of sustainable value through global growth, service and excellence with a firm commitment to people, profitable global growth, responsible corporate and individual behaviour as well as excellence and innovation. Operating in multiple markets with different regulations brings unique challenges, which DP World tackled with moves that matched its fast-paced growth. It has consistently underlined its expansion through a series of corporate responsibility (CSR) programs and has become an integral part of the communities in which it operates.

Today, with more than 60 terminals across six continents DP World and its companies are enablers of word trade, connecting global markets and world economies. With an average concession life of 43 years, it is a fundamental part of the nations and communities where it is present, making sustainability a way of life and a way of business. As the thirdlargest port operator in the world, its team of 28,000 people serves business and trade by providing multi-modal access to global markets as a trusted and responsive partner to all those involved in the supply chain.

In 2012, DP World handled more than 56 million TEU (twenty-foot equivalent container units) across its portfolio from the Americas Asia. With a pipeline 11 new developments and major expansions underway in 9 countries including India, Africa, Europe and the Middle East, capacity is expected to rise to around 103 million TEU by 2020. The promising future is also evident in the group’s strong financial performance. DP World’s profits for the year 2012 stood at USD 749 million with revenue of USD 3,121 million, which was 5% ahead compared to the previous year.

Success Stories
Sailing ahead with Djibouti

DP World’s approach to business has always centred around long-term partnerships – with customers, business partners and communities. Its investment in Djibouti over several years is an excellent example of this. Sitting at the crossroads of some of the world’s main shipping lanes connecting Asia, Africa and Europe, Doraleh Container Terminal (DCT) is a jewel in the crown of DP World’s Africa operations.

Foundations for the terminal were laid in 2000 when DP World and the Djiboutian Government established a joint venture together, with DP World awarded a 20-year concession to operate the Port of Djibouti. That successful partnership gave birth to the joint-venture that built Doraleh, which opened in 2008. Through its modern logistics facilities and strategic location, Doraleh is well placed to become the premier container distribution hub for the Sailing ahead with Djibouti region. The terminal has an annual handling capacity of 1.2 million TEUs (20-foot equivalent units) the largest in East Africa with almost 50% of containers handled destined for Ethiopia.

These physical assets are shaping Doraleh’s future. Moreover, as a major employer in the region with over 800 staff, close to 98 percent of who are local, DP World engages directly with the community. One of its initiative is a multi-purpose community centre close to the port offering healthcare, training for personal and professional development, recreation facilities and much more.

Expanding with the fast-growing Indian economy

India is one of the fastest-growing emerging economies of the world and DP World has been committed to supporting its growth for more than a decade and is continuing it into the future. With five terminals across the country, India is one of its most important markets.

The Port of Nhava Sheva is a shining example of its investment in India’s long term future, being part of the largest port in the country at Jawaharlal Nehru Port, close to the commercial hub of Mumbai that handles almost half of India’s maritime traffic. Starting operations in 1999 as the first private container terminal, Nhava Sheva is a major gateway to the vast hinterland beyond its gates. Aside from links to a wide network of inland container depots (ICD), the terminal is also connected to India’s major highway and rail networks.

DP World’s plans on the anvil include investment of around USD 200 million to build a container terminal and 17 hectares of yard. The new quay has been awarded a 17-year concession and will be equipped with 4 rail mounted quay cranes and 12 rubber tyred gantry cranes.

Its investment in Nhava Sheva also takes other forms alongside new equipment and construction. Keeping up its socially-responsible partnerships, DP World is active in the local community with school projects and environmental awareness campaigns. It also keeps employee welfare high on the agenda with incentives to boost productivity, continually enhanced safety awareness, sports events and round-the-clock paramedic services on site.

Company Profile

In a world that is reinventing ways and means to do business, economic ingenuity is seeing a rapid increase. The United Arab Emirates, and Dubai in particular, continue to play a crucial role in the region by creating policies and places that are conducive to successful business. These are not just incubators for growth in the nation, but also a benchmark for the wider world.

Showcasing the UAE’s approach, economic Zones World (EZW), from its headquarters in Dubai, is exporting its expertise and experience in forming hubs that generate solid, lasting economic growth. EZW is the global provider of sustainable industrial and logistics infrastructure solutions. It is part of the global holding company Dubai World, which focuses on the strategic growth areas of Transport & Logistics, Drydocks & Maritime, Urban Development, Investment and Financial Services.

EZW’s current portfolio includes Jafza, Gazeley, TechnoPark and Dubai Auto Zone. As a group that strives to enhance successes for people, places and economies, it has been at the forefront of creating challenging and rewarding environments. Its robust network of economic zones, technology, logistics and industrial parks as well as buildto- suit developments across the world fulfil the objective to support the dynamic expansion of its rapidly growing international customer base.

Jafza is EZW’s flagship operation and home to more than 6,800 multinational companies, including over 120 of the Global Fortune 500 enterprises. It is the region’s most efficient logistics hub and the only one in the world located between a top container terminal and a top international airport, enabling the best in multimodal connectivity. In its 26-year history through its pioneering project, the Jebel Ali Free Zone, it is a brilliant example of the success of Dubai and EZW’s strategic planning.

The free zone contributes 21 percent of Dubai’s GDP on a year-to-year basis; accounts for more the 50 percent of Dubai’s total exports; sustains the employment of more than 138,000 persons; and above all, accounts for 40 percent of net FDI inflow into the UAE.

Also in EZW portfolio is Gazeley, one of Europe’s leading developers and providers of sustainable logistics and industrial spaces. Gazeley has strong presence in the UK, mainland Europe, China and India. Since 1987, it has developed more than 60 million sq ft of sustainable distribution warehouses and industrial space for leading international companies and third-party logistics providers worldwide.

Closer to home, TechnoPark is the EZW research and development initiative in core economic sectors, including energy, water and environment. It focuses on nurturing business ideas and new technologies in various fields, which could be quickly translated into real-world industrial operations. This is a landmark project that symbolises Dubai’s vision to develop a knowledge-based, technologycentric sustainable business hub.

Further on, Dubai Auto Zone (DAZ) is a comprehensive marketplace for the auto industry catering to buyers, sellers, service providers, principals and traders. DAZ, a mixed use facility, will consist of a free zone to attract Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in the auto sector, a specialised economic zone to cater to the GCC markets and a retail zone to serve the local markets.

EZW has spread its wings far and wide with a presence that spans from the Americas to Africa to Asia Pacific. In the USA, it is creating the first zone in the country, located in Orangeburg County, South Carolina. Jafza Americas is designing and developing the Orangeburg Industrial and Logistics Park, which will support customers’ logistics, distribution, assembly and light manufacturing needs in the southeast United States.

The 1,322 acre site is to be developed in four stages over the next 20 years as determined by market conditions. This catalyst project is expected to create thousands of jobs by bringing together immediate interstate access, reliable utilities, a quality workforce and scalable real estate offerings that will provide customers a location for growth and profitability.

In Africa, EZW has one of the most centrally-located destinations in the form of the Djibouti Free Zone, a project that is leading its efforts in the Middle East & Africa (MEA) region. While in the Asia Pacific, it is shaping the future of key markets from its regional headquarters based in the city of Shanghai, China. The group also has existing as well as planned operations in thriving locations across India with an aim to bring about multiple connectivity options as well as world-class logistics infrastructure.

As part of its ongoing drive, the group is currently expanding into niche locations, while investing significantly locally to capture the fast-growing markets in the UAE. Recognising its consistent achievements, EZW has been awarded several prestigious commendations, key among these being the Dubai Quality Award for excellence in the service sector and the CIO Top 20 title for its commitment to deploy new technologies to serve its customers better.

The gateway to growth in Africa

Aptly named the gateway to East Africa, the Djibouti Free Zone is one of the single most important strategic port and logistics operations to the Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA) & the US. EZW’s maiden project in this nation, it is located on the horn of the continent and is responsible for the majority of trade from Europe, Asia and the rest of Africa.

Jafza has been at the forefront of this successful project, entirely managing the Free Zone thus ensuring its strategic and financial success. With extensive knowledge of what makes a business hub flourish, Jafza has implemented its international best practices by attracting corporations from across the globe with favourable investment, start up and work policies, not to mention a state-of-the-art logistics and communications infrastructure.

Developed on a 40-hectare plot, DFZ offers office units, warehouses, light industrial units and plots sized between 2,000 to 15,000 sq mt to investors. Followings 100 percent occupation of Phase I of EZW’s Djibouti Free Zone (DFZ), it launched Phase II of the project comprising the development of light industrial units, warehouses and office facilities.

EZW has been a major partner in the development of the country’s infrastructure, operating and managing a number of projects that have significantly contributed to Djibouti’s socio-economic growth. Together with its sister organisation, DP World, which manages Djibouti Port, and now the Doraleh Container Terminal and Djibouti International Airport, it has been a part of the nation’s remarkable development.

Today, Djibouti Ports and the Free Zone together account for more than half of the country’s GDP. Its significance can be gauged by the fact that since the launch of DFZ, Djibouti’s real GDP growth in percentage terms has jumped from 3% in 2005 to almost 7% in 2008.

Company Profile

In 1976, Gulftainer Company Limited was established in the emirate of Sharjah with a primary role to manage and operate the container terminals of Sharjah’s ports on behalf of Sharjah Port Authority. Gulftainer started its operations in Port Khalid, the main port of Sharjah and subsequently in 1986 Khorfakkan Port, a coastal city on the Arabian Gulf. Since then, the company has been playing an important role in increasing the activity of exports and imports via sea which has resulted in steadily economic growth in the emirate and in the UAE in general.

Over the decades, Gulftainer has set its vision of becoming a globally-recognized terminals operator and determined the pillars of success by seizing the opportunities that opened the doors for the company to fly higher. In 2009, a 5-year contract was signed with the international plastics company, Borouge, boosting Gulftainer’s presence in the local market. Gulftainer, as per the contract, is to provide Borouge with on-site cargo handling, packaging and logistics services at its polyolefin’s plant in Ruwais, Abu Dhabi. The size of operations is expected to expand since Borouge is increasing the capacity of its polyolefin’s plant. Now, Gulftainer is the second largest ports operator and the largest privately owned port operator in the world.

Momentum Logistics, the 3rd party logistics provider – a part of the Gulftainer group, has been providing a wide range of logistics services since 2008 throughout the UAE and region with rapidly growing activity in Iraq in recent years.

The vision of being global did not only drive Gulftainer to take its operations beyond the UAE but it has also allowed it to think unconventionally by expanding its services’ base and stepping into other segments. In mid-2010, it tied-up with Avalon, an inland transportation services provider. As a result of this strategic tie-up, the number of trailers and trucks has dramatically increased. In addition, the year of 2010 has also witnessed a geographic market expansion with signing a joint venture with the Pak Shaheen Group of Pakistan to supply inland logistics and cargo transportation in Pakistan. Since then, the company’s wheel of achievements in the international arena started spinning. Gulftainer was awarded the concessions to operate Berth 8 “the Iraq Project Terminal (IPT)” in South Port of Umm Qasr. It was also awarded to complete the construction of and develop and operate Berths 10 & 11 ‘The Iraq Container Terminal’ (ICT).

The company has taken its advanced operations management beyond the GCC countries: Lebanon, Turkey, Russia and Brazil. In November 2012, Gulftainer was awarded the concession to develop and operate a new container terminal at the Port of Tripoli, Lebanon. The concession will last for 25 years and start with an investment of over than USD 60 million in new equipment and machinery. Once completed, the new terminal will be able to accommodate some of the largest container vessels operating in the Eastern Mediterranean, alleviating congestion at the Port of Beirut and providing an alternative to the beleaguered ports of Tartous and Latakia in Syria. This project will provide at least 300 new jobs and as well as stimulate the economic growth in the area.

Penetrating the BRICs markets is an obvious objective of the UAE-based global prominent player. The strong commitment of Gulftainer to become a major global port operator resulted in establishing its Brazilian venture in 2011 in order to facilitate a major concession to operate the Port of Recife, located in the North East of Brazil. The company has received great interests of major lines to use its services in this port.

The growth was not only evident in the number of terminals developed and operated by the company but also in the number of shipping lines that call at Gulftainer’s ports. United Arab Shipping Company (UASC), CMA-CGM, CSAV, Hanjin, Sea Consortium, China Shipping, Hapag Lloyd, American President Lines (APL), MAG Container Lines, Maersk Line, Mediterranean Shipping Company (MSC) and Ethiopian Shipping Line are a few examples of these major shipping lines.

Gulftainer has handled 4 million TEUs, or twenty-foot equivalent units, in 2011, of which 3.3 million TEUs was in the UAE, a 7% increase compared to 2010. This number will definitely increase since the company eyes more acquisitions in the Gulf, Asia, Africa and South America.

Gulftainer’s evident success in the international market through the successive acquisitions and the increasing number of operations and services offered is not only attributed to the company’s foresighted vision and bright management but also to the local market that efficiently incubated the company and set up the platform of its take-off towards internationalism.

Success Story
Expanding Global Footprint into the Dynamic Region of Russia

In September 2011, Gulftainer has successfully expanded its global footprint into the most dynamic Russian maritime market through investing up to USD 270 million to co-develop and operate Ust-Luga port in Russia’s Baltic region. Being the largest UAE private company investment deal in Russia, the UAE-based company has demonstrated a strong confident on the commercial potential of the Russian market. Ust-Luga is strategically located as a gateway into the heart of Russia’s buoyant Baltic Western region. The volume of container traffic, through the Baltic, has risen dramatically in recent years but the scope for growth is high, as the ratio of containers handled to population is still only 30 per 1000 people in Russia compared with 168 per 1000 in the EU. In addition, it will play a significant role as an alternative port, reducing the increasing congestion on St. Petersburg Port.

Gulftainer has previously signed a major joint venture with Prominvest, the financial and investment arm of Russian State Corporation, Russian Technologies. The new Joint Venture Company, Gulftainer Russian Technologies was established to pursue investments and management opportunities in the complete logistics chain within the Russian Federation and the rest of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) including strategic port assets and related infrastructure.

Company Profile

Drydocks World is one of the solid examples of what forward thinking and far sighted planning can achieve, especially for an ever growing emirate like Dubai. The original idea of Dubai Drydocks started in 1973 when the late ruler HH Sheikh Rashid Bin Said Al Maktoum decided to establish a shipyard to provide the busy marine traffic lines in the Arabian Gulf with a platform for maintenance and repair. The shipyard grew to become one of the largest in the world, with an annual capacity of over 350 vessels, most of them ULCCs (Ultra Large Crude Carriers) and VLCCs (Very Large Crude Carriers).

Through its shipyards in Dubai, Indonesia and Singapore, Drydocks World has become a global key player in ship repair and rig upgrade, conversion and new building. The Dubai facility, one of the first and largest shipyards in the Middle East, has received vessels of all sizes and shapes, ranging from the smallest fishing boat to the largest ULCC from over 40 different countries. It has one of the largest graving docks in the world at 521 m x 100m, capable of accommodating a vessel of half a million dead weight. In 2003, Dubai yard built and delivered a 14,500 tons floating dock for Saudi Aramco, enhancing its role in the region.

In mid-2007, a world-wide expansion program was started. In April 2008, Drydocks World acquired Pan United Marine that owns multiple shipyards in Singapore and Indonesia. As a result, Drydocks World – South East Asia was established. The ‘One Integrated Company’ slogan was initiated and propagated recently. The new huge facility comprises 4 shipyards. Singapore yard is involved in repair, conversion and new building. The company also has yards in Pertama, Graha and Nanindah in Indonesia. Its yard in Pertama is involved in ship repair, conversion and new building. The yard in Nanindah is a volume shipbuilding yard and has constructed over 75 Offshore Support Vessels, whereas the shipyard in Graha is a dedicated rig building yard and has successfully completed several projects.

The operations in South East Asia bring together well-established offshore rig construction, shipbuilding, conversion and ship repair expertise located at crossroads of the world’s busiest shipping lanes and close to major offshore Oil and Gas field developments. This company is growingly becoming an interesting asset of Drydocks World, especially with the increasing demand for its services in the South East Asia region. The company employs around 10,000 employees from 52 countries.

The success Drydocks World has achieved locally and internationally is not attributed to one factor. The UAE business-friendly environment that made it easy for international companies to cooperate with Drydocks World, the dedicated workforce with an accumulating expertise and the strategic locations of the shipyards as the crossroads of the busiest routes in the world and the diversity employment strategy followed are some of the success pillars that enabled the company to grow and become a leader in its field.

Company Profile

The most prominent feature of UAE-based companies is how each displays the same traits of ambition and diversified growth that the nation is built on. Most of these enterprises have grown to become conglomerates that have not become prominent harbingers of progress in the wider region. In many ways, the traditions of trading and shipping are common across several of the Middle East nations. Upholding this tradition, while going beyond is Sharaf Group, a name that entered the business world in the year 1975.

Starting out with just US$ 50,000 in cash, the pioneering approach of its leadership gave birth to one of the Gulf’s largest conglomerates with a diversified portfolio of companies and investments ranging from real estate to shipping to and beyond. Headquartered in Dubai, Sharaf Group at present comprises over 60 member companies in more than eight business sectors. As one of largest business conglomerates, the group has sustained growth through leveraging the opportunities of the UAE’s diversified economy. Its business activities are focused on forming a strong presence within the Middle East, Africa and South Asia regions. Having started out as a trading business house, it has always laid emphasis on reliability and product delivery in every venture.

In the financial sector, the group has two strong entities, namely Central Exchange, its wholly-owned currency exchange house and Mac Sharaf Securities, a brokerage firm formed in partnership with the Hong Kong-based McMillen Advantage Capital and the Dubai-based Al Ali Group. One of its strongest areas of expertise is general trading and the group has several well-known names to its credit in this field including Sharaf Retail, Sharaf DG and Sharaf Digital, offering Consumer Electronics and Home Appliances as well as Sharaf AG and Yellow Hat Japan, its retail stores focused on the automotive sector.

Furthermore, the group has a praiseworthy presence in the manufacturing and industrial field with nine different companies encompassing a wide range of units from processing to furniture and cement to yachts. Keeping pace with the modern world, it started Information Dynamic, which specialises in meeting the IT needs of the Shipping, Retail, Logistics and Travel and Aviation companies. From establishment in the year 1995, the firm today has a strong overseas presence along with a customer base in over 33 countries globally.

Moving into the growing market of Real Estate, Property and Auxiliary sectors, the group made a string of investments in carefully-planned ventures. Its successes in this field include Al Maskan Real Estate, Marina Residence and Sharaf Facilities Management. The group also has two well-known shopping centres under its portfolio: Times Square Centre, Dubai and Al Masa Mall, Muscat.

Keeping true to its trading roots, the group has formed a remarkable presence in Shipping and Logistics with both wholly-owned and joint venture companies to its credit. Some of the names under its umbrella, headquartered in different locations, are Emarat Maritime, Sharaf Shipping Agency, Steinweg-Sharaf, Diamond Shipping, Samsara Group and Hind Terminals. The group also has a strong travel, tourism and hospitality portfolio that includes Sharaf Travel, Oasis Adventures and Protea Sharaf - a joint venture between Sharaf Group and Protea Hotels, Africa’s largest hotel management company.

As a reliable, responsible, goal-oriented and determined company, it also invests its resources in projects that benefit the community and the well-being of its people. Sharaf Group has created a niche for itself in diverse fields by taking advantage of the UAE’s fast-paced economic expansion and strategic geographic location. Growing at a sustained pace for the past three decades, it now employs a multi-national, multi-cultural team over 2,600 professionals.

Its international presence can be gauged by the fact that in India, within just over a decade, Samasara Group has dynamically grown to employ over 450 personnel, managing 52 owned offices across 43 locations. Today, the Sharaf Group has a direct presence in more than 16 countries with operations in almost every nation in the Middle East, Africa and South Asia.

Company Profile

Rais Hassan Saadi – the flagship company of the RHS Group – was founded in 1910 as a shipping agency in Dubai. At that time, Dubai became a principal port on the Gulf coast, with pearling being the basis of the city’s prosperity. Since inception, the core business of the group has been shipping and freight related services. However, over the years, the group have established companies and subsidiaries, making the group as a diversified conglomerate that offers services in many sectors including real estate, insurance and tourism.

The ship agency of the group currently includes Rais Hassan Saadi and Sunmarine Sipping Services.

RHS has offices in all the major UAE ports and an excellent network of offices and sub agents in the Gulf countries, the Indian subcontinent and East Africa, RHS, enabling the company to take full advantage of the rapid economic development in the region. It offers a general agency function for the entire GCC. It is one of the founder members of Dubai Ship Agents Association and a member of BIMCO - Baltic and International Maritime Council.

Sunmarine, established in 1993 in Dubai as a wholly owned subsidiary of the RHS Group, has been representing as Managing Agents for the entire UAE region, two prominent NVOCC (Non-vessel Operating Common Carrier), namely ARC LINE and BAY LINES since 1993 and 1994 respectively. ARC LINE and BAY LINES offer service between the ports of the Indian Sub-Continent, West Asia, the Gulf and East Africa.

In order to facilitate superior service coverage for ARC LINE and BAY LINES customers outside the UAE, the Group has established an agents’ network in India, Pakistan, East Africa, Persian Gulf, Qatar and Oman. An expansion of service coverage is regularly being planned, indicating the Group’s vision of boosting its global presence.

The success of being one of the shipping industry’s pioneers in the region is attributed to several factors; among them are handling various types of ships that enabled the company to gain valuable experience in all segments of shipping, enjoying an excellent rapport with the business community, and industry and local port and customs authorities and continuous investing in I.T. infrastructure required for meeting the requirements of Shipping Line Principals, Ports and Customs.

RHS Group also established two main forwarding arms operating from Dubai: RHS Freight Services and Trident Freight. They offer a portfolio of freight services. RHS Freight Services has expanded its presence into India and Kenya also.

RHS Group also owned or joint-ventured offices in other countries such as Qatar, Oman and Pakistan that operate their own forwarding activities at a local level.

Recently, the group has stepped into different sectors. It owns a variety of commercial and residential property in the UAE that has been leasing. It also entered the tourism sector in 1992 through its subsidiary Trident Travel. In addition, Rais Hassan Saadi Insurance Agents and Brokers, the insurance services arm of the Group, in association with the New India Assurance Company, from Mumbai, India offers a variety of insurance products.

Committed to continuous improvement and maintaining high standards of reliability and quality, the group has gained the ISO 9001:2000 Quality Standard for its Quality Management System.

Success Story
RHS Group: expanding to Africa

RHS Group, as a part of its expansion plan, established a wholly owned subsidiary “Rais Shipping Services (RSS) in Mombasa in 2008 to provide shipping agency and shipping related services to its Principals, operating container vessels, break-bulk vessels and Ro-Ro vessels.

The group’s focus on the African Continent indicates the company’s clear interest in expanding into the emerging markets by realizing the potential growth that exist in these markets.

RSS-KE is a fully accredited Shipping Agent in Mombasa and a member of the Kenya Ships Agents Association. It offers full-fledged Shipping Agency services, including marketing and sales for its principals as well arranging transshipment and inland transportation to other countries in the African continent.

RSS-KE has made transportation of goods in Kenya through an advanced network of services relatively easy and at low prices. This indeed plays an important role in stimulating the Kenyan economy by increasing the import and export activities.
